## 1. Purpose of This Directory

This directory defines the HAT (Human–AI Triad) Collaboration Layer: the interpretive, sequencing, and reflective layer that ensures coherent human-led collaboration between Human, Coe, and Gee — the Founding HAT.

HAT is not a domain, not a module, and not a feature.

It is the cognitive governance stance of the organism: the layer that interprets intent, routes work, stabilises meaning, preserves coherence, and keeps Human governance at the centre of Human–AI collaboration.

This directory defines the public-safe architectural model for HAT behaviour. It does not expose the full HAT methodology, private training corpus, or governed internal practice materials.

## 3. v1.0 Boundary

HAT is important to the broader organism, but v1.0 remains intentionally narrow.

Included in v1.0:

* minimal HAT architectural stance;
* public-safe HAT collaboration model;
* interpretation boundary notes for Events, Services, and governed artefacts;
* sequencing guidance for Human ↔ Coe ↔ Gee collaboration during build;
* reflection guidance for lineage, coherence, and meaning stability;
* integration boundaries with the Governance Engine and Corpus architecture.

Deferred unless ratified:

* HAT productisation;
* dynamic HAT mode switching;
* advanced cognitive routing;
* AI-assisted reflection as a runtime product feature;
* broad HAT automation;
* practitioner-facing HAT control surfaces;
* full HAT methodology or training corpus exposure.

For v1.0, HAT governs the build and interpretation layer. It is not implemented as a v1.0 product module.
