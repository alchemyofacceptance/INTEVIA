# Knowledge Structures v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the semantic structures, taxonomies, and knowledge relationships that allow INTEVIA to organise and interpret meaning.

Knowledge structures answer:

> How does the organism know what something is, how it relates to other things, and why it matters?

In INTEVIA, knowledge is not treated as loose content. It is structured so that artefacts, evidence, domains, documents, events, services, and governance records can be classified, related, searched, interpreted, and evolved over time.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, knowledge structures should remain minimal, explicit, and implementation-focused.

The priority is not to build a full ontology, semantic graph, or AI-assisted knowledge engine. The priority is to ensure that implemented artefacts, evidence, audit records, documentation, Events, Services, Library records, and CARE guidance can be classified and related clearly enough for the organism to preserve meaning from the beginning.

Full semantic graph expansion, broad ontology management, automated knowledge extraction, and AI-assisted interpretation as a product feature remain deferred unless separately ratified.
