# Recognition Overview v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the purpose, scope, and conceptual stance of the Recognition Domain as the organism’s becoming substrate.

Recognition answers:

> What has this human become through their participation, contribution, learning, support, and care?

Recognition applies to:

* learners;
* developers;
* volunteers;
* early adopters;
* DISCUSSION contributors;
* external advocates;
* governance participants;
* field-signal contributors;
* capability-forming humans;
* v1.0 rollout contributors.

Recognition is not certification.

Recognition is continuous, evidence-driven, identity-linked, formative, and governed.

Recognition transforms evidence into identity movement.

---

## Cadence as Recognised Becoming

Recognition also sees **HAT-Aligned Cadence** — the governed rhythm in which:

* Human meaning;
* AI execution;
* evidence;
* correction;
* governance;
* continuity;
* public traceability;

move together without fragmentation.

Cadence is not speed, productivity, or multitasking.

Cadence is a form of becoming specific to governed Human-AI collaboration.

HAT-aligned cadence is the music of the Triad.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Recognition should remain minimal, explicit, and meaningful.

The priority is to support evidence-linked recognition for learners, developers, volunteers, early adopters, DISCUSSION contributors, advocates, governance participants, and other humans helping INTEVIA emerge.

Recognition may include HAT-aligned cadence where evidence shows governed rhythm across parallel Human-AI workstreams without fragmentation, overclaim, or Human displacement.

Recognition must remain human-centred, evidence-based, non-coercive, non-gamified, and CARE-bounded.

Badges, scoring, rankings, leaderboards, automated reputation systems, credential wallets, marketplace recognition, payment-linked recognition, automated recognition inference, and certification engines remain deferred unless separately ratified.
