# Recognition Records v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how recognition records are structured, linked, evidenced, versioned, and governed.

A recognition record answers:

> What becoming has been seen, what evidence supports it, who recognised it, and how does it affect identity movement?

In INTEVIA, a recognition record is not a badge, compliment, score, or informal thank-you note. It is a governed recognition artefact with type, evidence, lineage, identity linkage, review state, audit trail, and CARE boundaries.

Recognition records apply to:

* learners;
* developers;
* volunteers;
* early adopters;
* DISCUSSION contributors;
* external advocates;
* governance participants;
* field-signal contributors;
* capability-forming humans;
* v1.0 rollout contributors.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Recognition records should remain minimal, explicit, and evidence-linked.

The priority is to support recognition records that can attach meaningful evidence to learners, developers, volunteers, early adopters, DISCUSSION contributors, advocates, governance participants, and other humans helping INTEVIA emerge.

Recognition records must not become vanity badges, rankings, reputation scores, or public status objects.

They should record evidenced becoming, preserve lineage, support governed identity movement, and remain CARE-bounded.
