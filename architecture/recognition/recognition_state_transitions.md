# Recognition State Transitions v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how recognition artefacts move through governed states and how identity movement is authorised.

Recognition transitions answer:

> What is the state of this recognition, what movement is allowed next, what evidence is required, and who may authorise it?

In INTEVIA, recognition artefacts are not simply created, granted, or displayed. They move through governed lifecycles. Each meaningful transition can carry evidence requirements, review authority, audit records, identity movement boundaries, CARE guidance, and lineage implications.

Recognition transitions apply to:

* learning;
* contribution;
* participation;
* engagement;
* governance behaviour;
* peer support;
* developer and volunteer contribution;
* external advocacy;
* field-signal contribution;
* capability formation.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Recognition state transitions should remain minimal, explicit, and reviewable.

The priority is to ensure that recognition artefacts can move through a small set of governed states such as proposed, reviewed, granted, revised, withdrawn, and archived without losing evidence, auditability, lineage, or meaning.

Recognition transitions must not enable automatic promotion, automated reputation inference, ranking escalation, public status competition, or unguided identity movement.

Recognition should move only where evidence, governance, CARE boundaries, and appropriate review support the transition.

Recognition transitions exist to protect humans from careless recognition as much as from invisibility.
