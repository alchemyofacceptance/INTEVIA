# Education State Transitions v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how curriculum, courses, and classes move through governed states and how learning outcomes produce evidence.

Education state transitions answer:

> What condition is this educational artefact in, what movement is allowed next, who may authorise it, and what evidence or audit record should be produced?

In INTEVIA, curriculum, courses, and classes are not merely created, edited, delivered, or closed. They move through governed lifecycles. Each meaningful transition can carry authority, evidence, audit, lineage, learner impact, educator responsibility, Event linkage, Service linkage, Library linkage, Corpus learning evidence, and CARE guidance.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, education state transitions should remain minimal, explicit, and executable.

The priority is to ensure that curriculum, courses, and class Events can move through a small set of governed states such as draft, published, active, delivered, completed, evidenced, reviewed, revised, deprecated, and archived without losing auditability, lineage, learner evidence, educator responsibility, or meaning.

Advanced workflow engines, automated assessment engines, semantic curriculum transition inference, cohort orchestration, certification engines, credential marketplaces, adaptive learning engines, broad learner analytics, full Exchange integration, and Education 2.0 automation remain deferred unless separately ratified.
