# Service Contracts v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how Services declare their inputs, outputs, constraints, responsibilities, and governed behaviour.

A Service contract answers:

> What does this Service require, what does it produce, who may deliver it, what rules constrain it, and what evidence should result?

In INTEVIA, a Service contract is not merely a technical interface. It is the governed agreement that allows a capability to be invoked, delivered, evidenced, audited, and linked to Events, Library artefacts, profile updates, and other organism records.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Service contracts should remain minimal, explicit, and executable.

The priority is not to create a full workflow-contract language. The priority is to ensure that each implemented Service can declare enough structure for the organism to govern delivery: what is required, what is produced, who may act, what evidence is created, what audit records are triggered, and what state transitions are allowed.

Advanced contract engines, dynamic contract authoring, automated Service orchestration, payment-linked contract rules, marketplace contract logic, and full Exchange integration remain deferred unless separately ratified.
