# 4. Structural Summary

## Foundations

Purpose, baseline boundaries, and advanced constraints.

Foundations define why continuity exists and what it must not become.

## Evidence & Records

Evidence, records, and markers.

This layer defines how continuity claims are substantiated, preserved, and signalled.

## Movement & Structure

Patterns, threads, state transitions, and re-entry.

This layer defines how continuity pauses, resumes, moves, splits, merges, and restores coherence.

## Governance & Change

Decisions, corrections, supersession, and archival.

This layer defines how continuity changes safely, how drift is corrected, how objects are replaced, and how closed lineage is preserved.

## Identity & Roles

Identity and role governance.

This layer defines who is acting, deciding, correcting, assisting, being recognised, or holding authority.

## Lineage & Risk

Continuity across time and risk mitigation.

This layer preserves the path of coherence and identifies threats such as false continuity, hidden state, fragmentation, over-preservation, boundary erosion, identity drift, and public-surface divergence.

## Public Surface & Governance

External alignment and Human-anchored governance.

This layer protects alignment between internal work and external representation.

# 6. Core Doctrine

Continuity preserves coherence, not volume.

Continuity supports re-entry, not memory reconstruction.

Continuity protects meaning, not speed.

Continuity is Human-governed, evidence-linked, explicit, bounded, and correctable.

Continuity is the organism remembering what matters across transition.
