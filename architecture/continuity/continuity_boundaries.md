# Continuity Boundaries v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the boundaries of the Continuity Engine: what Continuity may preserve, what it must not assume, and how it remains governed, evidence-linked, and safe.

Continuity protects coherence across time, context switches, life events, parallel workstreams, public-surface activity, and implementation phases.

Continuity is a constitutional safeguard, not a productivity mechanism.

---

## What Continuity Must Not Do

Continuity must not:

* replace Human memory or authority;
* create illusions of stability where none exists;
* over-preserve noise, drafts, or ungoverned artefacts;
* collapse into surveillance, productivity metrics, or behavioural scoring;
* override governance boundaries in the name of “keeping things moving”;
* treat every thread as equally important;
* preserve drift, confusion, or uncorrected errors;
* infer continuity where evidence does not support it;
* treat AI memory as equivalent to organism continuity;
* convert ordinary context into hidden profiling.

---

## What Continuity Must Always Do

Continuity must always:

* remain Human-governed;
* preserve meaning, not volume;
* maintain coherence, not speed;
* support correction before drift hardens;
* uphold v1.0 governance boundaries;
* protect the organism from fragmentation;
* preserve only what is relevant, contextual, and evidence-bearing;
* make continuity claims traceable to evidence;
* distinguish stable lineage from temporary working state;
* support safe re-entry after interruption or transition.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity should remain minimal, explicit, and evidence-linked.

The priority is to preserve coherence across meaningful workstreams without turning every activity, draft, interruption, or behavioural signal into permanent system memory.

Continuity must protect the organism from fragmentation without creating surveillance, false stability, or ungoverned preservation.

Continuity is the organism remembering what matters across transition.
