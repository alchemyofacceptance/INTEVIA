# Continuity Threads v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines Continuity Threads: governed parallel workstreams that maintain coherence across time, transitions, context shifts, life events, public-surface activity, and implementation phases without collapsing into noise or fragmentation.

Continuity Threads answer:

> How does the organism maintain coherence across multiple active workstreams?

Threads are not tasks.

Threads are not to-do lists.

Threads are not productivity lanes.

Threads are governed continuity lanes.

A Continuity Thread preserves the meaning, evidence, governance context, state, and lineage of a workstream so it can pause, resume, correct, align, or archive without losing coherence.

---

## Thread Characteristics v0.1

A Continuity Thread should be:

* **bounded** — clear scope, purpose, and governance limits;
* **evidence-linked** — anchored by records, markers, evidence, and lineage;
* **recoverable** — able to resume after interruption or context shift;
* **distinct** — not collapsed into adjacent threads without Human authority;
* **aligned** — consistent with Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, and relevant public-surface signals;
* **Human-governed** — not automatically inferred, merged, split, archived, or resumed;
* **state-aware** — clear whether it is active, paused, in correction, re-entering, or archived;
* **meaning-preserving** — designed to retain coherence, not volume.

Threads preserve parallel coherence.

They do not measure speed.

---

## Thread Structure v0.1

A Continuity Thread should include:

* **thread_id** — unique identifier for the continuity thread;
* **purpose** — what the thread exists to preserve or advance;
* **governance_context** — relevant scope, boundaries, authority, and constraints;
* **active_state** — current continuity state, such as active, paused, re-entry, correction, or archived;
* **continuity_records** — relevant records preserving thread state and context;
* **continuity_markers** — pause, re-entry, correction, boundary, lineage, archive, or workstream state markers;
* **continuity_evidence** — linked evidence supporting continuity claims;
* **lineage_links** — related commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, decisions, recognition events, or public-surface signals;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **re-entry_value** — what this thread preserves for safe future return;
* **boundary_note** — cautions against overclaim, hidden-state assumptions, noise preservation, or thread collapse.

## Minimal Thread Lifecycle v0.1

A Continuity Thread may move through the following lifecycle:

* **Created** — the thread is identified as meaningfully distinct.
* **Active** — the thread is being advanced under Human governance.
* **Paused** — the thread is safely interrupted and state is preserved.
* **Re-entering** — the Human or organism is restoring coherence before resuming.
* **Correcting** — drift, ambiguity, fragmentation, or boundary issues are being resolved.
* **Resumed** — the thread returns to active continuity after safe re-entry or correction.
* **Superseded** — the thread is replaced by a new governed thread, artefact, or decision.
* **Archived** — the thread is no longer active but remains preserved as lineage.

This lifecycle is intentionally minimal for v1.0.

---

## Thread Boundaries

Continuity Threads must not:

* imply hidden state;
* imply memory reconstruction;
* merge without Human authority;
* split without Human interpretation;
* fragment meaning across transitions;
* preserve noise as continuity;
* override governance boundaries;
* convert parallelism into productivity scoring;
* treat all workstreams as equally important;
* confuse active state with archived lineage;
* claim recoverability where evidence is missing.

Threads preserve coherence, not speed.

---

## Thread Merge and Split Boundaries v0.1

### Thread Merging

Threads may be merged only when:

* the Human confirms shared purpose or convergence;
* evidence links support the merge;
* governance context remains clear;
* no important boundary or identity distinction is lost;
* lineage remains traceable after merging.

Thread merging must not be used to hide fragmentation or simplify complexity falsely.

### Thread Splitting

Threads may be split when:

* a workstream develops a distinct purpose;
* governance boundaries differ;
* evidence or lineage requires separation;
* identity, recognition, or public-surface context would become confused if left merged;
* Human interpretation confirms the split.

Thread splitting must preserve lineage back to the original thread where relevant.

---

## Thread Failure Modes v0.1

A Continuity Thread may fail when:

* evidence is missing or incomplete;
* drift is uncorrected;
* lineage breaks;
* public-surface alignment collapses;
* identity boundaries are unclear;
* governance context is lost;
* re-entry cannot restore coherence;
* related threads are incorrectly merged;
* a thread is split without preserving lineage;
* archived lineage is mistaken for active state;
* parallelism becomes speed pressure rather than governed coherence.

Failure should trigger correction, archival, re-scope, or Human review — not continuation from a false state.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity Threads should remain minimal, explicit, evidence-linked, and Human-governed.

The priority is to preserve parallel coherence without implying hidden memory, autonomous continuity, productivity scoring, or automatic thread management.

Continuity Threads allow the organism to move through multiple workstreams without losing the shape of meaning.

They are governed lanes of coherence.
