# Continuity Identity v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how identity remains coherent across continuity: roles, authority, responsibility, participation, recognition, Human-AI boundaries, decisions, corrections, lineage, public-surface signalling, and supersession.

Continuity Identity answers:

> How does identity remain stable across transitions, corrections, lineage, and supersession?

Identity continuity protects the organism from drift, role confusion, false authority, recognition inflation, and public-surface identity misalignment.

Identity is not scoring.

Identity is not reputation.

Identity is not behavioural profiling.

Identity is the governed structure of who is acting, deciding, correcting, contributing, participating, or being recognised.

---

## Identity Components v0.1

Continuity Identity may include:

* Human roles and authority boundaries;
* Human-AI role distinctions;
* recognition-linked identity movement;
* participation boundaries;
* actor distinctions across threads;
* identity lineage across decisions and corrections;
* identity-safe public-surface signals;
* identity-linked governance context;
* identity supersession or archival where a role, boundary, or participation state changes.

Identity must remain Human-anchored.

---

## Identity Structure v0.1

An identity continuity entry should include:

* **timestamp** — when the identity confirmation, change, correction, or ratification occurred;
* **identity_context** — the role, authority, responsibility, participation, recognition, or boundary context involved;
* **identity_change_or_confirmation** — what changed, what remained stable, or what was reaffirmed;
* **evidence_links** — records, markers, decisions, recognition evidence, commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, or other evidence objects;
* **continuity_markers** — relevant identity, recognition, boundary, correction, lineage, public-surface, or archival markers;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **identity_value** — what coherence the identity entry preserves, protects, or clarifies;
* **supersession_or_archival_link** — prior or successor identity state, role, boundary, recognition event, or archived lineage where applicable;
* **human_interpretation_or_ratification** — Human-authored interpretation, correction, authority confirmation, or ratification;
* **boundary_note** — any caution needed to prevent false authority, role collapse, behavioural profiling, recognition inflation, or hidden-state assumptions.

Identity continuity must remain explicit and evidence-linked.

---

## Identity and Recognition

Recognition and Identity are related but distinct.

Recognition reflects evidenced becoming.

Identity defines who is acting, deciding, contributing, participating, or being recognised within governed boundaries.

Recognition may inform identity movement, but Recognition must not become:

* score;
* rank;
* reputation;
* hierarchy;
* productivity signal;
* permanent status;
* behavioural profile.

Identity continuity should preserve the relationship between Recognition and Human meaning without turning Recognition into social sorting.

---

## Human-AI Role Boundary

Continuity Identity must preserve the distinction between Human and AI roles.

The Human may govern, decide, ratify, correct, sequence, interpret, authorise, or withdraw authority.

AI nodes may assist, draft, analyse, synthesise, execute, propose, challenge, or surface risks within governed boundaries.

Continuity Identity must not collapse:

* Human authority into AI output;
* AI assistance into Human ratification;
* generated continuity into governed continuity;
* AI memory into organism identity;
* model behaviour into Human identity movement.

The Human remains the authority holder.

---

## Identity Boundaries

Identity continuity must not:

* imply hidden state;
* imply memory reconstruction;
* treat Recognition as scoring or ranking;
* collapse Human and AI roles;
* convert participation into behavioural profiling;
* treat identity movement as productivity;
* allow identity drift across threads without evidence;
* override governance boundaries;
* treat public identity signals as authoritative over governed evidence;
* preserve role ambiguity as continuity;
* imply authority without Human ratification.

Identity protects coherence.

It does not create hierarchy.

---

## Identity Failure Modes v0.1

Identity continuity may fail when:

* Recognition drifts from evidence;
* roles or authority become ambiguous;
* Human and AI roles collapse;
* identity lineage breaks across decisions;
* supersession is unclear or unlinked;
* public-surface identity signals diverge from internal evidence;
* Human authority becomes implicit rather than explicit;
* participation is treated as behavioural profile;
* identity movement is inferred without evidence;
* archived or superseded identity states continue to govern active work.

Failure should trigger correction, re-review, supersession, archival, or Human ratification.
