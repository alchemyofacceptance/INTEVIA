## 1. Purpose of This Directory

This directory defines the Continuity Engine: the layer that preserves the integrity of meaning, evidence, governance, identity movement, and workstreams across time, context switches, life events, and parallel execution.

Continuity ensures the organism remains coherent as it grows.

Continuity is not simply memory.

Continuity is not merely storage.

Continuity is not the AI remembering the Human.

Continuity is the organism remembering who it is, what has happened, what has been recognised, what has been governed, and what remains true across transitions.

## 3. Architectural Position

The Continuity Engine sits between:

* Recognition;
* Corpus;
* Governance Engine;
* Identity.

It binds these organs together so the organism does not lose itself as it grows.

Recognition sees evidenced becoming.

Corpus preserves memory and lineage.

Governance Engine preserves authority and rule-bound movement.

Identity preserves who can act, who can be referenced, and who carries responsibility.

Continuity ensures these organs remain coherent across time, transitions, context shifts, parallel execution, and future versioning.

## 4. v1.0 Boundary

Continuity is minimal but essential in v1.0.

Included in v1.0:

* continuity purpose definition;
* continuity boundary definition;
* continuity evidence definition;
* linkage to Recognition records;
* linkage to Corpus evidence;
* linkage to Governance Engine decisions;
* linkage to Identity records;
* context-switch continuity notes;
* workstream continuity notes;
* evidence lineage references;
* transition continuity markers;
* Human-governed correction points.

Deferred unless ratified:

* automated continuity inference;
* autonomous memory reconstruction;
* AI-only continuity claims;
* invisible state assumptions;
* cross-system memory automation;
* predictive identity movement;
* unreviewed continuity synthesis;
* continuity scoring;
* hidden behavioural profiling.

For v1.0, Continuity is a governed coherence layer, not an autonomous memory engine.
