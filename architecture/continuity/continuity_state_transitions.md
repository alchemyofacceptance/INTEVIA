# Continuity State Transitions v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how Continuity transitions occur, what they mean, and how they preserve coherence across time, context shifts, life events, public-surface activity, and parallel workstreams.

Continuity State Transitions answer:

> How does continuity move from one state to another without losing coherence?

In INTEVIA, continuity does not simply persist automatically. It moves through governed states. Each transition should preserve enough evidence, meaning, governance context, and workstream state for safe re-entry, correction, or archival.

Continuity transitions are not memory reconstruction.

They are governed coherence movements supported by evidence.

---

## Minimal v1.0 Continuity States

The minimal v1.0 continuity states are:

* **Active Continuity** — coherence is being maintained during active work.
* **Paused Continuity** — a safe interruption has occurred and relevant state is preserved.
* **Re-entry Continuity** — the Human returns and coherence is re-established.
* **Correction Continuity** — drift, ambiguity, or fragmentation is corrected and coherence is restored.
* **Archived Continuity** — lineage is preserved, but the continuity thread is no longer active.

These states are intentionally minimal for v1.0.

---

## Valid Transitions v0.1

Valid v1.0 continuity transitions include:

* Active Continuity → Paused Continuity
* Paused Continuity → Re-entry Continuity
* Re-entry Continuity → Active Continuity
* Active Continuity → Correction Continuity
* Correction Continuity → Active Continuity
* Active Continuity → Archived Continuity
* Paused Continuity → Archived Continuity
* Correction Continuity → Archived Continuity

All transitions must be Human-governed and evidence-linked.

---

## Prohibited Transitions v0.1

Continuity transitions must not include:

* AI-initiated continuity transitions without Human authority;
* transitions without supporting evidence;
* transitions that imply autonomous memory reconstruction;
* transitions that imply hidden state;
* transitions that bypass Human authority;
* transitions that preserve drift as if it were coherence;
* transitions that convert ordinary activity into surveillance;
* transitions that prioritise speed over meaningful continuity.

---

## Transition Evidence

Each continuity transition should include:

* **timestamp** — when the transition occurred or was recorded;
* **transition_type** — the continuity movement being recorded;
* **prior_state** — the state before transition;
* **new_state** — the state after transition;
* **evidence_links** — references to commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, screenshots, or other evidence objects;
* **human_interpretation_or_correction** — Human-authored interpretation, correction, ratification, or boundary note where applicable;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **re-entry_value** — what the transition preserves for safe continuation later;
* **boundary_note** — any caution needed to prevent false continuity, overclaim, or hidden-state assumptions.

---

## Transition Interpretation Boundaries

A continuity transition should be interpreted as evidence that coherence was preserved, restored, paused, or archived.

It should not be interpreted as proof that:

* nothing changed;
* all memory was preserved;
* the AI retained full context;
* the system can reconstruct missing meaning;
* the Human no longer needs to govern re-entry;
* speed or productivity increased.

Continuity transitions are coherence markers, not productivity markers.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity State Transitions should remain explicit, governed, and evidence-linked.

The priority is to preserve safe movement between active work, pause, re-entry, correction, and archival without implying autonomous memory, hidden state, or unreviewed continuity claims.

Continuity transitions should help the Human and organism resume, correct, or preserve work without losing meaning.

They must never replace Human authority over continuity.
