# Continuity Records v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the record structures that preserve continuity across time, context shifts, parallel workstreams, life events, public-surface activity, and implementation phases.

Continuity Records answer:

> What persisted across transition, what changed, what remained coherent, and how do we know?

Continuity Records capture the lineage of meaning, evidence, governance, workstream state, and identity movement.

They are not general logs.

They are not productivity records.

They are not behavioural surveillance.

They are governed continuity artefacts that preserve recoverable coherence.

---

## Record Types v0.1

Continuity Records may include:

* continuity lineage entries linking commits, posts, artefacts, evidence, and corrections;
* transition markers showing pauses, re-entries, and context shifts;
* workstream state anchors showing recoverable state after interruption;
* governance anchors showing boundaries, corrections, and decisions preserved across time;
* identity continuity anchors showing stable identity movement across cycles;
* recognition continuity anchors showing how recognised becoming remains linked to evidence;
* public-surface continuity anchors showing alignment between internal work and external signalling;
* re-entry notes that help the Human or organism resume without losing meaning.

These records must remain minimal, explicit, contextual, and evidence-linked.

---

## Record Structure v0.1

A Continuity Record should include:

* **timestamp** — when the continuity marker was created or observed;
* **workstream_or_context_id** — the relevant workstream, domain, artefact, or context;
* **continuity_marker_type** — the kind of continuity being preserved;
* **transition_context** — what changed, paused, resumed, corrected, or shifted;
* **evidence_links** — links or references to commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, screenshots, or other evidence objects;
* **human_interpretation_or_correction** — Human-authored interpretation, correction, ratification, or boundary note where applicable;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **re-entry_value** — what this record helps preserve or restore later;
* **boundary_note** — any caution needed to avoid overclaim, over-preservation, surveillance, or false continuity.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity Records should remain minimal, explicit, evidence-linked, and Human-interpreted.

The priority is to preserve recoverable coherence across meaningful transitions without turning every activity, interruption, draft, or behavioural signal into a permanent record.

Continuity Records must not become surveillance logs, behavioural scoring, productivity tracking, automated continuity claims, or hidden memory reconstruction.

Continuity Records should preserve what matters across transition while leaving noise, drift, and ungoverned artefacts behind.
