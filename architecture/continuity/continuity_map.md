# Continuity Map v0.1

## Purpose

This file provides a conceptual map of the Continuity Engine v0.1, showing how the continuity organs relate to each other.

Continuity Map answers:

> How do the parts of the Continuity Engine fit together?

This map is an orientation layer.

It helps readers understand the shape of the Continuity Engine without replacing the governed meaning contained in the individual files.

---

## High-Level Structure

Continuity Engine v0.1 consists of seven major domains:

1. **Foundations**
2. **Evidence & Records**
3. **Movement & Structure**
4. **Governance & Change**
5. **Identity & Roles**
6. **Lineage & Risk**
7. **Public Surface & Governance**

Each domain contains one or more continuity organs.

Together, these domains describe how the organism preserves coherence across time, interruption, correction, identity movement, public signalling, supersession, archival, and parallel workstreams.

## Cross-Organ Relationships

The Continuity Engine is not a flat file set. Its organs form relationships:

* **Evidence ↔ Records ↔ Markers**
  Continuity claims are supported by evidence, preserved in records, and signalled through markers.

* **Patterns ↔ Threads ↔ State Transitions ↔ Re-entry**
  Continuity moves through repeatable patterns, governed threads, explicit state transitions, and safe re-entry.

* **Decisions ↔ Corrections ↔ Supersession ↔ Archival**
  Continuity changes through governed choices, drift restoration, replacement, and closure.

* **Identity ↔ Roles ↔ Recognition**
  Continuity preserves who is acting, who holds authority, who is recognised, and which Human-AI boundaries apply.

* **Lineage ↔ Evidence ↔ Boundaries**
  Lineage shows the path of coherence, evidence substantiates the path, and boundaries prevent false continuity.

* **Public Surface ↔ Decisions ↔ Evidence**
  Public signalling must remain aligned with actual artefacts, decisions, corrections, and visible evidence.

* **Risks ↔ Corrections ↔ Boundaries**
  Risks reveal threats to coherence, corrections restore coherence, and boundaries prevent drift from recurring.

## Flow of Coherence v0.1

A typical continuity flow may look like this:

1. **Purpose defines why continuity matters.**
2. **Boundaries define what continuity must not become.**
3. **Evidence supports continuity claims.**
4. **Markers signal meaningful continuity movement.**
5. **Records preserve context.**
6. **Threads keep parallel workstreams distinct and recoverable.**
7. **Transitions move continuity between active, paused, re-entry, correction, and archived states.**
8. **Decisions shape what changes.**
9. **Corrections restore coherence when drift appears.**
10. **Supersession replaces outdated objects without erasing history.**
11. **Archival preserves closed lineage.**
12. **Lineage shows the path of coherence across time.**
13. **Identity and Roles protect Human authority and Human-AI boundaries.**
14. **Public Surface reflects internal continuity without overclaim.**
15. **Risks keep the organism alert to fragmentation, false stability, and hidden inference.**

This flow is conceptual.

Actual continuity may enter from any governed anchor.
