# Continuity Master Overview v0.1

## Purpose

This file provides a structured, hierarchical master overview of the Continuity Engine v0.1.

It consolidates the architecture, taxonomy, navigation, conceptual relationships, minimums, and meta-layer structure of all continuity organs and meta-layer artefacts without duplicating their full text.

The master overview is the canonical top-level navigational map of the continuity system.

It is descriptive and orienting.

It is not the source of governed meaning.

Governed meaning resides in the individual continuity organs.

---

# 1. Continuity Engine Structure

The Continuity Engine v0.1 consists of seven major domains:

1. Foundations
2. Evidence & Records
3. Movement & Structure
4. Governance & Change
5. Identity & Roles
6. Lineage & Risk
7. Public Surface & Governance

Each domain contains one or more continuity organs.

Together, these domains define how the organism preserves coherence across time, transition, interruption, correction, identity movement, public-surface activity, supersession, archival, and parallel workstreams.

# 4. Conceptual Architecture

## 4.1 Foundations

Foundations define the purpose, scope, and boundaries of continuity.

They explain why continuity exists and what it must not become.

## 4.2 Evidence & Records

Evidence & Records define what continuity rests on: evidence, context, and signals.

They protect continuity from becoming assertion, memory reconstruction, or hidden state.

## 4.3 Movement & Structure

Movement & Structure define how continuity moves, splits, merges, pauses, resumes, and restores coherence.

They support governed parallelism without collapsing workstreams into noise.

## 4.4 Governance & Change

Governance & Change define how continuity changes safely and how outdated, drifted, superseded, or closed material is corrected, replaced, or preserved.

## 4.5 Identity & Roles

Identity & Roles define who is acting, deciding, correcting, assisting, being recognised, or holding authority.

They preserve Human-AI boundaries and prevent role collapse.

## 4.6 Lineage & Risk

Lineage & Risk define continuity across time and the risks that threaten coherence.

They preserve the visible path of becoming while identifying false continuity, fragmentation, over-preservation, hidden state, and public-surface divergence.

## 4.7 Public Surface & Governance

Public Surface & Governance define how internal continuity aligns with external signalling and how continuity remains Human-anchored.

Public surface reflects coherence.

It does not create it.

# 5. Cross-Organ Relationships

## 5.1 Evidence & Records

* Evidence ↔ Records ↔ Markers
* Evidence ↔ Lineage
* Evidence ↔ Decisions

Evidence substantiates continuity claims.

Records preserve context.

Markers signal meaningful continuity movement.

## 5.2 Movement & Structure

* Patterns ↔ Threads
* Threads ↔ State Transitions
* Re-entry ↔ State Transitions

Patterns provide repeatable Human-governed movements.

Threads preserve bounded parallel workstreams.

Transitions and re-entry define how coherence moves safely across interruption.

## 5.3 Governance & Change

* Decisions ↔ Corrections ↔ Supersession ↔ Archival
* Corrections ↔ Public Surface
* Supersession ↔ Lineage
* Archival ↔ Lineage

Decisions shape continuity.

Corrections restore coherence.

Supersession replaces continuity objects without erasing history.

Archival preserves closed lineage without implying active continuity.

## 5.4 Identity & Roles

* Identity ↔ Roles
* Identity ↔ Governance
* Roles ↔ Human-AI Boundaries
* Identity ↔ Recognition

Identity defines who is acting, deciding, participating, or being recognised.

Roles define how participation occurs.

Human authority remains explicit.

## 5.5 Lineage & Risk

* Lineage ↔ Evidence
* Lineage ↔ Threads
* Risks ↔ Boundaries
* Risks ↔ Corrections

Lineage shows the path of coherence.

Risks identify what threatens coherence.

Boundaries and corrections protect the organism from carrying drift forward.

## 5.6 Public Surface

* Public Surface ↔ Evidence
* Public Surface ↔ Decisions
* Public Surface ↔ Corrections
* Public Surface ↔ Lineage

Public-surface activity must remain aligned with actual artefacts, evidence, decisions, corrections, and governed boundaries.
