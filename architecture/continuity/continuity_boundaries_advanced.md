# Continuity Boundaries Advanced v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines advanced boundary structures that govern continuity across complex transitions, multi-thread fields, identity movement, recognition, public-surface signalling, supersession, correction, archival, and lineage.

Advanced Continuity Boundaries answer:

> What limits ensure continuity remains safe, governed, and evidence-linked as complexity increases?

These boundaries extend the v1.0 baseline without expanding continuity into surveillance, hidden inference, behavioural profiling, productivity scoring, or autonomous reconstruction.

Advanced boundaries exist to preserve coherence under complexity without inflating continuity beyond evidence.

---

## Baseline vs Advanced Boundaries

The baseline Continuity Boundaries define what continuity must always protect:

* Human authority;
* evidence-linked coherence;
* meaning over volume;
* correction before drift hardens;
* v1.0 governance limits;
* protection from fragmentation;
* protection from surveillance and false stability.

Advanced Continuity Boundaries apply when complexity increases across:

* multiple parallel threads;
* public-surface signalling;
* recognition and identity movement;
* supersession;
* archival;
* lineage;
* correction;
* re-entry;
* complex governance decisions.

Advanced boundaries should not expand the implementation surface beyond v1.0 unless explicitly ratified.

They clarify how continuity remains safe when the organism becomes more complex.

---

## Advanced Boundary Types v0.1

Advanced boundaries may include:

* **Identity Boundaries** — preventing identity drift, role confusion, authority ambiguity, or responsibility misalignment.
* **Recognition Boundaries** — ensuring Recognition remains evidence-linked, contextual, non-hierarchical, and non-scoring.
* **Supersession Boundaries** — preventing false continuity when threads, artefacts, decisions, or versions are replaced.
* **Public-Surface Boundaries** — preventing divergence between internal work and external signalling.
* **Thread Boundaries** — preventing collapse, false merging, uncontrolled splitting, or fragmentation of parallel workstreams.
* **Lineage Boundaries** — preventing overclaim, noise preservation, behavioural profiling, or collapse of unrelated arcs.
* **Archival Boundaries** — preventing archived material from being treated as active continuity.
* **Correction Boundaries** — ensuring drift, ambiguity, overclaim, or fragmentation is corrected before continuation.
* **Re-entry Boundaries** — ensuring safe return does not rely on hidden memory or stale assumptions.
* **Decision Boundaries** — ensuring governed decisions remain evidence-linked, scoped, ratified, and correctable.

These boundaries preserve coherence without inflating continuity.

---

## Boundary Enforcement v0.1

Advanced boundaries should be enforced through:

* Continuity Markers;
* Continuity Records;
* Continuity Evidence;
* Continuity Lineage entries;
* Continuity Decisions;
* Continuity State Transitions;
* explicit Human interpretation;
* evidence-linked correction;
* Human ratification where required.

Boundaries must remain visible, reviewable, and correctable.

A boundary that cannot be inspected should not silently govern continuity.

---

## Boundary Failure Modes v0.1

An advanced boundary may fail when:

* identity or recognition drift occurs;
* supersession is unclear, incomplete, or unlinked;
* public-surface alignment collapses;
* lineage collapses unrelated threads into one false arc;
* archived material is mistaken for active state;
* drift is carried forward without correction;
* governance context is lost;
* hidden-state assumptions appear;
* evidence is used without context;
* Human authority becomes implicit rather than explicit;
* public claims exceed visible work;
* Recognition begins behaving like scoring, ranking, or reputation.

Failure should trigger correction, re-review, archival, supersession, or Human ratification — not continuation from a false state.

---

## Advanced Boundary Quality Criteria

An advanced boundary should be:

* **explicit** — visible enough to be inspected;
* **minimal** — no broader than necessary;
* **evidence-linked** — grounded in records, markers, lineage, decisions, or artefacts;
* **Human-interpreted** — meaningful under Human authority, not automatic inference;
* **scope-aware** — clear about where it applies and where it does not;
* **correctable** — capable of revision when evidence, governance, or context changes;
* **non-surveillant** — protecting coherence without tracking behaviour unnecessarily;
* **public-safe where needed** — preventing internal continuity assumptions from leaking into public claims.

Advanced boundaries protect coherence under complexity.

They should not create new forms of hidden control.
