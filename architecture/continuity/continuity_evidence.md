# Continuity Evidence v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the evidence structures, signals, and lineage anchors that allow the Continuity Engine to preserve coherence across time, load, context switches, life events, parallel workstreams, and implementation phases.

Continuity Evidence answers:

> What shows that the organism remained coherent across transition?

Continuity Evidence captures the organism’s ability to maintain meaning, evidence, governance, identity movement, and workstream integrity across time, load, and transition.

---

## Continuity Evidence May Include

Continuity Evidence may include:

* linked commits, posts, artefacts, and corrections forming a coherent lineage;
* timestamped transitions showing safe pauses, re-entries, and context shifts;
* preservation of meaning across multi-thread or multi-day work;
* governance-aligned corrections preventing drift or fragmentation;
* continuity signals such as meals, breaks, life events, or interruptions handled without collapse;
* public-surface alignment with actual work, including no overclaim and no divergence;
* evidence that Human authority remained intact across transitions;
* evidence that workstream state remained recoverable after interruption;
* evidence that prior decisions, boundaries, and corrections remained visible after context shifts;
* evidence that Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, and Identity remained aligned.

---

## Continuity Evidence Quality

Continuity Evidence should be:

* **relevant** — connected to a meaningful transition, workstream, correction, or re-entry;
* **contextual** — understandable within the surrounding governance and work history;
* **evidence-linked** — traceable to commits, posts, artefacts, records, corrections, or Human interpretation;
* **minimal** — preserving what matters without capturing unnecessary noise;
* **reviewable** — capable of being inspected, challenged, corrected, or revised;
* **Human-governed** — interpreted under Human authority rather than treated as automatic proof.

Continuity Evidence should help the organism preserve recoverable coherence.

It should not merely accumulate records.

---

## Interpretation Boundaries

Continuity Evidence must remain contextual, governed, and Human-interpreted.

Continuity Evidence is not:

* a metric;
* a score;
* a productivity measure;
* a behavioural profile;
* a claim that nothing changed;
* a claim that all memory was preserved;
* a substitute for Human review;
* proof of coherence without supporting lineage.

Continuity Evidence should support the question:

> Did the organism remain meaningfully coherent across transition?

It should not support the question:

> How fast or productive was the Human?

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity Evidence should remain minimal, explicit, and reviewable.

The priority is to support evidence that can show the organism remained coherent across interruptions, context switches, parallel workstreams, public-surface transitions, and implementation phases.

Continuity Evidence must not become surveillance, scoring, productivity tracking, behavioural profiling, or automated claims of coherence.

Evidence should support continuity only when interpreted through governance, context, Human review, and visible lineage.

Continuity Evidence exists to help the organism remember what matters across transition without reducing continuity to metrics.
