# Continuity Public Surface v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how continuity is preserved across public-surface activity: posts, commits, documentation, announcements, articles, release notes, and external signalling.

Continuity Public Surface answers:

> How does the organism maintain coherence between internal work and external representation?

Public-surface continuity protects trust, alignment, evidence integrity, and public interpretability.

Public-surface signals should reflect the organism’s actual state.

They should not create authority by themselves.

---

## Public-Surface Components v0.1

Continuity Public Surface may include:

* posts, updates, articles, or announcements;
* repo commits, tags, release notes, or public documentation changes;
* architectural statements or public doctrine;
* public corrections, clarifications, or withdrawals;
* public-surface lineage markers;
* public recognition or contribution signals;
* alignment checks between internal work and external claims;
* evidence links connecting public statements to visible artefacts.

Public-surface signals must remain evidence-linked.

---

## Public-Surface Alignment Requirements v0.1

Public-surface alignment requires:

* internal artefacts match external claims;
* external claims remain evidence-supported;
* no overclaim or underclaim;
* divergence is corrected before continuation;
* public-surface markers reflect actual work;
* public recognition and identity signals remain evidence-linked;
* governance boundaries remain visible externally where appropriate;
* public language distinguishes fact, interpretation, candidate doctrine, and vision;
* public statements do not expose governed/internal continuity material unintentionally.

Public-surface alignment protects coherence and trust.

---

## Surface-to-Evidence Alignment

A public-surface signal should be linked to at least one of the following where appropriate:

* repo commit;
* public documentation artefact;
* public architecture file;
* release note or tag;
* visible correction;
* ratified public statement;
* public-facing recognition event;
* public-safe evidence summary;
* Human-governed interpretation.

Public-surface continuity should allow an observer to see that the public claim corresponds to real work.

It should not require hidden context to be credible.

---

## Public-Surface Correction Rules v0.1

When public-surface divergence is detected, the organism should:

* pause before amplifying the claim further;
* identify whether the issue is overclaim, underclaim, ambiguity, stale information, or broken lineage;
* correct the internal artefact, public statement, or both;
* preserve the correction where relevant;
* clarify whether the prior public signal is superseded, corrected, withdrawn, or still valid with conditions;
* resume only when public-surface alignment is restored.

Correction protects trust.

It is not reputational failure.

---

## Public-Surface Boundaries

Public-surface continuity must not:

* imply hidden state;
* imply memory reconstruction;
* overclaim progress or coherence;
* conceal drift or fragmentation;
* convert signalling into productivity scoring;
* treat public surface as authoritative over evidence;
* expose governed/internal material without Human authority;
* present candidate doctrine as ratified canon;
* present aspiration as implementation evidence;
* collapse public narrative and private continuity substrate.

Public-surface signals reflect coherence.

They do not create it.

---

## Public-Surface Failure Modes v0.1

Public-surface failure may occur when:

* external claims diverge from internal artefacts;
* corrections are not reflected publicly where needed;
* lineage breaks between internal and external work;
* recognition signals drift from evidence;
* governance boundaries are unclear externally;
* public language overstates implementation maturity;
* public language understates meaningful progress;
* private continuity assumptions leak into public claims;
* old public signals continue to circulate after supersession.

Failure should trigger correction, clarification, withdrawal, supersession, archival, or Human review — not continuation from a false public state.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity Public Surface should remain minimal, explicit, Human-governed, and evidence-linked.

The priority is to preserve alignment between what the organism is doing and what the public surface says the organism is doing.

Public-surface continuity protects trust without implying hidden memory, autonomous continuity, productivity scoring, or public authority over evidence.

Public signals should let the work speak for itself without claiming more than the work can bear.
