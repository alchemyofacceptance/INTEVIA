# Continuity Examples Advanced v0.1

## Purpose

This file provides advanced, abstract examples of continuity movements involving multiple threads, boundaries, governance actions, identity movement, public-surface alignment, correction, supersession, archival, and lineage.

Continuity Examples Advanced answers:

> What do complex continuity movements look like when several organs interact?

These examples do not reference real individuals, organisations, private data, or governed internal material.

They are illustrative only.

They show governed movement patterns without implying automation, prediction, hidden state, or real-world implementation maturity.

## Example 1: Multi-Thread Drift and Correction

### Situation

Two parallel threads diverge in interpretation of a shared requirement.

### Movement

* divergence is detected across threads;
* boundary check is triggered;
* correction is created;
* evidence is linked from both threads;
* decision is ratified;
* threads are realigned.

### Organs Involved

* `continuity_threads.md`
* `continuity_corrections.md`
* `continuity_boundaries.md`
* `continuity_evidence.md`
* `continuity_decisions.md`

### What This Demonstrates

This example demonstrates how governed parallelism can remain coherent when multiple threads begin to diverge.

The key continuity movement is:

> divergence → boundary check → correction → decision → realignment

### What This Does Not Imply

This does not imply automatic thread monitoring, autonomous correction, or that all parallel divergence is harmful.
