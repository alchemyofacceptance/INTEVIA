# Continuity Re-entry v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines how the organism supports safe, governed re-entry after interruptions, context shifts, life events, device shifts, public-surface activity, or multi-day gaps.

Continuity Re-entry answers:

> How does the Human safely resume without losing meaning?

Re-entry is not memory reconstruction.

Re-entry is not automatic resume.

Re-entry is the restoration of coherence using evidence, markers, records, boundaries, and Human interpretation.

---

## Re-entry Requirements v0.1

A safe re-entry should include:

* review of relevant Continuity Markers;
* review of relevant Continuity Records;
* review of relevant Continuity Evidence;
* re-establishment of meaning and boundaries;
* confirmation of workstream state;
* identification of stale assumptions;
* correction of any drift detected during re-entry;
* alignment with public-surface signalling if applicable;
* reaffirmation of Human authority.

Re-entry is complete only when coherence is restored.

If coherence cannot be restored, the organism should pause, correct, archive, or re-scope rather than continue from a false state.

---

## Re-entry Patterns v0.1

### 1. Evidence-First Re-entry

Use when returning after interruption, context shift, or multi-day gap.

Pattern:

* review relevant markers and records;
* inspect evidence links;
* identify last-known governed state;
* resume only when evidence supports continuity.

---

### 2. Boundary Re-entry

Use when governance, scope, public-surface claims, or v1.0 boundaries may have shifted.

Pattern:

* re-check active boundaries;
* identify any outdated assumptions;
* reaffirm Human authority;
* resume only inside current governance limits.

---

### 3. Correction Re-entry

Use when drift, ambiguity, overclaim, or fragmentation is detected during return.

Pattern:

* identify the drift;
* correct before continuing;
* preserve the correction as evidence where needed;
* resume only after coherence is restored.

---

### 4. Workstream Re-entry

Use when returning to a specific thread inside a parallel field.

Pattern:

* identify the workstream;
* review its last-known state;
* separate it from adjacent threads;
* confirm dependencies;
* resume from an evidence-linked anchor.

---

### 5. Public-Surface Re-entry

Use when public posts, repo commits, articles, or external signalling have moved alongside internal work.

Pattern:

* compare public surface with actual artefacts;
* identify divergence, overclaim, or underclaim;
* correct before continuing;
* preserve alignment evidence where useful.

---

## Re-entry Failure Modes v0.1

Re-entry may fail when:

* evidence is missing or incomplete;
* continuity markers are absent or unclear;
* drift was not corrected earlier;
* workstream state is unrecoverable;
* identity boundaries are unclear;
* Recognition records are detached from evidence;
* public-surface alignment is broken;
* governance boundaries have changed;
* Human authority was not re-established;
* the system assumes memory that is not evidence-supported.

Failure modes should trigger correction, not continuation.

---

## Re-entry Boundaries

Re-entry must not:

* imply hidden state;
* reconstruct memory without evidence;
* assume coherence because the work feels familiar;
* preserve stale assumptions;
* bypass Human review;
* convert interruption into failure;
* treat all context as equally important;
* resume speed before restoring meaning.

Re-entry restores coherence.

It does not prove that nothing was lost.

## Boundary Note

For v1.0, Continuity Re-entry should remain Human-governed, evidence-linked, explicit, and minimal.

The priority is to help the Human and organism resume safely after interruption without losing meaning, carrying drift forward, or pretending that missing context has been reconstructed.

Re-entry restores coherence.

It does not reconstruct memory.
