# Continuity Glossary v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines key Continuity Engine terms for v0.1.

The glossary provides shared language for reading, reviewing, and implementing the Continuity Engine without replacing the fuller meaning contained in the individual architecture files.

Definitions are intentionally minimal.

---

## Glossary

### Continuity

The governed preservation of coherence across time, transitions, interruptions, corrections, lineage, identity movement, public-surface activity, and parallel workstreams.

### Continuity Engine

The INTEVIA architecture layer responsible for preserving coherence across meaningful transitions without becoming surveillance, productivity scoring, hidden memory, or autonomous reconstruction.

### Coherence

The maintained alignment of meaning, evidence, governance, identity, roles, decisions, workstream state, and public representation.

### Boundary

A constraint that prevents drift, overclaim, hidden inference, role collapse, surveillance, false continuity, or governance erosion.

### Baseline Boundary

A core continuity constraint that applies generally across v1.0 continuity.

### Advanced Boundary

A higher-specificity boundary used when continuity becomes more complex across identity movement, recognition, supersession, public surface, lineage, threads, or archival.

### Evidence

Governed artefacts, links, records, corrections, decisions, posts, commits, or other inspectable objects that substantiate continuity claims.

### Record

A structured preservation of continuity context, including what persisted, what changed, and how coherence can later be reviewed or restored.

### Marker

A minimal signal indicating that continuity was paused, resumed, corrected, aligned, archived, or otherwise moved.

### Pattern

A repeatable Human-governed continuity structure used to maintain or restore coherence across transitions.

### Thread

A governed parallel continuity workstream that preserves a distinct lane of meaning, evidence, state, and lineage.

### State Transition

A governed movement from one continuity state to another, such as active, paused, re-entry, correction, or archived continuity.

### Re-entry

A safe, Human-governed return to continuity after interruption, context shift, life event, device shift, or multi-day gap.

### Decision

A governed choice that shapes continuity, including boundaries, corrections, recognition, identity, supersession, archival, workstream movement, or public-surface alignment.

### Correction

A governed restoration of coherence after drift, ambiguity, fragmentation, overclaim, false continuity, broken lineage, or boundary confusion.

### Supersession

A governed replacement of one continuity object with another while preserving lineage between the superseded object and its successor.

### Archival

The governed preservation of closed lineage when a thread, decision, artefact, recognition event, or identity movement is no longer active.

### Lineage

The visible continuity arc across time: the path of coherence linking commits, posts, artefacts, evidence, corrections, decisions, transitions, recognition, and identity movement.

### Identity

The governed structure of who is acting, deciding, correcting, participating, holding responsibility, or being recognised.

### Role

The function performed within continuity, such as governor, contributor, reviewer, ratifier, assistant, executor, risk surface node, or workstream actor.

### Authority

The governed capacity to decide, ratify, correct, bind, supersede, archive, or enforce continuity boundaries.

### Human Governor

The Human authority holder who governs, ratifies, corrects, sequences, interprets, and preserves sovereignty over continuity.

### AI Assistant

An AI role that drafts, analyses, synthesises, proposes, surfaces risks, or supports continuity within governed boundaries.

### AI Executor

An AI role that performs bounded tasks under Human authority.

### Recognition

The governed reflection of evidenced becoming back to a Human, contributor, participant, or identity-linked actor.

### Risk

A condition that threatens coherence, including false continuity, hidden state, over-preservation, fragmentation, boundary erosion, identity drift, public-surface divergence, or unrecoverable state.

### Public Surface

External signalling such as posts, commits, documentation, announcements, articles, release notes, or public recognition that must remain aligned with internal evidence.

### Public-Surface Alignment

The state in which external claims remain consistent with internal artefacts, evidence, governance boundaries, and actual work.

### Governance

Human-anchored structures that keep continuity safe, bounded, evidence-linked, correctable, and coherent.

### Drift

A movement away from governed coherence, including meaning drift, boundary drift, public-surface drift, identity drift, recognition drift, or lineage drift.

### False Continuity

The appearance of coherence where evidence, governance, lineage, identity, or workstream state does not support it.

### Hidden State

An unsupported assumption that continuity, memory, authority, or context exists invisibly without evidence or Human-governed preservation.

### Memory Reconstruction

The false or unsupported reconstruction of continuity from assumed context rather than evidence, records, markers, lineage, and Human interpretation.

### Recoverable Coherence

A state in which enough meaning, evidence, records, markers, decisions, and boundaries remain available for safe re-entry or review.

### v1.0 Constraint

A disciplined implementation boundary limiting Continuity Engine behaviour to explicit, minimal, Human-governed, evidence-linked structures.

---

## Boundary Note

Definitions are minimal and non-exhaustive.

Meaning is governed by the individual files.

The glossary supports shared understanding, but it does not replace evidence, Human interpretation, or governed continuity.
