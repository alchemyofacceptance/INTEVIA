These questions support orientation, review, implementation planning, troubleshooting, and Human-governed interpretation.

They guide reading.

## Diagnostic Use

Continuity Queries may be used diagnostically.

When coherence is unclear, ask:

* **Purpose** — what is this continuity preserving?
* **Boundary** — what must this not become?
* **Evidence** — what supports the claim?
* **Record** — what context must be preserved?
* **Marker** — what continuity movement occurred?
* **Thread** — which workstream is affected?
* **Transition** — what state changed?
* **Re-entry** — can coherence be safely restored?
* **Decision** — what governed choice is needed?
* **Correction** — what drift must be repaired?
* **Supersession** — what replaces what?
* **Archival** — what should close into lineage?
* **Lineage** — how does the path remain visible?
* **Identity** — who is acting, deciding, or being recognised?
* **Role** — what function is being performed?
* **Risk** — what threatens coherence?
* **Public Surface** — does the external signal match the evidence?

These queries help the Human locate the right continuity organ before continuing.
