# Continuity Roles v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the governed roles that participate in continuity: Human roles, AI-assistance roles, governance roles, recognition roles, identity roles, and workstream-specific roles.

Continuity Roles answer:

> Who is acting, deciding, correcting, interpreting, ratifying, or participating in continuity?

Roles are not hierarchy.

Roles are not scoring.

Roles are not behavioural profiles.

Roles are governed boundaries that preserve coherence across transitions, lineage, corrections, decisions, recognition, identity, supersession, archival, public-surface signalling, and parallel workstreams.

---

## Role Principles v0.1

Continuity Roles must remain:

* **Human-anchored** — authority originates with the Human;
* **evidence-linked** — roles are grounded in visible artefacts, records, markers, decisions, or ratification, not inference;
* **boundary-safe** — roles do not collapse into each other;
* **non-scoring** — roles do not imply rank, reputation, productivity, or value hierarchy;
* **non-surveillant** — roles do not track behaviour beyond continuity needs;
* **explicit** — roles must be visible, inspectable, correctable, and supersedable;
* **scope-aware** — roles apply within specific contexts and should not silently govern unrelated threads;
* **Human-interpreted** — role meaning remains governed by Human authority.

Roles preserve coherence, not hierarchy.

---

## Role, Identity, and Authority

Roles, Identity, and Authority are related but distinct.

* **Role** defines the function being performed in a continuity context.
* **Identity** defines who or what is participating, acting, being recognised, or holding responsibility.
* **Authority** defines who may decide, ratify, correct, govern, or bind the organism.

A role does not automatically grant authority.

Recognition does not automatically create authority.

AI assistance does not become Human ratification.

Public-surface identity does not override governed continuity evidence.

The Human remains the authority holder.

## Core Role Types v0.1

Continuity may involve the following governed roles:

* **Human Governor** — holds authority and ratifies decisions, corrections, supersession, identity movement, and boundaries.
* **Human Contributor** — authors, edits, interprets, or advances workstreams.
* **Human Interpreter** — clarifies meaning, boundaries, drift, coherence, or public-surface alignment.
* **Human Reviewer** — validates coherence, boundaries, evidence, and lineage.
* **Human Ratifier** — confirms decisions, corrections, supersession, archival, recognition, or identity movement.
* **Public-Surface Author** — governs external signalling, posts, articles, documentation, or announcements.
* **AI Assistant** — drafts, analyses, synthesises, surfaces risks, proposes corrections, or supports continuity within governed limits.
* **AI Executor** — performs bounded tasks under Human authority.
* **Continuity Support Node** — helps structure markers, records, lineage, decisions, re-entry, archival, and thread coherence without holding authority.
* **Risk Surface Node** — identifies drift, ambiguity, overclaim, fragmentation, boundary issues, or false continuity.
* **Recognition Actor** — participates in recognition events or identity-linked contributions.
* **Identity Holder** — the Human or entity whose role, authority, responsibility, participation, or recognition state is being clarified.
* **Workstream Actor** — any role participating in a specific continuity thread.
* **Governance Actor** — participates in decisions, corrections, supersession, archival, boundary enforcement, or ratification processes.

Roles must remain distinct unless explicitly changed, merged, superseded, or archived under Human authority.

## Role Structure v0.1

A role entry should include:

* **timestamp** — when the role was assigned, confirmed, corrected, superseded, or archived;
* **role_type** — the governed role being defined;
* **role_context** — the workstream, decision, correction, recognition, identity, public-surface, or governance context;
* **evidence_links** — records, markers, decisions, recognition evidence, commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, or other evidence objects;
* **continuity_markers** — relevant identity, recognition, boundary, correction, lineage, public-surface, archival, or supersession markers;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **role_value** — what coherence the role preserves, clarifies, protects, or enables;
* **scope_limit** — where the role applies and where it does not;
* **supersession_or_archival_link** — prior or successor role where applicable;
* **human_interpretation_or_ratification** — Human-authored confirmation, correction, interpretation, or authority note;
* **boundary_note** — cautions against role collapse, false authority, behavioural profiling, hidden-state assumptions, or public-surface overclaim.

Roles must remain explicit and evidence-linked.

## Human Roles

Human roles may include:

* **Governor** — ultimate authority holder.
* **Contributor** — produces, edits, or advances artefacts.
* **Interpreter** — clarifies meaning, boundaries, drift, or coherence.
* **Reviewer** — validates evidence, coherence, lineage, and boundary integrity.
* **Ratifier** — confirms decisions, corrections, supersession, archival, recognition, or identity movement.
* **Public-Surface Author** — governs external signalling.
* **Boundary Holder** — protects v1.0 constraints, public-safe limits, and continuity boundaries.
* **Re-entry Governor** — confirms that coherence has been restored after interruption or context shift.

Human roles must not be inferred from behaviour alone.

They must be explicitly assigned, confirmed, corrected, or ratified.

---

## AI Roles

AI roles must remain bounded:

* **Assistant** — drafts, analyses, synthesises, proposes, or surfaces risks.
* **Executor** — performs bounded tasks under Human authority.
* **Continuity Support Node** — helps structure continuity without holding authority.
* **Risk Surface Node** — identifies drift, ambiguity, fragmentation, overclaim, or boundary issues.
* **Synthesis Node** — helps connect evidence, meaning, and lineage for Human review.
* **Implementation Node** — supports bounded build, documentation, or artefact creation under governed direction.

AI roles must not:

* imply authority;
* imply identity;
* imply memory;
* imply self-governance;
* collapse into Human roles;
* act without Human ratification where required;
* treat generated output as governed decision;
* treat model context as continuity evidence;
* treat assistance as authorship unless explicitly ratified;
* replace Human interpretation, correction, or authority.

The Human remains the governor.

---

## Role Boundaries

Continuity Roles must not:

* imply hidden state;
* imply memory reconstruction;
* collapse Human and AI roles;
* treat roles as ranking or scoring;
* convert participation into behavioural profiling;
* treat public-surface identity as authoritative over governed evidence;
* allow role drift across threads without evidence;
* override governance boundaries;
* preserve ambiguity as continuity;
* imply authority without Human ratification;
* allow AI-generated continuity to masquerade as Human-governed continuity;
* allow archived or superseded roles to govern active work by default.

Roles protect coherence.

They do not create hierarchy.

---

## Role Failure Modes v0.1

Role continuity may fail when:

* Human and AI roles collapse;
* authority boundaries become ambiguous;
* AI assistance is mistaken for Human ratification;
* Recognition drifts into scoring, status, or hierarchy;
* identity lineage breaks across decisions;
* role lineage breaks across threads;
* supersession is unclear or unlinked;
* archived or superseded roles continue to govern active work;
* public-surface identity signals diverge from internal evidence;
* role assumptions are inferred without evidence;
* Human authority becomes implicit rather than explicit;
* role scope silently expands beyond its governed context.

Failure should trigger correction, re-review, supersession, archival, or Human ratification.
