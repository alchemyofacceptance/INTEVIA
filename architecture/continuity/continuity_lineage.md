# Continuity Lineage v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines Continuity Lineage: the governed structure that links commits, posts, artefacts, evidence, corrections, decisions, transitions, and identity movements into a coherent trail across time.

Continuity Lineage answers:

> How do we see the path of coherence across transitions?

Lineage is not a log.

Lineage is not a transcript.

Lineage is not full memory.

Lineage is the minimal structure that preserves the shape of becoming.

---

## Lineage Components v0.1

Continuity Lineage may include:

* linked commits forming a coherent development arc;
* linked posts forming a coherent public-surface arc;
* linked artefacts forming a coherent implementation arc;
* linked corrections forming a coherent governance arc;
* linked recognition events forming a coherent identity arc;
* linked decisions forming a coherent authority arc;
* linked transitions forming a coherent continuity arc;
* linked archival events preserving closed threads as lineage;
* linked re-entry events showing how coherence was restored after interruption.

Lineage is the connective tissue of continuity.

---

## Lineage Arc Types v0.1

Continuity Lineage may preserve several kinds of coherent arcs:

* **Development Lineage** — how commits, implementation artefacts, and architecture files form a coherent build path.
* **Public-Surface Lineage** — how posts, public statements, repo surfaces, and external signalling remain aligned with actual work.
* **Governance Lineage** — how decisions, corrections, boundaries, and ratifications shape the organism over time.
* **Recognition Lineage** — how evidenced becoming remains connected to Human identity, contribution, and meaning.
* **Identity Lineage** — how identity movement remains stable across records, roles, authority, and participation.
* **Correction Lineage** — how drift, ambiguity, or overclaim was detected, corrected, and prevented from reappearing.
* **Archival Lineage** — how closed or superseded threads remain preserved without being mistaken for active continuity.
* **Re-entry Lineage** — how the Human or organism safely resumed after interruption, pause, or context shift.

These arcs may overlap, but they should not be collapsed into a single undifferentiated history.

---

## Lineage Structure v0.1

A lineage entry should include:

* **timestamp** — when the lineage link was created, observed, or preserved;
* **lineage_type** — the kind of lineage arc being preserved;
* **evidence_links** — commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, decisions, screenshots, records, or other evidence objects;
* **continuity_markers** — relevant markers showing pause, re-entry, correction, boundary, public-surface, identity, recognition, lineage, or archival movement;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **lineage_value** — what this lineage preserves for future interpretation or re-entry;
* **supersession_or_archival_link** — successor thread, archived thread, or replaced artefact where applicable;
* **human_interpretation_or_ratification** — Human-authored meaning, correction, boundary note, or ratification where applicable;
* **boundary_note** — any caution needed to prevent false continuity, overclaim, hidden-state assumptions, or thread collapse.

Lineage entries must remain minimal, explicit, contextual, and evidence-linked.

---

## Lineage Quality Criteria

Continuity Lineage should be:

* **coherent** — linking related evidence into a meaningful arc;
* **bounded** — clear about where the arc starts, changes, pauses, resumes, or ends;
* **evidence-linked** — traceable to visible or governed evidence;
* **interpretable** — understandable without requiring hidden memory;
* **Human-governed** — interpreted under Human authority;
* **non-exhaustive** — preserving the path of coherence, not the full terrain;
* **thread-aware** — avoiding collapse of unrelated workstreams into one false narrative.

Lineage should help the organism see how meaning moved across transition.

It should not attempt to preserve everything.

---

## Lineage Boundaries

Lineage must not:

* imply hidden state;
* imply memory reconstruction;
* imply continuity where evidence is missing;
* preserve noise or drafts as lineage;
* collapse unrelated threads into a single arc;
* convert lineage into behavioural profiling;
* overclaim coherence;
* treat every timestamp as meaningful;
* treat archived lineage as active state;
* erase correction, drift, pause, or supersession events;
* replace Human interpretation.

Lineage preserves the path, not the full terrain.

---

## Lineage vs Archive

Lineage and Archival are related but distinct.

Lineage shows the coherent path across time.

Archival preserves a thread when it is no longer active.

A lineage arc may include archived material, but archived material should not be treated as live continuity unless explicitly reopened under Human governance.
