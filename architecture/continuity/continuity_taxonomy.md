# Continuity Taxonomy v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines the hierarchical taxonomy of the Continuity Engine v0.1.

It organises all continuity organs into a structured, multi-level classification so the file set can be understood, reviewed, extended, and implemented without losing conceptual coherence.

Continuity Taxonomy answers:

> How are the Continuity Engine organs classified?

The taxonomy is descriptive.

It is not an authority hierarchy.

It is not a maturity model.

It is not an implementation sequence.

Governed meaning remains in the individual continuity organs.

## Taxonomy Interpretation

The taxonomy helps organise the Continuity Engine into conceptual domains:

* **Foundations** explain why continuity exists and how it remains safe.
* **Evidence & Records** explain how continuity claims are supported and preserved.
* **Movement & Structure** explain how continuity moves and resumes.
* **Governance & Change** explain how continuity is corrected, replaced, or closed.
* **Identity & Roles** explain who acts and who holds authority.
* **Lineage & Risk** explain how coherence is traced and protected.
* **Public Surface & Governance** explain how continuity remains aligned with external representation and Human authority.

These classifications are connected.

They should not be read as isolated silos.
