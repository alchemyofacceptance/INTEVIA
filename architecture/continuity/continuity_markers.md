# Continuity Markers v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines Continuity Markers: the minimal signals that show where continuity was preserved, paused, corrected, resumed, aligned, or archived.

Continuity Markers answer:

> What signals show that continuity was maintained across transition?

Markers are not logs.

Markers are not full records by themselves.

Markers are coherence anchors.

A marker indicates that a meaningful continuity event occurred. A Continuity Record preserves the fuller context. Continuity Evidence substantiates the claim. Continuity State Transitions define the governed movement.

---

## Marker Types v0.1

Continuity Markers may include:

* **Pause Marker** — indicates a safe interruption and preserved state.
* **Re-entry Marker** — indicates a Human-governed return and restored coherence.
* **Correction Marker** — indicates drift, ambiguity, or fragmentation was corrected before continuation.
* **Boundary Marker** — indicates a governance boundary was applied, reaffirmed, or restored.
* **Lineage Marker** — links commits, posts, artefacts, evidence, and corrections into a coherent trail.
* **Public-Surface Marker** — indicates alignment between internal work and external signalling.
* **Identity Marker** — indicates stable identity movement across cycles.
* **Recognition Marker** — indicates recognised becoming remained linked to evidence and identity.
* **Workstream State Marker** — indicates recoverable state after interruption.
* **Archive Marker** — indicates continuity has shifted from active state to preserved lineage.

Markers must remain minimal, explicit, contextual, and evidence-linked.

---

## Marker Structure v0.1

A Continuity Marker should include:

* **timestamp** — when the marker was created or observed;
* **marker_type** — the type of continuity marker;
* **workstream_or_context_id** — the relevant workstream, domain, artefact, or context;
* **transition_context** — what paused, resumed, corrected, aligned, or changed;
* **evidence_links** — links or references to commits, posts, artefacts, corrections, screenshots, records, or other evidence objects;
* **human_interpretation_or_correction** — Human-authored interpretation, correction, ratification, or boundary note where applicable;
* **linked_organs** — Recognition, Corpus, Governance Engine, Identity, or other relevant domains;
* **re-entry_value** — what this marker helps preserve or recover later;
* **boundary_note** — any caution needed to avoid hidden-state assumptions, overclaim, over-preservation, or false continuity.

---

## Marker Quality Criteria

A Continuity Marker should be:

* **minimal** — small enough to act as a signal, not a full narrative;
* **specific** — tied to a recognisable transition, correction, pause, re-entry, alignment, or archival event;
* **evidence-linked** — connected to visible or governed evidence;
* **Human-interpreted** — meaningful under Human governance, not automatically inferred;
* **recoverable** — useful for safe re-entry or future review;
* **bounded** — clear about what it does and does not prove.

Markers should help the organism preserve coherence.

They should not accumulate noise.

---

## Marker Boundaries

Markers must not:

* imply hidden state;
* imply memory reconstruction;
* imply continuity where evidence is missing;
* convert ordinary activity into surveillance;
* over-preserve noise or drafts;
* turn every interruption into a continuity event;
* replace Human interpretation;
* claim more coherence than the evidence supports.

Markers preserve coherence, not volume.
