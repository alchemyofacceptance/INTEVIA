# Continuity Crosslinks v0.1

## Purpose

This file defines explicit cross-organ linkages across the Continuity Engine v0.1.

Crosslinks show how continuity organs depend on, constrain, support, or inform each other.

Continuity Crosslinks answer:

> How do the Continuity Engine organs hold each other in coherence?

Crosslinks are not authority.

Crosslinks are relationship markers.

They clarify how continuity meaning moves between files without overriding the boundaries of any individual organ.

---

## Foundational Crosslinks

### Purpose ↔ Boundaries

* Purpose defines what continuity protects.
* Boundaries define how continuity remains safe.
* Purpose without boundaries risks over-expansion.
* Boundaries without purpose risk becoming abstract constraint.

### Boundaries ↔ All Organs

* Boundaries constrain evidence, records, markers, patterns, threads, transitions, re-entry, decisions, corrections, supersession, archival, lineage, identity, roles, risks, and public-surface activity.
* Every continuity organ must remain Human-governed, evidence-linked, explicit, and non-surveillant.
* Boundaries prevent continuity from becoming hidden memory, productivity scoring, behavioural profiling, autonomous reconstruction, or false stability.

### Advanced Boundaries ↔ Complex Continuity Contexts

* Advanced Boundaries apply when continuity crosses multiple threads, identity movement, recognition, supersession, public-surface signalling, archival, lineage, or correction.
* Advanced Boundaries clarify complexity without expanding v1.0 implementation scope by default.

## Evidence & Records Crosslinks

### Evidence ↔ Records

* Evidence substantiates continuity claims.
* Records preserve continuity context.
* A record without evidence risks becoming assertion.
* Evidence without records may lack context for future re-entry.

### Evidence ↔ Markers

* Markers signal where continuity evidence matters.
* Evidence substantiates what a marker indicates.
* A marker without evidence risks false continuity.
* Evidence without markers may remain difficult to interpret across transition.

### Records ↔ Markers

* Markers indicate meaningful continuity movement.
* Records preserve fuller continuity context.
* Markers are signals; records are structured preservation.

### Evidence ↔ Lineage

* Lineage is built from evidence across time.
* Evidence gives lineage inspectability.
* Lineage gives evidence continuity context.

## Movement & Structure Crosslinks

### Patterns ↔ Threads

* Patterns define repeatable Human-governed movements.
* Threads instantiate those movements within bounded workstreams.
* Patterns help threads pause, resume, correct, align, split, merge, and archive without losing coherence.

### Threads ↔ State Transitions

* Threads move through continuity states.
* State Transitions define governed movement between active, paused, re-entry, correction, and archived continuity.
* Thread state should not change without evidence-linked transition context.

### Re-entry ↔ State Transitions

* Re-entry is a controlled transition back into continuity.
* Re-entry uses records, markers, evidence, boundaries, and Human interpretation to restore coherence.
* Re-entry must not rely on hidden state or assumed memory.

### Re-entry ↔ Evidence

* Re-entry depends on evidence review.
* Evidence-first re-entry prevents stale assumptions and false continuity.

---

## Governance & Change Crosslinks

### Decisions ↔ Evidence

* Decisions must be evidence-linked.
* Evidence supports, challenges, or limits decisions.
* Decisions without evidence risk false authority.

### Decisions ↔ Markers

* Markers may signal where a decision affected continuity.
* Decisions may create, confirm, correct, supersede, or archive markers.

### Decisions ↔ Records

* Records preserve decision context.
* Decisions shape what records mean and how they govern future continuity.

### Corrections ↔ Decisions

* Corrections may revise, clarify, supersede, or archive decisions.
* Decisions may ratify corrections.
* Correction protects the organism from carrying drift forward as continuity.

### Corrections ↔ Evidence

* Corrections must be supported by evidence where possible.
* Evidence shows what drifted, what was corrected, and why the correction matters.

### Corrections ↔ Public Surface

* Public-surface divergence may trigger correction.
* Corrections may require public clarification, withdrawal, or supersession.

### Supersession ↔ Archival

* Supersession replaces.
* Archival preserves.
* A superseded object may be archived as lineage.
* Archived material should not govern active work unless explicitly restored under Human governance.

### Corrections ↔ Supersession

* Corrections may lead to supersession when repair is insufficient.
* Supersession may preserve correction history so replacement does not erase drift.

## Identity & Roles Crosslinks

### Identity ↔ Roles

* Identity defines who is acting, deciding, correcting, participating, or being recognised.
* Roles define how that actor participates in a continuity context.
* Role clarity protects identity continuity.
* Identity clarity prevents role collapse.

### Identity ↔ Recognition

* Recognition may inform identity movement.
* Recognition must remain evidence-linked, contextual, and non-scoring.
* Recognition must not become hierarchy, reputation, or behavioural profile.

> `continuity_recognition.md` is future / optional unless created and governed in the repository.

### Identity ↔ Governance

* Authority boundaries shape identity.
* Identity entries should clarify who holds authority, responsibility, participation, or recognition context.
* Human authority must remain explicit.

### Roles ↔ Human-AI Boundaries

* Roles preserve the distinction between Human authority and AI assistance.
* AI roles may draft, analyse, synthesise, execute, or surface risks within boundaries.
* AI roles must not imply authority, identity, memory, or Human ratification.

## Lineage & Risk Crosslinks

### Lineage ↔ Evidence

* Lineage is built from evidence.
* Evidence substantiates the path of coherence.
* Lineage without evidence risks narrative overclaim.

### Lineage ↔ Threads

* Threads form lineage arcs.
* Lineage helps preserve the path of a thread across pause, re-entry, correction, supersession, and archival.

### Lineage ↔ Decisions

* Decisions shape the path of coherence.
* Lineage shows how decisions affected continuity over time.

### Lineage ↔ Archival

* Archival preserves closed threads as lineage.
* Lineage may include archived material, but archived material should not be treated as active state.

### Risks ↔ Boundaries

* Risks define why boundaries exist.
* Boundaries mitigate continuity risks.
* Risk visibility helps prevent false continuity, hidden state, over-preservation, fragmentation, boundary erosion, identity drift, and public-surface divergence.

### Risks ↔ Corrections

* Risks may trigger correction.
* Corrections reduce the chance that drift becomes hidden structure.

## Public Surface & Governance Crosslinks

### Public Surface ↔ Evidence

* Public claims must be evidence-linked.
* Public-surface continuity should allow an observer to see that public claims correspond to real work.
* Public surface reflects coherence; it does not create it.

### Public Surface ↔ Decisions

* Public-surface claims may require governed decisions.
* Decisions may clarify whether a public signal is valid, corrected, superseded, withdrawn, or archived.

### Public Surface ↔ Corrections

* Divergence triggers public-surface correction.
* Public-surface correction protects trust and coherence.

### Public Surface ↔ Lineage

* Public posts, commits, announcements, and documentation can form public-surface lineage.
* Public lineage must remain aligned with internal evidence and actual artefacts.

### Public Surface ↔ Governance

* Governance ensures public signalling remains bounded, evidence-linked, and Human-authorised.
* Public-surface activity must not expose governed/internal continuity material without Human authority.

> `continuity_governance.md` is future / optional unless created and governed in the repository.

## Crosslink Quality Criteria

A continuity crosslink should be:

* **explicit** — visible enough to inspect;
* **bounded** — clear about what relationship it describes;
* **evidence-aware** — linked to evidence where claims depend on evidence;
* **Human-interpreted** — meaningful under Human governance;
* **non-authoritative by itself** — clarifying relationship, not creating governance authority;
* **correctable** — capable of revision when files, decisions, or continuity objects change.

Crosslinks help the organism understand relationships.

They do not replace the organs they connect.

## Boundary Note

Crosslinks clarify relationships but do not override individual organ boundaries.

A crosslink is not evidence by itself.

A crosslink is not authority by itself.

A crosslink helps the organism preserve coherence by making relationships visible, inspectable, and correctable.

Governed meaning remains in the individual continuity organs.
