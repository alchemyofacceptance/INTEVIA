## Purpose

Define a documentation-only constitutional mechanism called the Governance Valve.

The Governance Valve describes how expansion, mutation, and boundary transitions are regulated within the INTEVIA organism.

It is a conceptual instrumentation surface. It does not create runtime enforcement, automation, behavioural logic, command execution, routing, parsing, discovery, dynamic registration, or source-code mutation.

## Definition

The Governance Valve is a documentation-only constitutional instrumentation surface.

It describes how the organism preserves coherence when moving between:

- preparation and mutation;
- candidate surfaces and implementation authority;
- open sprint activity and sealed lineage;
- documentation-only expansion and runtime behaviour;
- internal vocabulary and executable command surfaces.

The Governance Valve is not executable.

It does not enforce rules by itself.

It names the governance logic that must be applied by the Human-governed workflow.

## Governance Functions

### 1. Expansion Flow

The Governance Valve describes how new surfaces may be introduced.

Expansion is permitted only when:

- the surface is inside an authorised sprint boundary;
- the surface is listed or implied by the candidate expansion perimeter;
- the surface remains documentation-only unless later implementation is explicitly authorised;
- the surface does not exceed the v1.0 boundary;
- the surface does not create appetite-driven or unbounded growth.

Expansion flow is governed movement, not acceleration for its own sake.

### 2. Mutation Control

The Governance Valve describes how mutation is permitted or held.

Mutation is permitted only when:

- Human authorisation is explicit;
- the intended mutation scope is narrow;
- pre-flight confirms repository cleanliness;
- affected paths match the authorised surface;
- VC reviews Terminal Echo before commit selection;
- commit and push remain separate gates.

Mutation control protects the repo from narrative momentum.

### 3. Boundary Transitions

The Governance Valve describes how sprint and artefact transitions are sequenced.

Boundary transitions must preserve the sequence:

- boundary before manifest;
- manifest before candidate expansion;
- candidate expansion before evidence-bearing work;
- evidence before commit;
- commit before push;
- push before public-surface confirmation;
- seal before lineage lock.

Boundary transitions may not be skipped, collapsed, or inferred from narrative.

### 4. Constitutional Invariants

The Governance Valve records non-negotiable invariants that remain active across Sprint 2:

- the Human is the governor;
- ME proposes;
- Human executes;
- VC reviews Terminal Echo;
- narrative is not lineage;
- documentation is not runtime;
- candidate surfaces are not implementation authority;
- sealed Sprint 1 lineage is not reopened;
- no `src/` mutation occurs without explicit Work Block authorisation;
- commit and push remain separate gates;
- the repo speaks.
