# Governance 2.0 — Public Anchor

**Status:** Public explanatory note  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Public Surface:** Approved for public repository visibility  
**Relationship to Internal Codex:** Public-safe translation of INTEVIA’s internal constitutional governance principles  

---

## Core Thesis

Governance 1.0 asks:

**How do we make good decisions?**

Governance 2.0 asks:

**Which decisions must never become choosable, even if everyone later agrees?**

This is the missing layer: governance systems that can govern their own future decision-space.

Some decisions must be structurally constrained at the architectural level.

---

## Why This Matters

AI-enabled systems are no longer just tools.

They can become infrastructure.

Once a tool becomes infrastructure, the real question is no longer only whether it works.

The question becomes:

- who holds authority;
- what consent actually means;
- whether people can leave;
- whether evidence remains visible;
- whether trust can be converted into dependency;
- whether the system can be captured, buried, or inverted over time.

Policy alone is not sufficient when a system can be extracted from, captured, or inverted by default.

For INTEVIA, the boundary has to be architectural.

---

## INTEVIA’s Public Commitment

INTEVIA is designed as a human-anchored governance architecture.

Its internal constitutional principles are designed to preserve:

- human authority;
- meaningful consent;
- exit integrity;
- evidence and lineage;
- public-benefit purpose;
- protection against capture, distortion, and misuse;
- responsible use of AI-enabled systems.

INTEVIA may grow, earn, partner, teach, and serve institutions.

But it must not do so by exploiting people, trapping dependency, obscuring exit, or turning trust into rent.

---

## Public-Safe Summary

INTEVIA is committed to remaining a human-anchored, purpose-driven organisation protected against capture, distortion, and misuse.

Its governance architecture is designed to ensure that growth remains aligned with human agency, meaningful consent, evidence, exit, and responsible use of technology.

## Keeper Lines

Governance 1.0 governs decisions.

Governance 2.0 governs the future decision-space itself.

Some decisions are outside the permitted decision-space.

Open does not mean extractable.

Public does not mean permissionless.

If a system can be extracted from, captured, or inverted by default, then policy alone is not sufficient.

The boundary has to be architectural.
