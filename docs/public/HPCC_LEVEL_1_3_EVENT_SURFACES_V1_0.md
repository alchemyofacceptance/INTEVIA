# HPCC Level 1–3 — Event Surfaces v1.0

## Status: Draft for mutation

## Purpose: Define HPCC Level 1–3 Event identity surfaces

## Scope: Field events, evidence-bearing moments, meaning movement, governance movement, sequencing movement, reversibility, emergence-safety observations, and lineage formation

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Event identity only; not certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, accreditation, deployed capability, runtime behaviour, trigger activation, or assessment activation

---

## 1. Purpose of the Event Surfaces

Event Surfaces define how HPCC may recognise and describe evidence-bearing moments inside a governed learning field.

An Event is not automatically a trigger.

An Event is not automatically an assessment.

An Event is not automatically an activation.

An Event is a governed moment where something relevant to practice becomes observable.

Events may include:

* meaning-field movement;
* governance-field movement;
* sequencing movement;
* reversible decision movement;
* boundary movement;
* emergence-safety observations;
* lineage movement.

Events are not assessments, evaluations, runtime behaviours, automatic triggers, or certification events by themselves.

They do not imply capability, mastery, admission, ranking, transformation, certification, or external validation.

Events are evidence-bearing surfaces.

They become useful only when they are captured, interpreted, stabilised, and governed.

## 3. Event Principles

### A. Meaning Behaviour First

Events should stabilise meaning before behaviour is interpreted.

If meaning is unclear, the Event Surface should say so.

### B. Governance Behaviour Before Insight

Events should stabilise governance before insight becomes claim.

A notable event without governance remains an observation, not doctrine.

### C. Sequencing Behaviour Before Acceleration

Events should respect sequencing discipline.

The event record should preserve what happened, in what order, under what authority, and with what correction path.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Event interpretations should remain correctable and reversible where appropriate.

Not every event can be reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Behaviour Safety

Events should not destabilise emergence or treat emergence as proof.

Emergence is not mastery.

Emergence is not doctrine.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Behaviour Integrity

Events should preserve HPCC lineage threads honestly.

The event record should show:

* what happened;
* what evidence exists;
* what changed;
* what did not change;
* what remains unresolved;
* what must not be overclaimed.

### G. Non-Automation

An event being observed does not mean a system action should automatically occur.

Human review remains required before an event becomes escalation, doctrine, synthesis, assessment, publication, or lineage anchor.

## 4. HPCC Level Identity

HPCC Levels 1–3 represent event complexity, not Human hierarchy.

### Level 1 — Meaning Stability Events

Level 1 events may describe:

* meaning stability behaviour;
* meaning drift behaviour;
* meaning correction behaviour;
* basic sequencing behaviour;
* low-complexity reversible decision behaviour;
* foundational boundary behaviour.

### Level 2 — Governance Stability Events

Level 2 events may describe:

* governance reflex behaviour;
* authority movement behaviour;
* boundary enforcement behaviour;
* governance sequencing behaviour;
* evidence interpretation behaviour;
* medium-complexity reversible decision behaviour.

### Level 3 — Emergence Stability Events

Level 3 events may describe, where evidence and governance allow:

* stability under multi-layer load;
* emergence safety behaviour;
* emergence restraint behaviour;
* emergence reversibility where possible;
* multi-layer behaviour;
* lineage-sensitive reversibility;
* high-complexity boundary behaviour.

Level 3 is especially sensitive.

Level 3 Event Surfaces do not imply Level 3 certification, readiness, mastery, credentialing, or external validation.

Levels do not imply:

* advancement;
* capability tiering;
* ranking;
* mastery;
* transformation;
* certification;
* Human superiority.

Levels are event surfaces only.

## 5. Event Surface Structure v1.0

Event Surfaces may contain three governed layers.

These layers are candidate structure, not fixed obligation.

---

### Layer 1 — Behaviour Layer

Defines how governed field behaviour may become observable across:

* meaning behaviour;
* governance behaviour;
* sequencing behaviour;
* reversible decision behaviour;
* authority movement behaviour;
* stability under load where relevant;
* emergence-safety behaviour where relevant;
* lineage behaviour.

Purpose:

> To describe what became observable without judging the Human.

---

### Layer 2 — Stability Layer

Defines how field behaviour may stabilise across:

* meaning behaviour stability;
* governance behaviour stability;
* sequencing behaviour stability;
* reversible decision behaviour stability;
* boundary behaviour stability;
* stability-under-load evidence where relevant;
* emergence-safety stability where relevant;
* lineage behaviour stability.

Purpose:

> To identify whether the field remained stable enough for Human review, correction, and continued governance.

---

### Layer 3 — Lineage Layer

Defines how field behaviour may become lineage across:

* lineage behaviour anchors;
* lineage behaviour threads;
* lineage behaviour stabilisation;
* lineage correction;
* lineage supersession;
* lineage coherence;
* lineage preservation.

Purpose:

> To preserve what the event allows HPCC to remember without pretending the event proves more than the evidence supports.

## 6. Event Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Events do not measure productivity.

### B. No Capability Implication

Events do not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Events do not evaluate participants as Humans.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Events do not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Event-Making

Participants should not be pressured to manufacture events.

“Nothing happened yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Event Surfaces must not become hidden ranking, scoring, certification, or engagement validation.

### G. No Surveillance Posture

Event Surfaces should remain governed evidence surfaces, not monitoring tools.

### H. No Drift

Events must protect meaning before behaviour is interpreted.

### I. No Automatic Triggering

Events must not imply automatic escalation, runtime activation, or system action.

## 8. Consent and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before Event Surfaces are used, participants should understand:

* what counts as an event;
* what does not count as an event;
* what evidence may be captured;
* why event evidence may be used;
* who may see event records;
* whether event records may contribute to synthesis;
* whether event records may be anonymised;
* whether event records may be excluded;
* how correction requests work;
* how boundary enforcement works;
* whether AI may assist in organising or summarising event evidence.

Consent is part of the Event Surface.

Event capture without consent boundaries risks becoming surveillance or extraction.

Your evidence belongs to you before it belongs to the cohort.
