# Consent Surface v1.0

Status: v1.0 structural surface  
Scope: Consent Surface Skeleton / test-case protection  
Runtime state: Not activated  
Public-surface posture: Articulation surface, not functional consent system

---

## Documentation Boundary

This surface is documentation only. It preserves inspectability and Human
authority without activating runtime behaviour, product, pilot, certification,
deployment, workflow, routing, review, audit, or readiness claims.


## 1. Definition

The Consent Surface Skeleton is the minimal governed consent structure required for v1.0 test-case protection.

It links a Human Node to consent state, consent band, consent flags, consent window, and a consent-history placeholder.

This surface does not activate consent.

It does not interpret consent.

It does not enact consent.

It creates inspectable structure only.

---

## 2. v1.0 Scope

Consent Surface Skeleton v1.0 exists to support:

- Human Node ↔ Consent linkage;
- minimal consent-state visibility;
- minimal consent-band visibility;
- consent-flag visibility;
- consent-window placeholder;
- consent-history placeholder;
- test-case protection;
- future governed design.

It does not create a consent-management system, consent workflow, notification system, CARE workflow, evidence cadence, queue mechanism, review router, cohort process, or runtime activation layer.

---

## 3. Runtime Boundaries

Consent Surface Skeleton v1.0 does not implement:

- behaviour;
- automation;
- activation;
- consent workflows;
- consent interpretation;
- notification systems;
- CARE workflows;
- evidence cadence;
- queue logic;
- review routing;
- cohort logic;
- v1.1 surfaces;
- runtime activation.

Any future activation must be separately designed, reviewed, authorised, and evidenced.

---

## 4. Governance

The Consent Surface Skeleton is governed by the same authority boundary as the wider INTEVIA v1.0 build:

The Human remains the governor.

AI-assisted structures may help describe, organise, inspect, and prepare consent-related data, but they do not authorise consent, interpret consent, enact consent, request consent, withdraw consent, notify anyone, route review, trigger CARE workflows, start evidence cadence, move queues, move cohorts, or activate runtime behaviour.

The skeleton exists to make later consent governance more inspectable, not to replace governance.

---

## 5. Human Node ↔ Consent Linkage Model

The Consent Surface links to a `HumanNode`.

The Human Node provides the inspectable structural anchor for identity and relationship posture.

The Consent Surface provides a non-activating consent posture attached to that Human Node.

This linkage does not create consent authority.

It does not make the Human Node active.

It does not create behavioural obligations.

It creates a minimal inspection point for future governed consent handling.

---

## 6. Consent-State Model

The v1.0 Consent Surface reuses the consent-state vocabulary from the Relationship Surface Skeleton:

- `unknown`
- `not_requested`
- `requested`
- `granted`
- `withdrawn`

These states do not define a full consent-management system.

They do not activate communications.

They do not interpret consent.

They do not replace future consent instrumentation.

They provide minimal visibility for consent handling under v1.0 test-case protection.

---

## 7. Consent-Band Model

The v1.0 Consent Surface includes a minimal consent-band vocabulary:

- `none`
- `minimal`
- `standard`
- `extended`
- `restricted`

These bands are descriptive only.

They do not authorise data handling.

They do not define consent scope.

They do not activate workflows.

They provide a structural vocabulary for future governed design.

---

## 8. Consent-Window Model

The `consent_window` field is a reserved structural field for possible future temporal consent handling.

It does not create expiry logic.

It does not create renewal logic.

It does not notify anyone.

It does not alter consent state.

It creates a future place for bounded consent-window design under separate governance.

---

## 9. Consent-History Model

The `consent_history` field is an empty placeholder list.

It does not create audit logic.

It does not record events automatically.

It does not validate consent history.

It does not replace future evidence or audit instrumentation.

It creates a future place for consent-history entries under separate governance.

---

## 10. Consent-Flag Model

The `consent_flags` field is a reserved structural field for future inspection signals.

It does not create enforcement logic.

It does not create automated holds.

It does not replace Human judgement.

It creates a future place for bounded consent indicators.
