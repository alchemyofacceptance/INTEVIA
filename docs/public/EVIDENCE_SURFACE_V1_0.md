# Evidence Surface v1.0

Status: v1.0 public documentation surface (non-activating)

Purpose: Define the documentation-only role and boundaries of evidence within
the INTEVIA v1.0 protective spine. This surface supports Human interpretation
and external inspectability without activating runtime behaviour, workflow
logic, routing logic, review logic, audit logic, readiness claims, evidence
verification, evidence validation, compliance checking, audit execution, or
operational evidence-state assignment.

---

## Documentation Boundary

This surface is documentation only. It preserves inspectability and Human
authority without activating runtime behaviour, product, pilot, certification,
deployment, workflow, routing, review, audit, or readiness claims.


## 1. Definition

The Evidence Surface Skeleton is the minimal governed evidence structure required for v1.0 test-case protection.

It provides an inspectable holding structure for pre-mutation evidence, lineage evidence, governance evidence, packet evidence, commit evidence, and audit-safe snapshots.

It links a Human Node, Consent Surface, Notification Surface, and Queue Surface to evidence state, evidence band, evidence flags, evidence window, evidence history placeholder, and evidence items placeholder.

This surface does not activate evidence.

It does not interpret evidence.

It does not route evidence.

It does not review evidence.

It does not imply audit execution or governance workflow.

It creates inspectable structure only.

---

## 4. Governance

The Evidence Surface Skeleton is governed by the same authority boundary as the wider INTEVIA v1.0 build:

The Human remains the governor.

AI-assisted structures may help describe, organise, inspect, and prepare evidence-related data, but they do not authorise evidence interpretation, evidence routing, evidence review, audit workflow, CARE workflow, evidence cadence, cohort movement, or runtime activation.

The skeleton exists to make later evidence governance more inspectable, not to replace governance.

## 5. Human Node ↔ Evidence Linkage Model

The Evidence Surface links to a `HumanNode`.

The Human Node provides the inspectable structural anchor for identity, relationship, consent posture, channel posture, and governance flags.

The Evidence Surface provides a non-activating evidence posture attached to that Human Node.

This linkage does not create evidence authority.

It does not make the Human Node active.

It does not create audit obligations.

It creates a minimal inspection point for future governed evidence handling.
