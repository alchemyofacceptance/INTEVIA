# Identity Profile Skeleton v1.0

Status: v1.0 public documentation surface (non-activating)

Purpose: Define the documentation-only role and boundaries of the Identity
Profile within the INTEVIA v1.0 protective spine. This surface supports Human
interpretation and external inspectability without activating runtime behaviour,
workflow logic, routing logic, review logic, audit logic, readiness claims, or
operational identity assignment.
Surface type: Public articulation surface
Runtime effect: None
Cohort effect: None
Certification effect: None
v1.1 effect: None

---

## Documentation Boundary

This surface is documentation only. It preserves inspectability and Human
authority without activating runtime behaviour, product, pilot, certification,
deployment, workflow, routing, review, audit, or readiness claims.


## 1. Definition

The Identity Profile Skeleton is the minimal governed identity structure required for v1.0 test-case protection.

It creates a bounded structure for holding identity-related state without activating runtime behaviour, cohort logic, notification workflows, CARE workflows, dashboards, or v1.1 surfaces.

This is an articulation surface, not a functional dashboard.

---

## 2. v1.0 Scope

The v1.0 Identity Profile Skeleton defines inspectable fields for:

* Human identity anchoring;
* cohort-state marking;
* queue-band marking;
* evidence consent;
* notification-preference cadence;
* publication boundary;
* email preference state;
* pause state;
* exit state;
* CARE touchpoint placeholders;
* review packet linkage;
* governance flags.

The structure exists to protect identity handling during bounded v1.0 test-case formation.

It does not admit, evaluate, rank, certify, transform, onboard, or activate any Human or organism.

---

## 3. Runtime Boundaries

The Identity Profile Skeleton must not implement:

* dashboard logic;
* queue UI;
* CARE workflows;
* notification system;
* review automation;
* cohort selection logic;
* cohort activation;
* v1.1 surfaces;
* runtime activation.

The presence of fields does not imply that the behaviours represented by those fields are active.

The field exists so future handling can remain inspectable before any runtime behaviour is authorised.

---

## 4. Governance

The Identity Profile Skeleton is governed by Human authority, bounded mutation, and evidence-aware identity handling.

It must preserve:

* consent visibility;
* reversibility;
* pause and exit protection;
* publication-boundary clarity;
* review packet linkage;
* non-evaluative identity handling;
* no implication of admission, selection, readiness, ranking, certification, or external validation.

The skeleton protects the Human by making identity state explicit without allowing the structure to shape the Human.

---

## 5. Consent Model

The consent model is structural only.

The `evidence_consent` field records whether evidence handling has been consented to within the bounded v1.0 context.

The `publication_boundary` field records the boundary under which identity-linked material may or may not be represented.

Consent fields do not create consent by themselves.

Consent remains governed, reversible, inspectable, and subject to Human review.

---

## 6. Notification-Preference Model

The notification-preference model is structural only.

The `email_opt_in` field records whether email contact is permitted.

The `cadence_band` field records the bounded preference band for contact cadence.

The `email_last_sent` and `email_next_window` fields create inspectable placeholders for future notification discipline.

These fields do not activate a notification system.

No email workflow, automation, queue, reminder, or outreach behaviour is activated by this skeleton.

---

## 7. Cohort-State Model

The cohort-state model is structural only.

The `cohort_state` field records bounded v1.0 state without implying cohort admission, cohort activation, certification readiness, participant selection, or evaluation.

The skeleton must not be read as a cohort membership record.

It does not activate HPCC cohorts, Organism Cohorts, training pathways, admission processes, or participant onboarding.

---

## 8. Queue-State Model

The queue-state model is structural only.

The `queue_band` field records a bounded queue-related marker without creating a queue UI, queue workflow, prioritisation engine, ranking mechanism, or selection process.

Queue state must not be interpreted as status, worth, capability, readiness, admission likelihood, or priority entitlement.

It is a governance placeholder only.

---

## 9. CARE Touchpoint Placeholder

The `care_touchpoint_placeholder` field provides a bounded location for future CARE-related linkage.

It does not activate CARE workflows.

It does not create therapeutic, clinical, coaching, safeguarding, support, or case-management behaviour.

It exists only so future CARE-related identity handling can remain inspectable if separately authorised.

---

## 10. Review Packet Linkage

The `review_packet_linkage` field provides a bounded location for future review-packet references.

It does not automate review.

It does not ratify evidence.

It does not authorise cohort activation, publication, certification, onboarding, or external representation.

It exists to preserve lineage between identity handling and governed review surfaces.

---

## 11. Governance Flags

The `governance_flags` field provides a bounded location for explicit governance warnings, holds, pauses, boundary markers, or review requirements.

Governance flags do not replace Human judgement.

They create inspectable markers that can support Human review.

---

## 12. v1.1 Runway

The Identity Profile Skeleton may support future v1.1 design work by preserving a clean structural boundary around identity handling.

This does not activate v1.1 surfaces.

This does not imply readiness for dashboards, cohorts, CARE workflows, notifications, runtime behaviour, certification pathways, or external validation.

Any v1.1 expansion requires separate Human authorisation, governed mutation, and review.
