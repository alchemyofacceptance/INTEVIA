# Notification Surface v1.0

Status: v1.0 structural surface  
Scope: Notification Surface Skeleton / test-case protection  
Runtime state: Not activated  
Public-surface posture: Articulation surface, not functional notification system

---

## Documentation Boundary

This surface is documentation only. It preserves inspectability and Human
authority without activating runtime behaviour, product, pilot, certification,
deployment, workflow, routing, review, audit, or readiness claims.


## 1. Definition

The Notification Surface Skeleton is the minimal governed notification structure required for v1.0 test-case protection.

It links a Human Node and Consent Surface to notification channel posture, notification flags, notification window, and a notification-history placeholder.

This surface does not activate notifications.

It does not interpret notifications.

It does not deliver notifications.

It does not enact any notification workflow.

It creates inspectable structure only.

---

## 2. v1.0 Scope

Notification Surface Skeleton v1.0 exists to support:

- Human Node ↔ Notification linkage;
- Consent Surface ↔ Notification linkage;
- minimal notification-channel visibility;
- notification-flag visibility;
- notification-window placeholder;
- notification-history placeholder;
- test-case protection;
- future governed design.

It does not create a notification-management system, delivery system, notification workflow, CARE workflow, evidence cadence, queue mechanism, review router, cohort process, or runtime activation layer.

---

## 3. Runtime Boundaries

Notification Surface Skeleton v1.0 does not implement:

- behaviour;
- automation;
- activation;
- notification workflows;
- notification interpretation;
- delivery systems;
- CARE workflows;
- evidence cadence;
- queue logic;
- review routing;
- cohort logic;
- v1.1 surfaces;
- runtime activation.

Any future activation must be separately designed, reviewed, authorised, and evidenced.

---

## 4. Governance

The Notification Surface Skeleton is governed by the same authority boundary as the wider INTEVIA v1.0 build:

The Human remains the governor.

AI-assisted structures may help describe, organise, inspect, and prepare notification-related data, but they do not authorise notification, interpret notification need, send messages, schedule messages, trigger delivery, route review, trigger CARE workflows, start evidence cadence, move queues, move cohorts, or activate runtime behaviour.

The skeleton exists to make later notification governance more inspectable, not to replace governance.

---

## 5. Human Node ↔ Notification Linkage Model

The Notification Surface links to a `HumanNode`.

The Human Node provides the inspectable structural anchor for identity, relationship, consent posture, channel posture, and governance flags.

The Notification Surface provides a non-activating notification posture attached to that Human Node.

This linkage does not create notification authority.

It does not make the Human Node active.

It does not create communication obligations.

It creates a minimal inspection point for future governed notification handling.

---

## 6. Consent Surface ↔ Notification Linkage Model

The Notification Surface links to a `ConsentSurface`.

The Consent Surface provides the non-activating consent posture associated with the Human Node.

The Notification Surface may reference that consent posture structurally, but it does not interpret consent.

It does not infer notification permission.

It does not send messages.

It does not create consent-driven notification behaviour.

This linkage exists only to preserve future governed design space.

---

## 7. Notification-Channel Model

The v1.0 Notification Surface reuses the notification-channel vocabulary from the Relationship Surface Skeleton.

These channel states are descriptive only.

They do not activate delivery systems.

They do not schedule communication.

They do not imply consent.

They do not trigger notification workflows.

They provide minimal visibility for notification handling under v1.0 test-case protection.

---

## 8. Notification-Window Model

The `notification_window` field is a reserved structural field for possible future temporal notification handling.

It does not create scheduling logic.

It does not create reminder logic.

It does not notify anyone.

It does not alter notification channel state.

It creates a future place for bounded notification-window design under separate governance.

---

## 9. Notification-History Model

The `notification_history` field is an empty placeholder list.

It does not create audit logic.

It does not record events automatically.

It does not validate notification history.

It does not replace future evidence or audit instrumentation.

It creates a future place for notification-history entries under separate governance.

---

## 10. Notification-Flag Model

The `notification_flags` field is a reserved structural field for future inspection signals.

It does not create enforcement logic.

It does not create automated holds.

It does not replace Human judgement.

It creates a future place for bounded notification indicators.
