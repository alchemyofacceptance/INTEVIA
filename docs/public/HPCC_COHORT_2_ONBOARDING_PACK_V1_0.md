## 1. Welcome to Cohort 2

Welcome to the candidate onboarding surface for HPCC Cohort 2.

Cohort 2 is not an advancement cohort.

Cohort 2 is a refinement cohort.

It is intended to deepen governed practice across:

* meaning protection;
* governance reflex;
* sequencing under load;
* authority movement;
* reversible decisions;
* evidence interpretation;
* emergence-safety preparation;
* lineage anchoring.

Cohort 2 should build only on what Cohort 1 actually evidences.

It does not imply capability tiering, ranking, certification, transformation, admission, mastery, or external validation.

In plain terms:

> **Cohort 2 is not promotion.
> Cohort 2 is refinement.**

## 5. Cohort 2 Cadence Overview

Cohort 2 cadence is intended to be refined and governance-first.

A candidate cadence may include:

* weekly Field Notes;
* weekly Reflection Sheets;
* bi-weekly Evaluation Events;
* monthly Meaning Preservation exercises;
* monthly Governance Sequencing exercises;
* periodic Authority Movement reviews;
* periodic Reversible Decision reviews;
* controlled Emergence Safety exercises where appropriate;
* continuous reversible governance;
* close-out synthesis.

This cadence is not final.

Cohort 2 Formation Surfaces define candidate structure, not a fixed schedule.

If evidence shows overload, the cadence should slow down.

If evidence shows under-instrumentation, the cadence may add structure.

The Human-governed evidence decides.

## 8. Meaning Protection Guidance

Meaning protection is the foundation of Cohort 2.

Participants may practise:

* meaning preservation;
* meaning correction;
* meaning clarification;
* meaning resonance where evidence supports it.

Meaning protection helps prevent:

* drift;
* collapse;
* misinterpretation;
* overclaim;
* extraction;
* unintended transformation;
* system-owned meaning.

Meaning resonance, if used, should remain evidence-bound.

It should not be treated as proof, doctrine, mastery, or personal elevation.

## 9. Governance Guidance

Governance stabilises the field before insight is allowed to become claim.

Participants may practise:

* authority movement governance;
* governance sequencing;
* boundary recognition;
* boundary enforcement;
* evidence interpretation;
* public-surface restraint where applicable.

Governance supports:

* sovereignty;
* reversibility;
* lineage integrity;
* non-collapse correction;
* responsible interpretation.

The question is not:

> How impressive was the output?

The question is:

> Could the Human still inspect, correct, refuse, reverse, and govern the work?

## 10. Sequencing Guidance

Sequencing is the backbone of reversible governance.

Participants may practise:

* sequencing discipline;
* sequencing under load;
* decision staging;
* evidence-before-claim habits;
* correction-before-publication habits;
* cognitive–meaning coupling where evidence supports it.

Sequencing supports:

* stability;
* clarity;
* reversibility;
* lineage;
* safer acceleration.

Cognitive–meaning coupling is a sensitive advanced concept.

It should remain evidence-bound and Human-governed.

It should not be treated as proof of Level 3 mastery.

---

## 11. Reversibility Guidance

Reversibility is the governance safety valve.

Participants may practise:

* low-complexity reversible decisions;
* medium-complexity reversible decisions;
* high-complexity reversible decisions where appropriate;
* correction of decisions before they harden;
* lineage repair;
* public-surface hold where needed.

Reversibility helps prevent:

* collapse;
* overcommitment;
* premature claim;
* irreversible drift;
* unintended transformation;
* unsupported authority movement.

Not every decision can be fully reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

---

## 12. Emergence-Safety Guidance

Emergence must remain controlled, bounded, and reversible where possible.

Participants may practise:

* stability under load;
* emergence recognition;
* emergence restraint;
* boundary checks during acceleration;
* lineage preservation during multi-layer work;
* pausing when the field becomes too dense to govern cleanly.

Emergence does not prove anything by itself.

Emergence is not doctrine.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

Cohort 2 may prepare participants to notice and handle emergence safely, but it does not imply Level 3 certification, mastery, or readiness.

## 13. Consent and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before participating, each participant should understand:
