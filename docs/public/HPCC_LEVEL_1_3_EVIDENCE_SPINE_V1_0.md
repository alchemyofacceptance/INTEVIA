## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Evidence Spine identity only; not assessment activation, certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, accreditation, deployed capability, or runtime activation

---

## 1. Purpose of the Evidence Spine

The Evidence Spine is the governed structure that binds HPCC Levels 1–3 together.

It defines how HPCC evidence may be:

* captured;
* interpreted;
* stabilised;
* preserved;
* corrected;
* superseded;
* linked to lineage;
* bounded by Human sovereignty.

The Evidence Spine is not an assessment system by itself.

It does not imply scoring, ranking, capability tiering, mastery, transformation, admission, certification, accreditation, or external validation.

The Evidence Spine is the governed backbone of HPCC evidence work.

It helps HPCC ask:

> What evidence exists?
> What does it support?
> What does it not support?
> What must remain unresolved?
> What requires correction, follow-up, or Human review?

## 3. Evidence Principles

### A. Meaning First

Evidence must protect meaning before interpretation.

If meaning is unclear, the evidence record should say so.

No evidence statement should overwrite participant meaning.

### B. Governance Before Insight

Evidence must remain governed before insight becomes claim.

A strong insight without governed evidence remains a candidate observation.

### C. Sequencing Before Acceleration

Evidence must preserve sequencing.

The Evidence Spine should show what happened, in what order, under what authority, and with what correction path.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Evidence interpretations should remain correctable and reversible where appropriate.

Not every event can be reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Safety

Evidence should not destabilise emergence or treat emergence as proof.

Emergence is not mastery.

Emergence is not doctrine.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Integrity

Evidence must preserve HPCC lineage threads honestly.

The Evidence Spine should record:

* what evidence was created;
* what it connects to;
* what it changes;
* what it does not change;
* what remains unresolved;
* what must not be overclaimed.

### G. Correction and Supersession

Evidence may be incomplete, incorrect, ambiguous, superseded, or later clarified.

The Evidence Spine must support correction and supersession rather than pretending every record remains final.

Correction protects lineage.

Supersession protects truthfulness.

## 4. HPCC Level Identity

HPCC Levels 1–3 represent evidence complexity, not Human hierarchy.

### Level 1 — Meaning Stability Evidence

Level 1 evidence may capture:

* meaning stability;
* meaning drift detection;
* meaning correction;
* basic sequencing;
* low-complexity reversible decisions;
* foundational boundary awareness.

### Level 2 — Governance Stability Evidence

Level 2 evidence may capture:

* governance reflex;
* authority movement;
* boundary enforcement;
* governance sequencing;
* evidence interpretation;
* medium-complexity reversible decisions.

### Level 3 — Emergence Stability Evidence

Level 3 evidence may capture:

* stability under multi-layer load;
* meaning resonance where evidenced;
* cognitive–meaning coupling where evidenced;
* emergence safety;
* emergence restraint;
* emergence reversibility where possible;
* multi-layer behaviour;
* lineage-sensitive reversibility.

Level 3 is especially sensitive.

Level 3 evidence does not imply Level 3 certification, readiness, mastery, credentialing, or external validation.

Levels do not imply:

* advancement;
* capability tiering;
* ranking;
* mastery;
* transformation;
* certification;
* Human superiority.

Levels are evidence surfaces only.

## 5. Evidence Spine Structure v1.0

The Evidence Spine consists of three primary surfaces.

### 1. Field Notes Surfaces — Evidence Capture

Field Notes capture participant-authored evidence of governed practice, including:

* meaning behaviour;
* governance reflex;
* sequencing under load;
* reversible decisions;
* authority movement;
* stability under load where relevant;
* emergence-safety observations where relevant;
* lineage threads.

Field Notes are evidence surfaces.

They are not surveillance.

They are not hidden scoring.

They should help the practitioner see their own practice becoming governable.

### 2. Reflection Sheet Surfaces — Evidence Interpretation

Reflection Sheets support participant-governed interpretation of evidence, including:

* meaning interpretation;
* governance interpretation;
* sequencing interpretation;
* reversible decision interpretation;
* stability under load interpretation where relevant;
* emergence-safety interpretation where relevant;
* lineage interpretation.

Reflection Sheets may include introspection, but they must not become forced introspection.

They should help the participant understand their own evidence without surrendering meaning.

### 3. Evaluation Event Surfaces — Evidence Stabilisation

Evaluation Events stabilise evidence inside the governance field, including:

* meaning interpretation;
* governance interpretation;
* sequencing interpretation;
* reversible decision interpretation;
* stability under load interpretation where relevant;
* emergence-safety interpretation where relevant;
* lineage interpretation.

Evaluation Events do not judge the Human.

They interpret evidence inside the governance field.

## 6. Evidence Spine Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Evidence does not measure productivity.

### B. No Capability Implication

Evidence does not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Evidence does not evaluate participants as Humans.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Evidence does not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Evidence

Participants should not be pressured to manufacture evidence.

“Nothing changed yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Evidence surfaces must not become hidden ranking, scoring, certification, or engagement validation.

### G. No Surveillance Posture

Evidence surfaces should remain governed learning surfaces, not monitoring tools.

### H. No Drift

Evidence must protect meaning before interpretation.

### I. No Over-Acceleration

Evidence should help the field notice when pace outruns governance.

## 8. Consent and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before evidence surfaces are used, participants should understand:

* what evidence may be captured;
* why evidence is captured;
* who may see the evidence;
* whether evidence may contribute to synthesis;
* whether evidence may be anonymised;
* whether evidence may be excluded;
* how correction requests work;
* how boundary enforcement works;
* whether AI may assist in organising or summarising evidence.

Consent is part of the Evidence Spine.

Evidence without consent boundaries risks becoming extraction.

Your evidence belongs to you before it belongs to the cohort.
