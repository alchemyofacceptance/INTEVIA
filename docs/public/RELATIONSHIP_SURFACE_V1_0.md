# Relationship Surface v1.0

Status: v1.0 public documentation surface (non-activating)

Purpose: Define the documentation-only role and boundaries of relationship
state within the INTEVIA v1.0 protective spine. This surface supports Human
interpretation and external inspectability without activating runtime behaviour,
workflow logic, routing logic, review logic, audit logic, readiness claims,
consent handling, or operational relationship assignment.

---

## Documentation Boundary

This surface is documentation only. It preserves inspectability and Human
authority without activating runtime behaviour, product, pilot, certification,
deployment, workflow, routing, review, audit, or readiness claims.


## 1. Definition

The Relationship Surface Skeleton is the minimal governed relationship structure required for v1.0 test-case protection.

It provides a bounded way to describe the relationship posture between an Identity Profile and the channels through which future governed interaction may be represented.

This surface does not activate runtime behaviour.

It creates inspectable structure only.

---

## 2. v1.0 Scope

Relationship Surface Skeleton v1.0 exists to support:

- minimal relationship-state vocabulary;
- consent-state visibility;
- non-activating notification-channel posture;
- non-activating CARE-channel posture;
- non-activating evidence-channel posture;
- non-activating queue-channel posture;
- non-activating review-channel posture;
- test-case protection;
- future governed design.

It does not create a relationship management system, communication system, CARE workflow, evidence cadence, queue mechanism, review router, cohort process, or dashboard.

---

## 3. Runtime Boundaries

Relationship Surface Skeleton v1.0 does not implement:

- behaviour;
- automation;
- activation;
- CARE workflows;
- notification systems;
- evidence cadence;
- queue logic;
- review routing;
- cohort logic;
- v1.1 surfaces;
- runtime activation.

Any future activation must be separately designed, reviewed, authorised, and evidenced.

---

## 4. Governance

The Relationship Surface Skeleton is governed by the same authority boundary as the wider INTEVIA v1.0 build:

The Human remains the governor.

AI-assisted structures may help describe, organise, inspect, and prepare relationship-related data, but they do not authorise relationship movement, channel activation, CARE touchpoints, notification, review routing, cohort participation, evidence cadence, or runtime activation.

The skeleton exists to make later governance more inspectable, not to replace governance.

---

## 5. Relationship-State Model

The v1.0 skeleton includes a minimal relationship-state vocabulary:

- `unassigned`
- `observed`
- `active_review`
- `paused`
- `exited`

These states are descriptive only.

They do not activate relationship governance.

They do not trigger workflow, communication, assessment, cohort movement, CARE action, or review routing.

They exist to support test-case protection and future governed design.

---

## 6. Consent-State Model

The v1.0 skeleton includes a minimal consent-state vocabulary:

- `unknown`
- `not_requested`
- `requested`
- `granted`
- `withdrawn`

These states do not define a full consent-management system.

They do not activate communications.

They do not replace future consent instrumentation.

They provide minimal visibility for relationship handling under v1.0 test-case protection.

---

## 7. Channel Model

The v1.0 skeleton includes five non-activating channel postures.

### 7.1 Notification Channel

The notification channel describes possible future communication posture.

It does not send notifications.

It does not create email automation.

It does not trigger cadence or communication workflows.

### 7.2 CARE Channel

The CARE channel reserves a structural place for future CARE-aligned relationship handling.

It does not activate CARE workflows.

It does not create intervention logic.

It does not create support obligations.

### 7.3 Evidence Channel

The evidence channel reserves a structural place for future evidence handling.

It does not create evidence cadence.

It does not ratify evidence.

It does not automate evidence capture.

### 7.4 Queue Channel

The queue channel reserves a structural place for future queue posture.

It does not create queue logic.

It does not determine priority.

It does not activate placement or cohort flow.

### 7.5 Review Channel

The review channel reserves a structural place for future review posture.

It does not create review routing.

It does not automate review.

It does not authorise relationship-state change.
