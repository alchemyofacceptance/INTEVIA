## Status: Draft for mutation

## Scope: Participant-governed interpretation, meaning behaviour, governance reflex, sequencing under load, reversible decisions, stability observations, emergence-safety observations, lineage interpretation, and evidence sovereignty

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Reflection Sheet identity candidate only; not cohort activation, assessment, certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, deployed capability, diagnosis, or surveillance mechanism

---

## 1. Purpose of the Reflection Sheet Surface

Reflection Sheets are a primary participant-governed interpretation surface for Cohort 2.

They may help participants interpret:

* meaning behaviour;
* governance reflex;
* sequencing under load;
* reversible decisions;
* authority movement;
* boundary strain;
* correction moments;
* stability under load where relevant;
* emergence-safety observations where relevant;
* lineage threads;
* unresolved questions.

Reflection Sheets are interpretation surfaces.

They may include introspection, but they must not become forced introspection.

They do not imply performance, capability, ranking, transformation, mastery, certification, admission, or external validation.

Reflection Sheets should help the practitioner understand their own evidence without surrendering meaning.

They should not turn the participant into a subject of extraction.

## 3. Reflection Principles

### A. Meaning Before Interpretation

Interpretation should stabilise meaning, not judge it.

If meaning is unclear, the Reflection Sheet should be allowed to say so.

### B. Governance Before Insight

Governance stabilises the field before insight becomes claim.

A Reflection Sheet may capture insight, but insight does not become doctrine by appearing in a Reflection Sheet.

### C. Sequencing Before Acceleration

Interpretation should respect sequencing discipline.

The Reflection Sheet may help identify whether the practitioner moved too quickly, paused appropriately, restored sequence, or needs follow-up.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Reflection Sheet interpretations should remain correctable and reversible where appropriate.

Not every event can be reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Safety

Emergence, if reflected on, should be interpreted carefully.

Emergence is not proof.

Emergence is not mastery.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Integrity

Reflection Sheets should preserve lineage threads honestly.

Cohort 2 should inherit only what Cohort 1 evidences.

A Reflection Sheet should not pretend that a later interpretation was known at the time unless that lineage is explicitly recorded.

### G. Participant Sovereignty

The participant keeps the meaning.

The Reflection Sheet supports interpretation.

The system does not own the Human.

## 4. Cohort 2 Reflection Sheet Structure v1.0

Each Reflection Sheet may contain seven governed sections.

These sections are candidate structure, not fixed obligation.

### 1. Meaning Interpretation

Interprets:

* meaning stability;
* drift;
* correction;
* clarification;
* resonance where evidence supports it;
* unresolved meaning questions.

Purpose:

> To help the participant interpret how meaning moved without surrendering ownership of that meaning.

### 2. Governance Interpretation

Interprets:

* authority origin;
* authority movement;
* authority correction;
* boundary recognition;
* boundary enforcement;
* refusal, pause, hold, or follow-up;
* governance uncertainty.

Purpose:

> To help the participant understand whether the Human remained able to inspect, correct, refuse, reverse, and govern the work.

### 3. Sequencing Interpretation

Interprets:

* sequencing behaviour under shifting conditions;
* acceleration points;
* pauses;
* corrections;
* skipped steps;
* restored sequence;
* overload signals.

Purpose:

> To help the participant understand whether the work moved in a governable order.

### 4. Reversible Decision Interpretation

Interprets:

* decisions made;
* decisions held;
* decisions reversed;
* decisions hardened;
* evidence supporting the decision;
* what remains reversible;
* what may no longer be reversible.

Purpose:

> To help the participant understand correction paths and avoid premature hardening.

### 5. Stability Under Load Interpretation

Interprets, where relevant:

* emotional-field observations;
* cognitive load;
* stress signals;
* pace strain;
* pause needs;
* overload risk;
* recovery moments.

Purpose:

> To help the participant understand whether the Human field remained stable enough for governance.

This is not diagnosis.

This is not therapy.

This is not emotional scoring.

It is participant-governed interpretation of field conditions where relevant.

### 6. Emergence-Safety Interpretation

Interprets, where relevant:

* emergence signals;
* multi-layer behaviour;
* acceleration patterns;
* boundary checks;
* pause or hold decisions;
* claims avoided;
* lineage protection during complexity.

Purpose:

> To help the participant interpret emergence-related observations without treating emergence as proof, doctrine, or authority.

Emergence-safety reflection should remain especially bounded.

### 7. Lineage Interpretation

Interprets:

* what this evidence connects to;
* what it changes;
* what it does not change;
* what remains unresolved;
* what may require follow-up;
* whether the reflection may contribute to cohort synthesis.

Purpose:

> To preserve how interpretation moves through the learning field.

## 5. Reflection Sheet Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Reflection Sheets do not measure productivity.

### B. No Capability Implication

Reflection Sheets do not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Reflection Sheets do not evaluate the Human.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Reflection Sheets do not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Introspection

Participants should not be pressured to reveal more than the governed purpose requires.

“I am not ready to interpret that yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Reflection Sheets must not become hidden ranking, scoring, certification, or engagement validation.

### G. No Surveillance Posture

Reflection Sheets should remain participant-governed interpretation surfaces, not monitoring tools.

### H. No Drift

Reflection Sheets should protect meaning before interpretation.

### I. No Over-Acceleration

Reflection Sheets should help the field notice when pace outruns governance.

## 7. Consent and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before Reflection Sheets are used in Cohort 2, participants should understand:

* what Reflection Sheets are;
* why Reflection Sheets are used;
* who may see them;
* how they may contribute to synthesis;
* whether they may be anonymised;
* whether they may be excluded;
* how correction requests work;
* how boundary enforcement works;
* whether AI may assist in organising or summarising them.

Consent is part of the Reflection Sheet surface.

Reflection without consent boundaries risks becoming extraction.

---

## 8. AI-Assisted Processing Boundary

AI may assist with Reflection Sheets by:

* organising entries;
* clustering themes;
* identifying repeated friction points;
* supporting lineage traceability;
* preparing draft summaries;
* flagging possible overclaim;
* identifying candidate follow-up questions.

AI may not:

* rank participants;
* score participants;
* certify participants;
* diagnose participants;
* infer hidden meaning;
* determine mastery;
* decide admission;
* independently validate engagement;
* treat emergence as proof;
* replace Human review.

The Human remains the final authority.

---

## 9. Cohort 1 Dependency Boundary

Cohort 2 Reflection Sheets should be informed by Cohort 1 evidence.

Before finalising the Cohort 2 Reflection Sheet structure, governance should ask:

* Which Cohort 1 reflection prompts worked?
* Which prompts created friction?
* Which prompts protected meaning?
* Which prompts risked performance pressure?
* Which prompts helped participants interpret their own practice?
* Which prompts created useful lineage?
* Which prompts should not be scaled?
* What should remain optional?

Cohort 2 Reflection Sheets should not be finalised before the Cohort 1 evidence boundary is understood.

Scale should follow governed evidence.
