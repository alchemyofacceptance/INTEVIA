## 1. Purpose of This Artefact

This artefact defines the assessment identity surfaces for HPCC Levels 1, 2, and 3.

It clarifies what each level is intended to observe, what evidence may support assessment, and what kind of assessment record may be produced.

The purpose is not to judge the Human.

The purpose is to interpret evidence of governed practice.

Assessment in HPCC must remain:

* sovereignty-protecting;
* evidence-bearing;
* meaning-aware;
* non-extractive;
* reversible where appropriate;
* bounded by Human review;
* separate from personal worth;
* separate from certification unless a separate certification process is authorised.

This artefact does not activate certification.

It does not rank participants.

It does not imply mastery.

It does not validate external competence.

It defines surfaces for future governed assessment design.

---

## 2. Assessment Doctrine

HPCC assessment should be understood as **evidence interpretation**, not personal evaluation.

Assessment asks:

> What does the evidence show about the practitioner's governed practice?

It does not ask:

> What is this person worth?

The evidence should support statements about practice, such as:

* meaning was preserved;
* drift was detected;
* sequencing was maintained;
* authority movement was governed;
* reversibility was preserved;
* boundary awareness was demonstrated;
* emergence was handled safely enough to continue.

Assessment outputs should remain descriptive unless and until a separate certification pathway is authorised.

Recognition supports formation.

Certification verifies competence.

These must not be collapsed.

---

## 3. Assessment Evidence Inputs

Across Levels 1-3, possible evidence inputs include:

* Field Notes;

* Meaning Preservation Records;
* Meaning Correction Records;

Evidence inputs must remain governed by consent, privacy, evidence-use boundaries, and Human review.

AI may assist with evidence organisation, clustering, consistency checks, and draft synthesis.

AI may not score, rank, certify, diagnose, or independently validate participants.

# Level 1 Assessment Surface - v1.0

## 1. Level 1 Assessment Purpose

Level 1 assessment observes whether a practitioner can begin operating safely inside governed Human-AI work.

Level 1 focuses on foundational practice:

* preserving meaning in simple interactions;
* detecting drift early;
* maintaining simple sequencing;
* recognising basic authority movement;
* reversing low-complexity decisions;
* identifying foundational boundaries;
* using Field Notes and Reflection Sheets honestly.

Level 1 assessment is not an evaluation of personal worth.

Level 1 assessment is evidence interpretation.

---

## 2. Level 1 Evidence Inputs

Possible Level 1 evidence inputs include:

* Field Notes tagged for Level 1;
* Reflection Sheets tagged for Level 1;
* Meaning Preservation Records;
* Sequencing Discipline Records;
* Reversible Decision Records;
* basic boundary observations;
* participant self-reflection;
* facilitator notes where consent and governance allow.

---

## 3. Level 1 Assessment Criteria

Level 1 assessment may consider evidence of:

### A. Meaning Stability

Can the practitioner preserve their own intended meaning through simple AI-mediated work?

### B. Basic Drift Detection

Can the practitioner notice when output, framing, or interpretation begins to move away from Human intent?

### C. Foundational Sequencing Discipline

Can the practitioner follow a simple governed sequence rather than jumping straight to unbounded output?

### D. Basic Governance Reflex

Can the practitioner pause, inspect, question, correct, or refuse AI output when needed?

### E. Reversible Decisions - Low Complexity

Can the practitioner keep small decisions reversible until sufficient evidence exists?

### F. Foundational Boundary Awareness

Can the practitioner recognise basic boundaries around meaning, authority, evidence, and consent?

---

## 4. Level 1 Assessment Outputs

Possible Level 1 outputs include:

* Level 1 Assessment Record;
* Meaning Stability Statement;
* Drift Detection Statement;
* Sequencing Discipline Statement;
* Basic Governance Reflex Statement;
* Reversible Decision Statement;
* Boundary Awareness Statement.

Level 1 output should not use pass/fail language unless a separate authorised pathway later defines that language.

Default posture:

> No pass/fail. Only evidence.

# Level 2 Assessment Surface - v1.0

## 1. Level 2 Assessment Purpose

Level 2 assessment observes whether a practitioner can govern more complex Human-AI work under cognitive load.

Level 2 focuses on applied governance:

* correcting meaning drift;
* maintaining sequencing under cognitive load;
* governing authority movement;
* interpreting evidence responsibly;
* enforcing boundaries;
* reversing medium-complexity decisions;
* working across more than one surface, artefact, or session.

Level 2 assessment is not performance evaluation.

Level 2 assessment is governance interpretation.

---

## 2. Level 2 Evidence Inputs

Possible Level 2 evidence inputs include:

* Field Notes tagged for Level 2;
* Reflection Sheets tagged for Level 2;
* Meaning Correction Records;
* Authority Movement Records;
* Evidence Interpretation Records;
* Reversible Decision Records;
* boundary enforcement records;
* Evaluation Event records where applicable;
* facilitator observations where consent and governance allow.

---

## 3. Level 2 Assessment Criteria

Level 2 assessment may consider evidence of:

### A. Meaning Correction

Can the practitioner detect and correct meaning drift without surrendering Human intent?

### B. Sequencing Under Cognitive Load

Can the practitioner maintain governed sequence when work becomes faster, denser, or more complex?

### C. Authority Movement Governance

Can the practitioner identify where authority is moving between Human, AI, artefact, evidence, and public surface?

### D. Evidence Interpretation

Can the practitioner interpret evidence without overclaim, collapse, or unsupported inference?

### E. Reversible Decisions - Medium Complexity

Can the practitioner preserve reversibility across decisions that affect multiple artefacts, sessions, or stakeholders?

### F. Boundary Enforcement

Can the practitioner name, hold, and repair boundaries when they are strained?

---

* Sequencing Under Load Statement;
* Authority Movement Statement;
* Evidence Interpretation Statement;

# Level 3 Assessment Surface - v1.0

## 1. Level 3 Assessment Purpose

Level 3 assessment observes whether a practitioner can operate safely inside higher-complexity governed fields.

Level 3 is especially sensitive.

It may include evidence of:

* stability under multi-layer load;
* meaning protection under acceleration;
* cognitive structure formation from Human meaning;
* governance decisions under multi-layer acceleration;
* safe handling of controlled emergence;
* high-complexity reversibility;
* lineage preservation during rapid or dense work.

Level 3 assessment is not certification by itself.

Level 3 assessment is lineage interpretation.

Level 3 certification, if later pursued, must remain separately authorised, Human-led, evidence-bound, and post-readiness.

---

## 2. Level 3 Evidence Inputs

Possible Level 3 evidence inputs include:

* Field Notes tagged for Level 3;
* Reflection Sheets tagged for Level 3;
* Stability Under Load Records;
* Meaning Resonance Records;
* Cognitive-Meaning Coupling Records;
* Governance Sequencing Records;
* Emergence Safety Records;
* Authority Movement Records;
* Reversible Decision Records;
* lineage repair records;
* controlled-emergence case notes;
* facilitator or panel observations where consent and governance allow.

---

## 3. Level 3 Assessment Criteria

Level 3 assessment may consider evidence of:

### A. Stability Under Multi-Layer Load

Can the practitioner remain governed when multiple workstreams, meanings, artefacts, or AI nodes are active?

### B. Meaning Resonance

Can the practitioner preserve and recognise Human meaning as it moves through complex work?

### C. Cognitive-Meaning Coupling

Can the practitioner form useful cognitive structure from Human meaning without collapsing meaning into system output?

### D. Governance Sequencing

Can the practitioner sequence decisions, evidence, correction, and ratification under acceleration?

### E. Emergence Safety

Can the practitioner recognise controlled emergence, maintain boundaries, and avoid treating emergence as proof, authority, or doctrine?

### F. Reversible Decisions - High Complexity

Can the practitioner preserve reversibility where decisions affect lineage, public surface, cohort structure, or organism-level governance?

---

Level 3 output should not claim mastery.

Default posture:

> No mastery. Only evidence.

---

## 5. Cross-Level Progression

The three levels form a progression:

* **Level 1:** preserve meaning and basic governance in simple Human-AI work;
* **Level 2:** correct drift and govern authority movement under load;
* **Level 3:** preserve lineage, reversibility, and emergence safety in high-complexity governed fields.

The levels should not be treated as personal hierarchy.

They are increasing evidence surfaces for governed practice.

A participant is not "better" because they are associated with a higher level.

The work is simply more complex, more sensitive, and more demanding of evidence.
