## 1. What Cohort 1 Is

Cohort 1 is the intended **founding governance cohort** of INTEVIA.

It is not:

* a product trial;
* a marketing campaign;
* a scale programme;
* a generic user study;
* a productivity challenge;
* a certification launch by itself;
* a claim that INTEVIA is complete.

Cohort 1 is also not only a course.

It is being designed as:

> **A bounded soft-launch system test of whether INTEVIA can support a governed learning field.**

That learning field is where Humans begin to practise governed Human-AI work under constitutional conditions, with meaning protection, evidence capture, and lineage preservation.

In plain terms:

> **Cohort 1 is where the Human-side governance discipline begins to meet lived work.**

---

## 2. Why Cohort 1 Exists

Cohort 1 exists because governed Human-AI work cannot mature through theory alone.

It must be practised, observed, corrected, evidenced, and refined.

The constitutional spine defines the public boundary.

HPCC defines the Human-side discipline.

INTEVIA defines the constitutional automation direction.

But the **field** - the governed Human-AI work field - must become visible through careful, bounded practice.

Cohort 1 is intended to become that first evidence-generating learning field.

It is not proof of completion.

It is not external validation.

It is not a universal claim.

It is the beginning of governed observation.

## 3. The Cohort 1 Premise

Cohort 1 begins from three public principles:

1. **Governance is a Human capability.**
2. **Meaning protection is a lived discipline.**
3. **Constitutional automation requires Human sovereignty.**

Cohort 1 is intended to create a bounded environment where these principles can be practised, observed, corrected, and evidenced.

The goal is not to make people dependent on AI.

The goal is to help Humans learn how to govern the work they do with AI.

## 7. Field Notes, Not Field Logs

Cohort 1 uses **Field Notes**.

Not Field Logs.

The term matters.

Field Notes are participant-authored evidence surfaces that support reflective learning, practitioner observation, and non-surveillance governance.

They are intended to help the practitioner see their own practice becoming governable.

Field Notes should support:

* self-observation;
* meaning preservation;
* evidence capture;
* correction;
* reflection;
* practitioner agency;
* cohort synthesis where consent and governance allow.

Field Notes are not surveillance records.

They are not compliance tracking.

They are not hidden scoring.

They are not a mechanism for extracting meaning from participants.

The Human keeps the meaning.

## 12. Recognition, Engagement, and Certification Boundaries

Cohort 1 must keep formation, contribution, recognition, and certification distinct.

Recognition supports formation.

Certification verifies competence.

Recognition may acknowledge contribution without pretending contribution is certification.

Engagement is not activity.

Engagement is evidenced contribution.

Community signal may inform governance, but it must not become social scoring.

Advocacy must remain invited, not coerced.

AI may assist with evidence review, pattern detection, clustering, and consistency checks.

AI may not score, rank, certify, or independently validate engagement.

The Human remains the final ratifier.
