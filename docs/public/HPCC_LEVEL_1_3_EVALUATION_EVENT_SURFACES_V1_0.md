# HPCC Level 1–3 — Evaluation Event Surfaces v1.0

## Status: Draft for mutation

## Purpose: Define HPCC Level 1–3 Evaluation Event identity surfaces

## Scope: Evidence interpretation, meaning stabilisation, governance reflex, sequencing, reversibility, stability under load, emergence-safety restraint, and lineage anchoring

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Evaluation Event identity only; not certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, accreditation, deployed capability, assessment activation, or runtime activation

---

## 1. Purpose of the Evaluation Event Surfaces

Evaluation Events interpret evidence inside the governance field.

They do not interpret the Human as an object.

They may help stabilise:

* meaning;
* governance;
* sequencing;
* reversible decisions;
* authority movement;
* stability under load where relevant;
* emergence-safety observations where relevant;
* lineage threads.

Evaluation Events are evidence-interpretation rituals.

They are not personal-worth assessments.

They do not imply scoring, ranking, capability tiering, mastery, transformation, admission, certification, accreditation, or external validation.

The governing question is:

> What does the evidence allow us to say about governed practice?

Not:

> What is this Human worth?

## 3. Evaluation Principles

### A. Meaning First

Meaning should be stabilised before interpretation.

If meaning is unclear, the Evaluation Event should say so.

No Evaluation Event should overwrite participant meaning.

### B. Governance Before Insight

Governance stabilises the field before insight becomes claim.

An insight may be discussed only within clear evidence, authority, consent, and lineage boundaries.

### C. Sequencing Before Acceleration

Evaluation should respect sequencing discipline.

The event should help identify whether work moved in a governable order before interpreting its significance.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Evaluation interpretations should remain correctable and reversible where appropriate.

Not every event can be reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Safety

Evaluation should not destabilise emergence or treat emergence as proof.

Emergence is not mastery.

Emergence is not doctrine.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Integrity

Evaluation Events should preserve HPCC lineage threads honestly.

They should record what changed, what did not change, what remains unresolved, and what must not be overclaimed.

### G. Human Authority

AI may assist with evidence organisation, clustering, consistency checks, and draft synthesis.

AI may not score, rank, certify, diagnose, determine mastery, infer hidden meaning, or independently validate participants.

The Human remains the final authority.

## 4. HPCC Level Identity

HPCC Levels 1–3 represent interpretation complexity, not Human hierarchy.

### Level 1 — Meaning Stability Surface

Level 1 interpretation may focus on:

* meaning stability;
* meaning drift detection;
* meaning correction;
* basic sequencing;
* low-complexity reversible decisions;
* foundational boundary awareness.

### Level 2 — Governance Stability Surface

Level 2 interpretation may focus on:

* governance reflex;
* authority movement;
* boundary enforcement;
* sequencing under load;
* evidence interpretation;
* medium-complexity reversible decisions.

### Level 3 — Emergence Stability Surface

Level 3 interpretation may focus on:

* stability under multi-layer load;
* meaning resonance where evidenced;
* cognitive–meaning coupling where evidenced;
* emergence safety;
* emergence restraint;
* lineage-sensitive reversibility;
* multi-layer behaviour.

Level 3 is especially sensitive.

Level 3 Evaluation Events do not imply Level 3 certification, readiness, mastery, credentialing, or external validation.

Levels do not imply:

* advancement;
* capability tiering;
* ranking;
* mastery;
* transformation;
* certification;
* Human superiority.

Levels are interpretation surfaces only.

## 5. Evaluation Event Surface Structure v1.0

Each Evaluation Event Surface may contain seven governed sections.

These sections are candidate structure, not fixed obligation.

### 1. Meaning Evaluation Surface — Level 1 Primary

Interprets meaning behaviour, including:

* meaning stability interpretation;
* meaning drift interpretation;
* meaning correction interpretation;
* meaning resonance interpretation where evidence supports it.

Purpose:

> To interpret how meaning behaved without taking meaning away from the participant.

### 2. Governance Evaluation Surface — Level 2 Primary

Interprets governance behaviour, including:

* governance reflex interpretation;
* authority movement interpretation;
* boundary enforcement interpretation;
* governance sequencing interpretation.

Purpose:

> To interpret whether Human authority remained inspectable, correctable, and governable.

### 3. Sequencing Evaluation Surface — Level 1–2 Primary

Interprets sequencing behaviour, including:

* sequencing discipline interpretation;
* sequencing under load interpretation;
* cognitive–meaning coupling interpretation where evidence supports it.

Purpose:

> To interpret whether work moved in a governable order.

### 4. Reversible Decision Evaluation Surface — Levels 1–3

Interprets reversible decision behaviour, including:

* low-complexity reversibility interpretation;
* medium-complexity reversibility interpretation;
* high-complexity reversibility interpretation where appropriate;
* limits of reversibility.

Purpose:

> To preserve correction paths and avoid premature hardening.

### 5. Stability Under Load Evaluation Surface — Level 3 Sensitive

Interprets field stability where relevant, including:

* stability under load;
* pace strain;
* overload signals;
* recovery moments;
* field conditions affecting governance.

Purpose:

> To interpret whether the Human field remained stable enough for governance.

This is not diagnosis.

This is not therapy.

This is not emotional scoring.

### 6. Emergence-Safety Evaluation Surface — Level 3 Sensitive

Interprets emergence-related evidence where relevant, including:

* emergence safety;
* emergence acceleration;
* emergence restraint;
* emergence reversibility where possible;
* claims avoided;
* lineage protection during complexity.

Purpose:

> To interpret emergence-related observations without treating emergence as proof, doctrine, authority, or mastery.

### 7. Lineage Evaluation Surface — Levels 1–3

Interprets lineage behaviour, including:

* lineage anchor interpretation;
* lineage thread interpretation;
* lineage stabilisation interpretation;
* unresolved lineage questions;
* follow-up needs.

Purpose:

> To preserve how evidence moves through the governance field.

## 6. Evaluation Event Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Evaluation Events do not measure productivity.

### B. No Capability Implication

Evaluation Events do not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Evaluation Events do not evaluate the Human.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Evaluation Events do not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Evidence

Participants should not be pressured to manufacture evidence for an Evaluation Event.

“Nothing changed yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Evaluation Events must not become hidden ranking, scoring, certification, or engagement validation.

### G. No Surveillance Posture

Evaluation Events should remain governed interpretation surfaces, not monitoring tools.

### H. No Drift

Evaluation Events should protect meaning before interpretation.

### I. No Over-Acceleration

Evaluation Events should help the field notice when pace outruns governance.

## 8. Consent and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before Evaluation Events are used, participants should understand:

* what evidence may be interpreted;
* why the event is being held;
* who may see the Evaluation Event Record;
* whether the record may contribute to synthesis;
* whether it may be anonymised;
* whether it may be excluded;
* how correction requests work;
* how boundary enforcement works;
* whether AI may assist in organising or summarising evidence.

Consent is part of the Evaluation Event surface.

Evaluation without consent boundaries risks becoming extraction.
