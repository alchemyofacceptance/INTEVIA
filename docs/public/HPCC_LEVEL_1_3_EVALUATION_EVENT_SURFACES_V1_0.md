# HPCC Level 1–3 Evaluation Event Surfaces v1.0

## Status: Draft for mutation

## Purpose: Define Evaluation Event identity surfaces for HPCC Levels 1–3

## Scope: Evidence review, meaning stabilisation, governance interpretation, sequencing, reversibility, boundary awareness, and lineage anchoring

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, evidence-bearing, meaning-stabilising

## Claim status: Evaluation Event identity only; not certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, accreditation, or deployed assessment capability

---

## 1. Purpose of This Artefact

This artefact defines the Evaluation Event identity surfaces for HPCC Levels 1, 2, and 3.

An Evaluation Event is a governed moment where evidence is reviewed, meaning is stabilised, boundaries are checked, and lineage is anchored.

It is not a judgement of the Human.

It is not a ranking mechanism.

It is not certification by itself.

It is not a performance evaluation.

It is not a claim of transformation, admission, mastery, or status.

An Evaluation Event asks:

> What does the evidence allow us to say about governed practice?

It does not ask:

> What is this person worth?

The Human remains sovereign.

The evidence remains bounded.

The interpretation remains reversible where appropriate.

---

## 2. Cross-Level Evaluation Event Doctrine

Across HPCC Levels 1–3, Evaluation Events should remain:

* evidence-led;
* meaning-protective;
* sovereignty-preserving;
* consent-aware;
* non-ranking;
* non-diagnostic;
* non-extractive;
* Human-reviewed;
* lineage-anchored;
* reversible where appropriate.

Evaluation Events may support later recognition, readiness review, or certification design only if a separate pathway is authorised.

Recognition supports formation.

Certification verifies competence.

Evaluation Events must not collapse those functions.

AI may assist with organising evidence, clustering themes, checking consistency, and preparing draft summaries.

AI may not score, rank, certify, diagnose, judge, or independently validate a participant.

# Level 1 Evaluation Event Surface — v1.0

## 1. Level 1 Purpose

A Level 1 Evaluation Event reviews foundational evidence from simple governed Human–AI interactions.

It may help:

* interpret foundational evidence;
* stabilise meaning;
* identify early drift;
* anchor simple sequencing discipline;
* anchor low-complexity reversible decision capability;
* identify foundational boundary awareness;
* protect participant sovereignty.

It does not evaluate the Human.

It does not imply certification.

It does not create pass/fail status.

It interprets evidence.

---

## 2. Level 1 Evidence Inputs

Possible Level 1 evidence inputs include:

* Field Notes tagged for Level 1;
* Reflection Sheets tagged for Level 1;
* Meaning Preservation Records;
* Sequencing Discipline Records;
* Reversible Decision Records;
* basic boundary observations;
* participant clarification or correction requests;
* facilitator observations where consent and governance allow.

Evidence inputs must remain consent-bounded, meaning-protective, and participant-respecting.

---

## 3. Level 1 Event Structure

A Level 1 Evaluation Event may follow this structure:

### A. Opening Meaning Check

Clarify the participant’s intended meaning and current understanding.

### B. Evidence Review

Review selected Field Notes, Reflection Sheets, and relevant evidence records.

### C. Drift Detection Review

Identify whether evidence shows early drift detection or missed drift.

### D. Sequencing Discipline Review

Review whether simple governed sequence was followed.

### E. Reversible Decision Review

Review whether low-complexity decisions remained reversible until enough evidence existed.

### F. Boundary Check

Check meaning, consent, authority, evidence-use, and participant-sovereignty boundaries.

### G. Closing Lineage Anchor

Record what the evidence supports, what it does not support, and what should be followed up.

---

## 4. Level 1 Event Outputs

Possible Level 1 outputs include:

* Level 1 Evaluation Event Record;
* Meaning Stability Interpretation;
* Drift Detection Interpretation;
* Sequencing Discipline Interpretation;
* Reversible Decision Interpretation;
* Boundary Awareness Interpretation;
* Follow-Up Note where needed;
* Closing Lineage Anchor.

Default posture:

> No ranking. Only evidence.

### E. Authority Movement Review

Review where authority moved between Human, AI, artefact, evidence, facilitator, and public surface.

Review whether evidence was interpreted without overclaim, unsupported inference, or collapse.

### G. Reversible Decision Review

Review whether medium-complexity decisions remained reversible across dependencies.

### H. Boundary Check

Check consent, meaning, authority, lineage, evidence-use, and public-surface boundaries.

### I. Closing Lineage Anchor

Record what the evidence supports, what remains unresolved, and what follow-up is required.

# Level 3 Evaluation Event Surface — v1.0

## 1. Level 3 Purpose

A Level 3 Evaluation Event reviews higher-complexity evidence from multi-layer governed fields.

Level 3 is especially sensitive and must remain tightly bounded.

A Level 3 Evaluation Event may help interpret evidence of:

* stability under multi-layer load;
* meaning resonance under acceleration;
* cognitive–meaning coupling;
* governance sequencing under acceleration;
* controlled emergence handling;
* high-complexity reversible decisions;
* boundary integrity under pressure;
* lineage preservation during complex work.

It does not imply mastery.

It does not imply transformation.

It does not imply certification.

It does not activate Level 3 credentialing.

It interprets lineage-sensitive evidence only where evidence permits.

Level 3 certification, if later pursued, must remain separately authorised, Human-led, evidence-bound, and post-readiness.

---

## 2. Level 3 Evidence Inputs

Possible Level 3 evidence inputs include:

* Field Notes tagged for Level 3;
* Reflection Sheets tagged for Level 3;
* Stability Under Load Records;
* Meaning Resonance Records;
* Cognitive–Meaning Coupling Records;
* Governance Sequencing Records;
* Emergence Safety Records;
* Authority Movement Records;
* Reversible Decision Records;
* boundary integrity records;
* lineage repair records;
* controlled-emergence case notes;
* facilitator or panel observations where consent and governance allow.

Level 3 evidence should not be forced.

If the evidence is not present, the event should say so.

Absence of evidence should not become personal judgement.

---

## 3. Level 3 Event Structure

A Level 3 Evaluation Event may follow this structure:

### A. Opening Meaning Check

Clarify the Human meaning being protected and the complexity conditions being reviewed.

### B. Stability Under Load Review

Review whether the field remained stable enough for Human authority, correction, and continued governance.

### C. Meaning Resonance Review

Review whether meaning helped stabilise complex or accelerated work.

### D. Cognitive–Meaning Coupling Review

Review whether useful cognitive structure formed from meaning without collapsing meaning into system output.

### E. Governance Sequencing Review

Review whether decisions, evidence, correction, and ratification were sequenced under acceleration.

### F. Emergence Safety Review

Review whether emergent patterns were recognised without being treated as proof, doctrine, or authority.

### G. Reversible Decision Review

Review whether high-complexity decisions remained reversible across lineage, public surface, cohort, or organism-level implications.

### H. Boundary Check

Check meaning, consent, authority, lineage, emergence, public-surface, evidence-use, and sovereignty boundaries.

### I. Closing Lineage Anchor

Record what the evidence supports, what it does not support, what remains unresolved, and what must not be overclaimed.
