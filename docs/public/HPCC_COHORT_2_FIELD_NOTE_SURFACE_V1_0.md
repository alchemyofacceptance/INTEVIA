# HPCC Cohort 2 — Field Notes Surface v1.0

## Status: Draft for mutation

## Purpose: Define the candidate Field Notes identity for HPCC Cohort 2

## Scope: Participant-authored evidence, meaning behaviour, governance reflex, sequencing under load, reversible decisions, stability observations, emergence-safety observations, lineage threads, and evidence sovereignty

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Field Notes identity candidate only; not cohort activation, assessment, certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, deployed capability, or surveillance mechanism

---

## 1. Purpose of the Field Notes Surface

Field Notes are a primary participant-authored evidence surface for Cohort 2.

They may capture:

* meaning behaviour;
* governance reflex;
* sequencing under load;
* reversible decision practice;
* authority movement;
* boundary strain;
* correction moments;
* stability under load where relevant;
* emergence-safety observations where relevant;
* lineage threads;
* unresolved questions.

Field Notes are evidence, not evaluation.

They do not imply performance, capability, ranking, transformation, mastery, certification, admission, or external validation.

Field Notes should help the practitioner see their own practice becoming governable.

They should not turn the practitioner into a data object.

Field Notes must protect:

* Human sovereignty;

## 3. Field Notes Principles

### A. Meaning First

Meaning should be stabilised before interpretation.

If meaning is unclear, the Field Note should be allowed to say so.

### B. Governance Before Insight

Governance stabilises the field before insight becomes claim.

A Field Note may capture insight, but insight does not become doctrine by appearing in a Field Note.

### C. Sequencing Before Acceleration

Sequencing discipline helps prevent collapse under load.

The Field Note should help identify whether the practitioner moved too quickly, paused appropriately, or preserved sequence.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Field Notes should preserve correction paths and reversible interpretation where appropriate.

Not every event can be reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Safety

Emergence, if observed, should be described carefully.

Emergence is not proof.

Emergence is not mastery.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Integrity

Field Notes should preserve lineage threads honestly.

Cohort 2 should inherit only what Cohort 1 evidences.

A Field Note should not pretend that a later interpretation was known at the time unless that lineage is explicitly recorded.

### G. Participant Sovereignty

The participant keeps the meaning.

The Field Note records evidence.

The system does not own the Human.

## 4. Cohort 2 Field Notes Structure v1.0

Each Field Note entry may contain seven governed sections.

These sections are candidate structure, not fixed obligation.

### 1. Meaning Behaviour Record

Captures:

* meaning stability;
* drift detection;
* meaning correction;
* meaning clarification;
* meaning resonance where evidence supports it;
* unresolved meaning questions.

Purpose:

> To record how meaning behaved without taking meaning away from the participant.

### 2. Governance Reflex Record

Captures:

* authority origin;
* authority movement;
* authority correction;
* boundary recognition;
* boundary enforcement;
* refusal, pause, hold, or follow-up;
* governance uncertainty.

Purpose:

> To record whether the Human remained able to inspect, correct, refuse, reverse, and govern the work.

### 3. Sequencing Under Load Record

Captures:

* sequencing behaviour under shifting conditions;
* points of acceleration;
* pauses;
* corrections;
* skipped steps;
* restored sequence;
* overload signals.

Purpose:

> To record whether the work moved in a governable order.

### 4. Reversible Decision Record

Captures:

* decisions made;
* decisions held;
* decisions reversed;
* decisions hardened;
* evidence supporting the decision;
* what remains reversible;
* what may no longer be reversible.

Purpose:

> To preserve correction paths and avoid premature hardening.

### 5. Stability Under Load Record

Captures, where relevant:

* emotional-field observations;
* cognitive load;
* stress signals;
* pace strain;
* pause needs;
* overload risk;
* recovery moments.

Purpose:

> To record whether the Human field remained stable enough for governance.

This is not diagnosis.

This is not therapy.

This is not emotional scoring.

It is evidence of field conditions where relevant.

### 6. Emergence-Safety Record

Captures, where relevant:

* emergence signals;
* multi-layer behaviour;
* acceleration patterns;
* boundary checks;
* pause or hold decisions;
* claims avoided;
* lineage protection during complexity.

Purpose:

> To record emergence-related observations without treating emergence as proof, doctrine, or authority.

Emergence-safety notes should remain especially bounded.

### 7. Lineage Thread Record

Captures:

* what this evidence connects to;
* what it changes;
* what it does not change;
* what remains unresolved;
* what may require follow-up;
* whether the note may contribute to cohort synthesis.

Purpose:

> To preserve how evidence moves through the learning field.

## 5. Field Notes Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Field Notes do not measure productivity.

### B. No Capability Implication

Field Notes do not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Field Notes do not evaluate the Human.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Field Notes do not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Evidence

Participants should not be pressured to manufacture evidence.

“Nothing changed yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Field Notes must not become hidden ranking, scoring, certification, or engagement validation.

### G. No Surveillance Posture

Field Notes should remain participant-authored evidence surfaces, not monitoring tools.

### H. No Drift

Field Notes should protect meaning before interpretation.

### I. No Over-Acceleration

Field Notes should help the field notice when pace outruns governance.

## 7. Consent and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before Field Notes are used in Cohort 2, participants should understand:

* what Field Notes are;
* why Field Notes are used;
* who may see them;
* how they may contribute to synthesis;
* whether they may be anonymised;
* whether they may be excluded;
* how correction requests work;
* how boundary enforcement works;
* whether AI may assist in organising or summarising them.

Consent is part of the Field Notes surface.

Field Notes without consent boundaries risk becoming extraction.

---

## 8. AI-Assisted Processing Boundary

AI may assist with Field Notes by:

* organising entries;
* clustering themes;
* identifying repeated friction points;
* supporting lineage traceability;
* preparing draft summaries;
* flagging possible overclaim;
* identifying candidate follow-up questions.

AI may not:

* rank participants;
* score participants;
* certify participants;
* diagnose participants;
* infer hidden meaning;
* determine mastery;
* decide admission;
* independently validate engagement;
* treat emergence as proof;
* replace Human review.

The Human remains the final authority.

---

## 9. Cohort 1 Dependency Boundary

Cohort 2 Field Notes should be informed by Cohort 1 evidence.

Before finalising the Cohort 2 Field Notes structure, governance should ask:

* Which Cohort 1 Field Notes prompts worked?
* Which prompts created friction?
* Which prompts protected meaning?
* Which prompts risked performance pressure?
* Which prompts helped participants see their practice becoming governable?
* Which prompts created useful lineage?
* Which prompts should not be scaled?
* What should remain optional?

Cohort 2 Field Notes should not be finalised before the Cohort 1 evidence boundary is understood.

Scale should follow governed evidence.
