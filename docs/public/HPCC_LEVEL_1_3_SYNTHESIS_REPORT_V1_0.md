# HPCC Level 1–3 — Synthesis Report Surface v1.0

## Status: Draft for mutation

## Purpose: Define HPCC Level 1–3 Synthesis Report identity surface

## Scope: Evidence gathering, interpretation, stabilisation, synthesis, correction, lineage anchoring, and sovereignty protection across HPCC Levels 1–3

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Synthesis Report identity only; not a completed synthesis report, assessment activation, certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, accreditation, deployed capability, or runtime activation

---

## 1. Purpose of the Synthesis Report Surface

The Synthesis Report Surface defines how HPCC may gather, interpret, stabilise, and bind evidence into lineage across Levels 1–3.

It may help HPCC:

* gather evidence;
* interpret evidence;
* stabilise evidence;
* synthesise evidence;
* preserve evidence as lineage;
* identify correction needs;
* identify supersession needs;
* identify what evidence does not yet support;
* prepare future cohort or curriculum refinement.

The Synthesis Report is not an assessment, personal evaluation, ranking, scoring, or certification artefact by itself.

It does not imply capability, mastery, transformation, admission, accreditation, or external validation.

The Synthesis Report Surface is a lineage-anchoring structure for HPCC evidence.

It asks:

> What does the evidence allow us to say across the field?

It does not ask:

> What are these Humans worth?

## 3. Synthesis Principles

### A. Meaning First

Synthesis must protect meaning before interpretation.

If meaning is unclear, the report should say so.

No synthesis statement should overwrite participant meaning.

### B. Governance Before Insight

Synthesis must remain governed before insight becomes claim.

A pattern is not a conclusion until evidence, consent, authority, and lineage boundaries are clear.

### C. Sequencing Before Acceleration

Synthesis should respect sequencing discipline.

The report should preserve what happened, in what order, under what authority, and with what correction path.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Synthesis interpretations should remain correctable and reversible where appropriate.

Not every event can be reversed.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Safety

Synthesis should not destabilise emergence or treat emergence as proof.

Emergence is not mastery.

Emergence is not doctrine.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Integrity

Synthesis must preserve HPCC lineage threads honestly.

The report should show:

* what evidence was created;
* what evidence was interpreted;
* what evidence was stabilised;
* what changed;
* what did not change;
* what remains unresolved;
* what must not be overclaimed.

### G. Correction and Supersession

Synthesis must allow correction and supersession.

If evidence is incomplete, incorrect, ambiguous, superseded, or later clarified, the report should support repair rather than pretending finality.

Correction protects lineage.

Supersession protects truthfulness.

## 4. HPCC Level Identity

HPCC Levels 1–3 represent synthesis complexity, not Human hierarchy.

### Level 1 — Meaning Stability Synthesis

Level 1 synthesis may include:

* meaning stability evidence;
* meaning drift evidence;
* meaning correction evidence;
* basic sequencing evidence;
* low-complexity reversible decision evidence;
* foundational boundary evidence.

### Level 2 — Governance Stability Synthesis

Level 2 synthesis may include:

* governance reflex evidence;
* authority movement evidence;
* boundary enforcement evidence;
* governance sequencing evidence;
* evidence interpretation patterns;
* medium-complexity reversible decision evidence.

### Level 3 — Emergence Stability Synthesis

Level 3 synthesis may include:

* stability under multi-layer load where evidenced;
* meaning resonance where evidenced;
* cognitive–meaning coupling where evidenced;
* emergence safety evidence;
* emergence restraint evidence;
* emergence reversibility where possible;
* multi-layer behaviour evidence;
* lineage-sensitive reversibility.

Level 3 is especially sensitive.

Level 3 synthesis does not imply Level 3 certification, readiness, mastery, credentialing, or external validation.

Levels do not imply:

* advancement;
* capability tiering;
* ranking;
* mastery;
* transformation;
* certification;
* Human superiority.

Levels are synthesis surfaces only.

## 5. Synthesis Report Structure v1.0

The Synthesis Report Surface may contain three governed layers.

These layers are candidate structure, not fixed obligation.

---

### Layer 1 — Evidence Layer

The Evidence Layer synthesises source evidence from:

* Field Notes;
* Reflection Sheets;
* Evaluation Events;
* Assessment Identity Records where authorised;
* Meaning Preservation Records;
* Meaning Correction Records;
* Governance Sequencing Records;
* Authority Movement Records;
* Reversible Decision Records;
* Stability Under Load Records where relevant;
* Emergence Safety Records where relevant;
* Lineage Anchors.

It may include:

* meaning evidence;
* governance evidence;
* sequencing evidence;
* reversible decision evidence;
* authority movement evidence;
* stability under load evidence;
* emergence safety evidence;
* lineage evidence.

The Evidence Layer should distinguish:

* evidence observed;
* evidence not observed;
* evidence insufficient to interpret;
* evidence requiring follow-up;
* evidence suitable for anonymised synthesis;
* evidence not suitable for inclusion;
* evidence requiring correction or boundary enforcement.

---

### Layer 2 — Interpretation Layer

The Interpretation Layer synthesises interpretation across Levels 1–3.

It may include:

* meaning interpretation synthesis;
* governance interpretation synthesis;
* sequencing interpretation synthesis;
* reversible decision interpretation synthesis;
* authority movement interpretation synthesis;
* stability under load interpretation synthesis where relevant;
* emergence safety interpretation synthesis where relevant;
* lineage interpretation synthesis.

Interpretation should not exceed evidence.

The report should distinguish:

* supported interpretation;
* tentative interpretation;
* unresolved interpretation;
* interpretation requiring follow-up;
* interpretation requiring correction.

---

### Layer 3 — Lineage Layer

The Lineage Layer synthesises evidence and interpretation into governed lineage.

It may include:

* lineage anchor synthesis;
* lineage thread synthesis;
* lineage stabilisation synthesis;
* lineage correction synthesis;
* lineage supersession synthesis;
* lineage coherence synthesis;
* lineage preservation synthesis;
* future refinement recommendations.

The Lineage Layer does not create constitutional identity by itself.

It proposes what the evidence may allow governance to preserve, refine, correct, or supersede.

Human review remains required.

## 6. Synthesis Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Synthesis does not measure productivity.

### B. No Capability Implication

Synthesis does not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Synthesis does not evaluate participants as Humans.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Synthesis does not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Evidence

Participants should not be pressured to manufacture evidence for synthesis.

“Nothing changed yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Synthesis must not become hidden ranking, scoring, certification, or engagement validation.

### G. No Surveillance Posture

Synthesis should remain a governed evidence surface, not a monitoring tool.

### H. No Drift

Synthesis must protect meaning before interpretation.

### I. No Over-Acceleration

Synthesis should help the field notice when pace outruns governance.

## 8. Consent, Anonymisation, and Evidence-Use Boundary

Before synthesis occurs, participants should understand:

* what evidence may be included;
* why synthesis is being prepared;
* who may see the synthesis;
* whether evidence may be anonymised;
* whether evidence may be excluded;
* how correction requests work;
* how boundary enforcement works;
* whether AI may assist in organising or summarising evidence.

Consent is part of the Synthesis Report Surface.

Synthesis without consent boundaries risks becoming extraction.

Your evidence belongs to you before it belongs to the cohort.

## 10. Publication Boundary

The Synthesis Report should not be published as a completed report until:

* the relevant cohort or evidence body has actually run;
* evidence has been collected under consent;
* evidence-use boundaries have been reviewed;
* participant privacy has been protected;
* anonymisation has been applied where required;
* synthesis statements have been checked against evidence;
* overclaim has been removed;
* Human review has occurred;
* publication has been explicitly authorised.

Until then, this artefact is a Synthesis Report Surface / Structure.

It is not a completed Synthesis Report.
