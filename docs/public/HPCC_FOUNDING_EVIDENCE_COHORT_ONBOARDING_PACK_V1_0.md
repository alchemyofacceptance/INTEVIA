## 1. Purpose of the Onboarding Pack

This onboarding pack helps prospective and future participants understand the HPCC Founding Evidence Cohort.

It explains:

* what the cohort is;
* what the cohort is not;
* what participants may receive;
* what participants may contribute;
* how evidence is handled;
* how sovereignty is protected;
* how Field Notes and Reflection Sheets work;
* how Founder / Creator proximity should be understood;
* how the cohort supports the emerging HPCC evidence base.

This pack does not open applications.

It does not confirm participation.

It does not activate certification.

It does not claim external validation.

It provides a governed orientation surface for future cohort use.

---

## 2. Participant-Facing Welcome

Welcome to the HPCC Founding Evidence Cohort.

You are entering the orientation surface for a governed learning field designed to help Humans see their own practice more clearly, more safely, and more meaningfully inside AI-mediated work.

This cohort is not only a course.

It is a bounded formation field where lived practice may become evidence, and where evidence may help shape the future of Human-AI governance.

The purpose is not to extract your meaning.

The purpose is to help you observe your own practice becoming governable.

---

## 3. What This Cohort Is

The HPCC Founding Evidence Cohort is intended to be:

* a governed learning field;
* a reciprocal formation environment;
* a place to practise meaning protection;
* a place to develop governance reflex;
* a place to observe Human-AI work under constitutional boundaries;
* a place where participant-authored evidence may contribute to the emerging HPCC evidence base.

In plain terms:

> **The cohort helps Humans practise governed Human-AI work and make that practice inspectable without surrendering meaning.**

---

## 4. What This Cohort Is Not

The HPCC Founding Evidence Cohort is not:

* a product trial;
* a performance evaluation;
* a test of personal worth;
* a promise of transformation;
* a certification award by itself;
* a scarcity or prestige mechanism;
* a place where Founder proximity implies access, status, or special authority;
* a place where participant meaning becomes system property;
* a place where AI judges, scores, ranks, diagnoses, or certifies participants.

It is a governed learning field.

The Human remains sovereign.

---

## 5. Why This Is Called the Founding Evidence Cohort

This cohort is called **Founding** because it is intended to establish the first HPCC evidence base and set the tone for future cohort governance.

It is called **Evidence** because the cohort is built around participant-authored practice evidence, not abstract claims.

The Founding Evidence Cohort is intended to help:

* discover the HPCC value proposition through lived practitioner experience;
* anchor the first lineage threads;
* test whether INTEVIA can support a governed learning field;
* refine Field Notes, Reflection Sheets, and evidence surfaces;
* clarify recognition, engagement, and certification boundaries;
* preserve the constitutional tone for future cohorts.

This is not proof of completion.

It is the beginning of governed evidence formation.

## 9. Evidence Sovereignty Principle

Your evidence belongs to you before it belongs to the cohort.

This principle matters.

You should understand:

* what you submit;
* why you submit it;
* how it may be used;
* whether it may be anonymised;
* whether it may contribute to cohort synthesis;
* whether it may enter INTEVIA lineage;
* what correction, removal, or boundary enforcement paths exist.

Participant evidence must not be treated as automatic platform property.

Evidence sovereignty protects the Human before it serves the cohort.

## 11. AI-Assisted Processing Boundary Summary

AI assistance may support governed evidence work.

It may assist with:

* pattern detection;
* clustering;
* consistency checks;
* summarisation;
* lineage support;
* evidence organisation;
* draft synthesis for Human review.

AI may not:

* evaluate participants;
* judge personal worth;
* score, rank, or certify;
* diagnose;
* infer hidden meaning;
* override Human judgement;
* independently validate engagement;
* decide whether a participant is competent;
* treat participant evidence as permission for extraction.

AI assistance must remain bounded, governed, reversible, meaning-protective, and lineage-aware.

The Human remains the final authority.

## 14. How to Use the Baseline Survey

The Baseline Survey captures the participant's starting point.

It may help identify:

* initial meaning state;
* current AI-use habits;
* governance reflex;
* sequencing discipline;
* evidence awareness;
* reversible decision capability;
* comfort with reflection and correction;
* perceived risks, hopes, and boundaries.

The Baseline Survey is not a judgement of the participant.

It is a starting map.

It helps the participant and the field understand where the practice begins.

---

## 15. How to Use Field Notes

Field Notes help participants observe practice as it happens.

They may capture:

* what was attempted;
* what AI assistance was used;
* where Human authority moved;
* what evidence appeared;
* what meaning needed protection;
* where uncertainty arose;
* what correction was needed;
* what became clearer;
* what remained unresolved.

Field Notes are not surveillance records.

They are participant-authored evidence surfaces.

They should help the practitioner see their own practice becoming governable.

---

## 16. How to Use the Reflection Sheets

Reflection Sheets help participants interpret Field Notes after practice.

They may support reflection on:

* meaning movement;
* governance reflex;
* authority movement;
* evidence quality;
* lineage;
* correction;
* confidence;
* uncertainty;
* reversible decisions;
* learning that became visible.

Reflection Sheets are not confessions.

They are governed learning surfaces.

They help the participant understand what changed, what did not change, and what may need further observation.

## 17. What Happens at the End of the Cohort

At the end of the cohort, evidence may be reviewed under governance.

This may include:

* reviewing Field Notes;
* reviewing Reflection Sheets;
* mapping lineage;
* identifying governance reflex development;
* examining meaning protection patterns;
* understanding reversible decision capability;
* identifying cohort-level patterns;
* preparing synthesis where consent and governance allow.

The end of the cohort is not a final verdict on the participant.

It is a governed synthesis point.

The evidence helps the field understand what became visible.
