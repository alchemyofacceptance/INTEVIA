# HPCC Cohort 2 — Cadence Surface v1.0

## Status: Draft for mutation

## Purpose: Define the candidate cadence identity for HPCC Cohort 2

## Scope: Governed rhythm, meaning protection, Field Notes, Reflection Sheets, Evaluation Events, sequencing under load, reversible governance, emergence-safety preparation, and lineage anchoring

## Tone: Non-evaluative, sovereignty-protecting, lineage-aware, evidence-bearing

## Claim status: Cadence candidate only; not cohort activation, certification, ranking, transformation, admission, mastery, external validation, deployed capability, or fixed schedule

---

## 1. Purpose of the Cadence Surface

The Cadence Surface defines how Cohort 2 may move.

It does not define how Cohort 2 performs.

Cohort 2 cadence is intended to support:

* meaning stabilisation;
* sovereignty protection;
* governance reflex;
* sequencing under load;
* reversible decision practice;
* controlled emergence-safety preparation;
* lineage thread strengthening;
* evidence capture without performance pressure;
* Human-governed refinement after Cohort 1.

Cadence is governance-first, not performance-first.

The purpose of cadence is not to make the field faster.

The purpose of cadence is to keep the field governable.

Cohort 2 cadence does not imply:

* advancement;

Cadence is a governed rhythm.

It is not a performance schedule.

It is not a productivity target.

It is not an obligation to produce evidence on demand.

---

## 3. Cadence Principles

### A. Governance First

Governance stabilises the field before any insight is allowed to become a claim.

### B. Meaning Before Movement

Meaning should be sufficiently clear before cadence accelerates.

If meaning is unstable, the field should pause, clarify, or slow down.

### C. Sequencing Before Acceleration

Sequencing discipline helps prevent collapse under load.

The field should not speed up merely because output is available.

### D. Reversibility Where Appropriate

Cadence decisions, interpretations, evidence-use choices, and public-facing conclusions should remain reversible where appropriate.

Where reversibility is limited, the boundary should be named.

### E. Emergence Safety

Emergence should remain controlled, bounded, Human-governed, and reversible where possible.

Emergence is not proof.

Emergence is not authority.

Emergence is a condition requiring governance.

### F. Lineage Integrity

Cadence must preserve Cohort 1 lineage threads honestly.

Cohort 2 should inherit only what Cohort 1 evidences.

### G. Humane Sustainability

Cadence must respect Human attention, rest, cognitive load, and participant dignity.

A rhythm that overwhelms the Human is not governed.

## 4. Cohort 2 Candidate Cadence Structure v1.0

Cohort 2 cadence may consist of seven governed rhythms.

These rhythms are candidate structures, not fixed obligations.

### 1. Weekly Field Notes — Governance-First Rhythm

Purpose:

* capture meaning behaviour;
* capture governance reflex;
* observe sequencing under load;
* record reversible decisions;
* record authority movement;
* identify boundary strain;
* preserve lineage threads.

Field Notes are evidence surfaces.

They are not surveillance.

They are not performance records.

They should help the practitioner see their own practice becoming governable.

### 2. Weekly Reflection Sheets — Interpretation-First Rhythm

Purpose:

* interpret meaning movement;
* interpret governance reflex;
* interpret sequencing;
* interpret reversibility;
* identify uncertainty;
* name correction needs;
* preserve participant-owned interpretation.

Reflection Sheets are governed learning surfaces.

They are not confessions.

They are not forced introspection.

### 3. Bi-Weekly Evaluation Events — Evidence Interpretation Rhythm

Purpose:

* interpret evidence inside the governance field;
* stabilise meaning;
* review boundary conditions;
* check authority movement;
* review reversible decisions;
* anchor lineage;
* identify follow-up needs.

Evaluation Events do not judge the Human.

They interpret evidence.

### 4. Monthly Meaning Preservation Exercises — Stability Rhythm

Purpose:

* stabilise meaning;
* identify drift;
* practise meaning correction;
* protect sovereignty;
* reduce misinterpretation;
* strengthen participant control over meaning.

Meaning preservation should not become meaning control.

The participant keeps the meaning.

### 5. Monthly Governance Sequencing Exercises — Governance Rhythm

Purpose:

* strengthen governance reflex;
* practise authority movement awareness;
* practise boundary enforcement;
* sequence evidence before claim;
* sequence correction before publication;
* maintain Human authority under load.

Governance sequencing protects the field from acceleration without accountability.

### 6. Periodic Emergence Safety Exercises — Acceleration-Restraint Rhythm

Purpose:

* practise stability under load;
* identify signs of uncontrolled acceleration;
* preserve Human review during multi-layer work;
* avoid treating emergence as proof;
* practise pause, hold, follow-up, or STOP where needed;
* preserve lineage during complex work.

Frequency should be evidence-led.

Quarterly may be suitable for mature or advanced contexts.

Earlier or lighter-touch review may be safer during initial formation.

Emergence-safety work should not imply Level 3 certification, mastery, or readiness.

### 7. Continuous Reversible Governance — Constitutional Reflex

Purpose:

* keep decisions reversible where possible;
* preserve Human review;
* identify hardening points;
* hold claims until evidence supports them;
* maintain correction paths;
* protect meaning and consent.

Reversible governance is not an extra activity.

It is the reflex that keeps the cadence safe.

## 5. Cadence Adjustment Rules

Cohort 2 cadence should remain adjustable.

### A. Slow Down When Meaning Becomes Unstable

If meaning drifts, collapses, or becomes unclear, cadence should slow down.

### B. Pause When Sovereignty Is Threatened

If participant sovereignty, consent, or evidence-use boundaries are unclear, the affected process should pause.

### C. Add Structure When Evidence Is Too Weak

If evidence is too thin to interpret, the field may add prompts, exercises, or review points.

### D. Remove Structure When Process Becomes Performative

If the cadence creates performance pressure, the field should simplify.

### E. Escalate When Boundaries Are Crossed

If governance, meaning, consent, or lineage boundaries are crossed, escalation should occur before continuation.

### F. Review Cadence Against Evidence

Cadence should be refined through evidence, not preference, ambition, or pressure.

---

## 6. Cadence Safety Rules

### A. No Performance Pressure

Cadence is not a productivity schedule.

### B. No Capability Implication

Cadence does not imply skill level, advancement, mastery, or status.

### C. No Personal-Worth Evaluation

Cadence does not evaluate the Human.

### D. No Transformation Claim

Cadence does not transform the Human by claim or expectation.

### E. No Forced Evidence

Participants should not be pressured to manufacture evidence.

“Nothing changed yet” may be valid evidence.

### F. No Hidden Scoring

Cadence outputs must not become hidden participant scoring.

### G. No Drift

Cadence must protect meaning before movement.

### H. No Over-Acceleration

Cadence must not create more work than the Human field can govern.

* identifying repeated friction points;
* preparing draft summaries;
* supporting lineage traceability;
* flagging possible overclaim;
* identifying candidate cadence refinements.

AI may not:

* rank participants;
* score participants;
* certify participants;
* diagnose participants;
* infer hidden meaning;
* determine mastery;
* decide admission;
* independently validate engagement;
* treat emergence as proof;
* replace Human review.

The Human remains the final authority.

## 9. Cohort 1 Dependency Boundary

Cohort 2 cadence should be informed by Cohort 1 evidence.

Before finalising cadence, governance should ask:

* What cadence did Cohort 1 actually sustain?
* Where did Cohort 1 create useful evidence?
* Where did Cohort 1 create overload?
* Which Field Notes prompts worked?
* Which Reflection Sheet prompts worked?
* Which Evaluation Event rhythm was useful?
* Where did cadence become performative?
* Where did cadence protect meaning?
* Where did cadence need more reversibility?
* What should not be scaled?

Cohort 2 cadence should not be finalised before the Cohort 1 evidence boundary is understood.

Scale should follow governed evidence.
