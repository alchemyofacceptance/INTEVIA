## 1. Purpose of the Runbook

This runbook defines how the HPCC Founding Evidence Cohort is prepared, opened, facilitated, evidenced, closed, and synthesised.

It exists to protect:

* Human sovereignty;
* meaning protection;
* evidence integrity;
* participant dignity;
* reversible governance;
* lineage formation;
* constitutional boundaries;
* facilitator discipline;
* cohort synthesis quality.

This runbook is facilitator-side and governance-grade.

It does not replace the Consent & Evidence Use Form.

It does not certify participants.

It does not activate Cohort 1 by itself.

It defines how Cohort 1 should be operated if and when authorised.

---

## 2. Operating Principles

Cohort 1 is governed by the following operating principles:

* sovereignty first;
* meaning protection always;
* governance before interpretation;
* evidence over performance;
* reversibility before commitment;
* consent before use;
* lineage before claim;
* lean execution;
* correction without collapse;
* Founder boundary protection.

The cohort is not designed to make participants perform certainty.

It is designed to help Humans practise governed Human-AI work and make that practice inspectable without surrendering meaning.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Founder / Creator

The Founder / Creator:

* holds constitutional boundaries;
* protects meaning discipline;
* oversees lineage integrity;
* protects Founder boundary;
* reviews critical governance escalations;
* ratifies structural decisions where required;
* prevents public-surface overclaim.

Founder involvement is structural, constitutional, and governance-related.

It is not access, prestige, scarcity, status, or personal authority over participant meaning.

### 4.2 Educator / Facilitator / Organism Holder

The educator, facilitator, or authorised organism holder:

* facilitates cohort sessions;
* protects participant sovereignty;
* supports use of Field Notes and Reflection Sheets;
* forms or supports Evaluation Events where authorised;
* reviews submitted evidence under consent boundaries;
* identifies drift, ambiguity, and follow-up needs;
* supports meaning protection;
* preserves lineage;
* escalates boundary concerns.

The educator may review, acknowledge, organise, and prepare participant evidence for governed synthesis.

The educator does not own participant meaning.

The educator does not certify participants unless a separate certification process is authorised.

### 4.3 Participant

The participant:

* engages voluntarily with the governed learning field;
* completes baseline and reflection materials where applicable;
* submits Field Notes where applicable;
* practises meaning protection;
* observes authority movement;
* practises reversible governance;
* names uncertainty;
* requests correction or boundary enforcement when needed;
* retains sovereignty over meaning and evidence.

The participant is not being judged for personal worth.

The participant is practising governed Human-AI work.

## 6. Participant Selection / Acceptance Process

Participation should be voluntary, bounded, and sovereignty-aware.

The acceptance process should protect:

* informed participation;
* consent readiness;
* basic fit for a governed learning field;
* willingness to reflect;
* willingness to work with evidence;
* ability to respect boundaries;
* ability to accept correction without collapse;
* safety and privacy considerations where relevant.

This is not a performance selection process.

It is not a worth assessment.

It is not a prestige gate.

It is appropriate to assess readiness, fit, consent, and boundary understanding.

It is not appropriate to screen for personal status, social value, Founder proximity, or pre-existing ideological alignment.

Application is not admission.

Application is an expression of intent.

## 7. Consent Gate

Before participation, each participant should acknowledge:

* participation is voluntary;
* the cohort is a governed formation field;
* the cohort is not certification by itself;
* participant evidence belongs to the participant before it belongs to the cohort;
* evidence use is governed by consent;
* AI may assist with evidence handling but does not score, rank, certify, diagnose, or judge participants;
* correction, removal, clarification, and boundary enforcement paths exist;
* meaning remains Human.

The consent gate protects the participant before it serves the cohort.

## 10. Cohort Rhythm and Cadence

A minimal viable Cohort 1 rhythm may include:

* weekly governed session;
* weekly Field Notes;
* weekly Reflection Sheets;
* bi-weekly Evaluation Events where appropriate;
* continuous meaning protection;

Cohort 1 should not overbuild process before evidence shows what the field needs.

The aim is signal, not noise.

## 11. Module / Session Operating Pattern

A typical cohort session may follow this pattern:

1. opening meaning check;
2. consent and boundary reminder where needed;
3. review of selected Field Notes;
4. review of selected Reflection Sheet themes;
5. meaning protection exercise;
6. governance reflex exercise;
7. sequencing discipline exercise;
8. authority movement check;
9. reversible decision check;
10. follow-up actions;
11. closing lineage anchor.

The session should not turn into performance evaluation.

The facilitator's role is to help the field remain governable.

* where Human authority moved;
* where meaning needed protection;
* where uncertainty arose;
* what evidence appeared;
* what correction was needed;
* what became clearer;
* what remained unresolved.

The facilitator may review Field Notes to identify:

* meaning themes;
* governance reflex patterns;
* sequencing patterns;
* authority movement;
* evidence quality;
* possible drift;
* follow-up needs;
* lineage threads.

Field Notes are not surveillance records.

They are participant-authored evidence surfaces.

They should help the practitioner see their own practice becoming governable.

Peer or educator review should protect:

## 17. Issue / Concern Escalation Procedure

Concerns should be escalated according to the boundary involved.

### Meaning Threat

If participant meaning is being distorted, extracted, overwritten, or misread:

* pause interpretation;
* clarify participant meaning;
* correct the record;
* anchor the correction in lineage;
* confirm boundary restoration.

### Sovereignty Threat

If participant sovereignty is threatened:

* stop the affected process;
* clarify consent;
* restore Human authority;
* correct evidence-use boundaries;
* re-establish participation conditions.

### Governance Threat

If authority movement, lineage, evidence, or reversibility is compromised:

* identify the governance break;
* pause affected decisions;
* repair authority chain;
* create or update reversible decision record;
* record lineage correction.

### Public-Surface Threat

If an output risks public overclaim or misrepresentation:

* hold publication;
* review claim status;
* correct language;
* confirm evidence boundary;
* publish only after Human review.
