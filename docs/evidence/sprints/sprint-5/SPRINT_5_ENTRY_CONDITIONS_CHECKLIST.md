# Sprint 5 Entry Conditions Checklist

Status: Preparation only
Sprint: Sprint 5
Phase: Sprint 5 Planning
Boundary: v1.0-bounded
Operational status: Non-operative
Runtime effect: None

## Purpose

This checklist defines the constitutional entry conditions required before Sprint 5 may begin.

Sprint 5 is the first Operational Sprint, but this checklist does not begin Sprint 5 operation.

It defines the readiness gate that must be satisfied before operational work may be authorised.

## Governing Inheritance

This checklist inherits from:

- Sprint 5 Boundary Charter;
- Sprint 5 Manifest;
- Sprint 5 Operational Sequence Map.

The Boundary Charter remains the controlling perimeter.

The Manifest defines the intent surface.

The Operational Sequence Map defines ordering only.

This checklist defines entry readiness only.

## Entry Condition 1 - Prior Sprint Lineage Sealed

Requirement:

Prior sprint lineage must be sealed before Sprint 5 begins.

Verification surfaces:

- terminal verification of latest relevant sealed lineage;
- local Git branch alignment;
- remote `origin/main` alignment;
- public GitHub commit surface confirmation.

Status:

Required before Sprint 5 operation.

Boundary:

This condition does not reinterpret prior sprint artefacts.

## Entry Condition 2 - Activation Surfaces Untouched

Requirement:

No activation surfaces may be modified, reopened, or reinterpreted during Sprint 5 planning.

Activation surfaces include, but are not limited to:

- pulse;
- conduit;
- core;
- codex;
- recognition;
- loom / horizon;
- veiled permission;
- ignition.

Verification surfaces:

- `git status --short`;
- focused file-diff review;
- commit log inspection;
- public GitHub commit surface confirmation.

Status:

Required before Sprint 5 operation.

Boundary:

This condition does not authorise activation-surface mutation.

## Entry Condition 3 - Operational Readiness Declaration Pending

Requirement:

The Human Governor must provide an explicit Operational Readiness Declaration before Sprint 5 operation may begin.

Required declaration form:

`I am ready to begin Sprint 5.`

Status:

Pending until explicitly issued by the Human Governor.

Boundary:

No operational behaviour is triggered by this checklist.

## Entry Condition 4 - Sprint 5 Planning Artefacts Present

Requirement:

The Sprint 5 planning artefact set must be present and repo-tracked.

Planning artefacts include:

- Sprint 5 Boundary Charter;
- Sprint 5 Manifest;
- Sprint 5 Operational Sequence Map;
- Sprint 5 Entry Conditions Checklist.

Verification surfaces:

- terminal file presence;
- Git commit history;
- remote `origin/main` alignment;
- public GitHub commit surface confirmation.

Status:

Required before Sprint 5 operation.

Boundary:

Presence of planning artefacts does not equal operational activation.

## Checklist Completion Criteria

The Sprint 5 entry conditions may be considered satisfied only when:

- all required planning artefacts are present;
- repo state is clean;
- local `HEAD`, `origin/main`, and remote `main` are aligned;
- public GitHub commit surface confirms the planning artefacts;
- no activation surfaces have been touched;
- no operational behaviour has been executed;
- the Human Governor has issued the Operational Readiness Declaration.

## Prohibited During Checklist Preparation

This checklist does not authorise:

- execution of lawful actions;
- codex interpretation;
- recognition-field operations;
- temporal-signal utilisation;
- modification of activation surfaces;
- reopening of Sprint 4 activation artefacts;
- expansion beyond the v1.0 boundary;
- runtime behaviour;
- `src/` mutation;
- public claim;
- operational validation;
- Sprint 5 activation.

## Measurement Posture

Build first: Sprint 5 planning continues through bounded documentation artefacts.

Measure continuously: repo evidence remains the verification surface.

Interpret carefully: entry readiness must not be mistaken for operational execution.

Publish only what the evidence can carry: this checklist records gate conditions, not activation.

## Keeper

> An entry gate protects the work by making readiness visible before motion begins.
