# Sprint 3 Candidate Expansion Surfaces

Status: Candidate surface definition
Sprint: Sprint 3
Scope: Documentation-only candidate expansion perimeter
Lineage: Follows Sprint 3 Boundary Charter, Sprint 3 Manifest, and sealed Sprint 1 and Sprint 2 evidence spines
Runtime authority: None
v1.0 boundary: Preserved

## Purpose

Define the bounded set of documentation-only surfaces that Sprint 3 may prepare within its constitutional perimeter.

These candidate surfaces do not authorise implementation, runtime behaviour, source-code mutation, routing, parsing, discovery, dynamic registration, telemetry, automation, or behavioural activation.

Sprint 3 inherits governance, not motion.

Sprint 3 does not reopen Sprint 1.

Sprint 3 does not reopen Sprint 2.

## Candidate Surface 1 — Documentation Orientation Surfaces

Sprint 3 may prepare documentation-only orientation surfaces.

These surfaces may clarify how Sprint 3 understands its own boundary, intent, candidate surfaces, and evidence spine.

They must remain:

- static;
- human-readable;
- documentation-only;
- non-executable;
- non-behavioural;
- v1.0-bounded;
- subject to later Human-selected Work Block authorisation.

They must not create routing, parsing, discovery, dynamic registration, runtime behaviour, or source-code mutation.

## Candidate Surface 2 — Lineage-Preserving Surfaces

Sprint 3 may prepare documentation-only lineage-preserving surfaces.

These surfaces may describe how sealed Sprint 1 and Sprint 2 lineage is referenced without being reopened, modified, or reinterpreted.

They must preserve sealed history.

They must not mutate sealed artefacts.

They must not convert sealed lineage into an active mutation surface.

## Candidate Surface 3 — Governance Continuity Surfaces

Sprint 3 may prepare documentation-only governance continuity surfaces.

These surfaces may record or clarify governance instrumentation inherited from Sprint 1 and Sprint 2, including Terminal Echo discipline, commit and push separation, VC review, and GitHub visual confirmation.

They must describe governance.

They must not automate governance.

They must not create behavioural enforcement.

## Candidate Surface 4 — Evidence Spine Surfaces

Sprint 3 may prepare a Sprint 3 Evidence Log and related documentation-only evidence surfaces.

These surfaces may record Work Block movement, pre-flight verification, mutation scope, commit selection, push separation, and repository alignment.

They must not substitute narrative for repository state.

They must not create runtime state.

## Candidate Surface 5 — Bounded Internal Documentation Surfaces

Sprint 3 may prepare bounded internal documentation surfaces selected by later Human authorisation.

These surfaces may include orientation, introspection, governance, or lineage documentation.

They must remain candidates until explicitly authorised by a later Human-selected Work Block.

They must not create implementation authority.

## Prohibited Surfaces

Sprint 3 may not prepare or authorise:

- behavioural implementation;
- runtime command behaviour;
- command execution;
- command routing;
- command parsing;
- command discovery;
- dynamic command registration;
- telemetry;
- automation;
- hidden inspection;
- source-code mutation without explicit Human-selected Work Block authorisation;
- mutation of sealed Sprint 1 artefacts;
- mutation of sealed Sprint 2 artefacts;
- reinterpretation of sealed Sprint 1 lineage;
- reinterpretation of sealed Sprint 2 lineage;
- expansion beyond the Sprint 3 Boundary Charter;
- appetite-driven or unbounded surfaces.
