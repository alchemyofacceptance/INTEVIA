## Purpose

Define the bounded set of internal organismal surfaces that Sprint 2 is constitutionally authorised to prepare.

These surfaces are documentation-only candidate surfaces. They do not authorise runtime mutation, command execution, source-code change, behavioural implementation, routing, parsing, discovery, or dynamic command registration.

Sprint 2 may prepare candidate surfaces only inside the boundary established by the Sprint 2 Boundary Charter and the intent established by the Sprint 2 Manifest.

## Candidate Surface 1 — Internal Command Surfaces

Sprint 2 may document new bounded internal command surfaces.

These candidate surfaces must remain:

- static;
- human-readable;
- documentation-only;
- non-executable;
- non-behavioural;
- explicitly subject to later Human-selected implementation authority.

They must not introduce routing, parsing, discovery, dynamic registration, runtime command behaviour, or source-code mutation.

## Candidate Surface 2 — Organismal Introspection Surfaces

Sprint 2 may document candidate surfaces that describe internal state, structure, or organismal orientation.

These surfaces may clarify how the organism understands its own documented state.

They must not introduce runtime state mutation, behavioural logic, hidden telemetry, automated inspection, or executable introspection.

## Candidate Surface 3 — Constitutional Instrumentation Surfaces

Sprint 2 may document candidate governance and constitutional instrumentation surfaces.

These surfaces may strengthen review, evidence, lineage, boundary inspection, and Terminal Echo discipline.

They must reinforce the constitutional substrate rather than expand runtime capability.

They do not authorise automated governance, command execution, or behavioural enforcement.

## Candidate Surface 4 — Static Internal Command Index Extensions

Sprint 2 may document additions or extensions to the static internal command index.

These extensions must remain:

- static;
- human-readable;
- documentation-only;
- non-executable;
- aligned with real or candidate bounded command surfaces;
- subject to later review before implementation.

They must not create command discovery, dynamic registration, routing, parsing, or runtime command execution.

## Candidate Surface 5 — Internal Operational Primitives

Sprint 2 may document candidate internal operational primitives.

These primitives may describe concepts, structures, or bounded mechanisms that could support later implementation.

They must remain conceptual, documented, and non-behavioural unless a later Human-selected Work Block explicitly authorises implementation.

## Prohibited Surfaces

Sprint 2 may not prepare or authorise:

- behavioural implementation of candidate surfaces;
- mutation of sealed Sprint 1 artefacts;
- source-code changes without explicit Work Block authorisation;
- routing, parsing, discovery, or dynamic command registration;
- runtime command execution;
- hidden automation;
- public-facing mutation without Velvet Gloves review;
- expansion beyond the v1.0 boundary;
- appetite-driven or unbounded surfaces.
