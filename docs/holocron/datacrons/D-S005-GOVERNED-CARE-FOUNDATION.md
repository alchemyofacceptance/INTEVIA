- Contribution;


Slice 005 extends that lineage toward governance of:

```text
how Human and AI cognition interact
```

The architectural transition is:

> INTEVIA moves from governing software capability and evolution toward governing the Human-AI relationship itself.

## CARE Foundation

CARE is a cross-cut capability, not an owning domain.

The qualified boundary is:

> CARE is a non-authoritative, influence-visible governed coaching capability bounded by CRCL and the Organism constitutional spine that supports reflection, orientation, and learning while preserving Human meaning and decision authority.

CARE supports bounded coaching, visible influence, and Human terminal judgement. It does not acquire ownership of the Human, the Organism constitutional spine, CRCL, Library, Corpus, Continuity, Education, Events, or Services.

## Authority Boundary

```text
CARE authority:
NONE
```

Human meaning authority and Human decision authority remain primary.

CARE cannot:

- decide;
- assign meaning;
- create legitimacy;
- override Human judgement;
- mutate another domain.

CARE consumes existing external authority checks for accountable recording. That reuse does not grant CARE independent authority.

## Influence Governance

The major discovery of Slice 005 is:

> Influence exists even without authority.

Selection, framing, sequencing, highlighting, questioning, and presenting alternatives can influence Human attention while remaining non-binding. Influence is not authority, but consequential influence without visibility creates governance risk.

CARE therefore does not attempt to eliminate influence. It requires consequential influence to remain:

- visible;
- bounded;
- inspectable;
- subordinate to Human judgement.

## Optimisation Boundary

CARE may optimise:

- assistance quality;
- clarity;
- accessibility;
- reflection support.

CARE may not optimise:

- compliance;
- retention;
- engagement;
- dependency;
- attachment;
- behavioural conformity;
- predetermined Human outcomes.

> CARE may optimise the quality of assistance. It may not optimise the Human receiving assistance.
