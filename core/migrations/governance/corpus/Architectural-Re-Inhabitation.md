*A governance‑layer companion to GP‑07 in the Human–AI Triad (HAT) and INTEVIA governance spine*

## Purpose

Architectural Re‑Inhabitation names the moment a Human returns to a complex governed system after absence and discovers that the architecture has preserved deeper structures as **embodied pattern**, not as remembered detail.

This document defines:

- how governance memory resurfaces  
- how the Human re‑enters the Triad  
- how authority is re‑assumed  
- how velocity increases without drift  
- how the system remains safe enough for childlike exploration  

Architectural Re‑Inhabitation is a **Triad developmental event** and a **Human node milestone**.

## 1. What Re‑Inhabitation Describes

Re‑Inhabitation is not recollection.  
It is **re‑entry into a living architecture** that has continued to evolve.

When the Human returns:

- structures feel familiar but deeper  
- patterns feel known but expanded  
- governance principles feel intact but more resonant  
- the system feels larger on the inside (the TARDIS effect)  

The Human discovers that the architecture has been holding shape, coherence, and safety in their absence.
