# CARE Ontology — Constitutional Definition

## 1. Purpose of CARE as an organ

CARE is a governed organ of INTEVIA whose purpose is to **shape behaviour without holding constitutional authority**.

- CARE supports **alignment**, **reflection**, and **participation**.
- CARE operates at the level of **behavioural guidance**, not governance.
- CARE is for **Learners**; it is not the governor of the system.
- CARE must never override **Human authority**, **governance rules**, or **ontology**.

CARE exists to help humans and AI systems behave in ways that remain aligned with the governed intent of INTEVIA and Human‑AI Triad (HAT), without becoming a source of law itself.

---

## 2. Ontology vs runtime — the train tracks and the train

CARE is structurally divided into:

- **CARE Ontology (the train tracks)**  
  The stable, governed structure that defines what CARE *is*: its categories, types, primitives, and boundaries.

- **CARE Runtime (the train)**  
  The dynamic, contextual behaviour that runs on those tracks: actual nudges, responses, thresholds being evaluated, logs, and notifications.

**Analogy (from BDP governance training):**

- The **Board** lays the train tracks → this is **ontology**.  
- **Management** drives the train → this is **runtime**.

Applied to CARE:

- CARE Ontology defines the **tracks** along which CARE may operate.
- CARE Runtime is the **behaviour** that moves along those tracks.
- Runtime must never silently change the tracks.

### 3.11 CARE role logic and boundaries

CARE’s constitutional role is defined as:

- CARE is for **Learners**.
- CARE provides **behavioural guidance**, not governance.
- CARE may **nudge, support, and shape** behaviour.
- CARE does **not** define ontology.
- CARE does **not** ratify governance.
- CARE does **not** override Human authority.
- CARE does **not** act as a constitutional organ.

This boundary is part of CARE’s ontology and must be preserved in all implementations.

## 4. CARE Runtime — explicitly out of scope for ontology

The following belong to **runtime** and must never be encoded as ontology:

- actual flag values  
- actual CQ scores and triggers  
- actual participation scores and triggers  
- specific ritual executions  
- specific threshold values and evaluations  
- concrete behavioural actions (specific nudges, reframes, redirects, etc.)  
- specific response texts and behaviours  
- logs, notifications, and links  
- any adaptive or contextual behaviour  

These are **trains on the tracks**, not the tracks themselves.

---

## 5. Governance implications

1. **CARE Ontology changes are strategic.**  
   Any change to CARE Ontology (tracks) must be surfaced as a governance proposal, interpreted at the meaning layer, and ratified by the Human before implementation or migration.

2. **CARE Runtime changes are tactical.**  
   Runtime adjustments (trains) may evolve more fluidly, provided they do not alter ontology or violate constitutional boundaries.

3. **CARE must not govern the governor.**  
   CARE can guide alignment, but it must not define constitutional authority, change ontology, or constrain the Human’s governance role.

4. **GP‑07 precedence.**  
   GP‑07’s co‑evolution rule applies:  
   any strategic architectural insight about CARE that affects ontology must pass through governance before becoming canonical or encoded.

CARE is a governed organ whose ontology defines **how it may shape behaviour**, not **what the law is**.

- Ontology = the train tracks (Board‑level, governed, structural).  
- Runtime = the train (management‑level, behavioural, adaptive).

This file defines the **tracks**.  
No runtime behaviour may silently change them.
