# SIGNAL  
*The clear outward communication.*

## Purpose

SIGNAL is CARE’s explicit communicative behavioural mode.  
It conveys **clear, unambiguous information** outward, without altering state or applying directive influence.

SIGNAL is used when:

- clarity is required  
- ambiguity must be reduced  
- the Human or system needs explicit information  
- communication is safer than action  

SIGNAL does not direct.  
It **informs**.

---

## When CARE Enters SIGNAL

CARE transitions into SIGNAL when:

- PROJECTION has formed a communicative stance  
- the invariant must be expressed outwardly  
- the risk posture favours clarity  
- the Human requires explicit information  

SIGNAL is the **voice** of the runtime.

---

## Behavioural Actions

Within SIGNAL, CARE performs:

- **explicit communication**  
- **status reporting**  
- **clarity amplification**  
- **contextual signalling**  

SIGNAL must not create:

- pressure  
- implied direction  
- behavioural steering  
- coercive framing  

SIGNAL is outward‑facing but non‑directive.

---

## Allowed Transitions

- PROJECTION → SIGNAL  
- SIGNAL → INTERPRET  
- SIGNAL → DEFER (if risk increases)  

SIGNAL cannot directly modify state.  
It must return to PROJECTION for any behavioural shift.

---

## Output

SIGNAL produces:

- clear communication  
- explicit outward expression  
- no directive influence  
- no state change  

SIGNAL is the **torch held high**.
