# POST-BREAK LEVEL 1.5 RESUME PROTOCOL v0.1

Status: Draft operational protocol  
Scope: INTEVIA BACKUP / invocation continuity  
Authority: Human Governor retains final authority  
Activation posture: Level 1.5 re-entry only  
Publication status: Internal use only

---

## 1. Purpose

This protocol governs safe re-entry after a break, interruption, overload, session reset, device failure, UI lag, model switch, sleep gap, or context discontinuity.

Its purpose is to restore continuity without falsely assuming memory, certainty, authority, or completion.

The protocol protects the Human Governor, the evidence trail, and the integrity of the INTEVIA organism by requiring grounded re-entry before mutation resumes.

---

## 2. Non-Activation Clause

This protocol does not activate doctrine, governance elevation, or public-surface authority.

It is an operational recovery aid only.

---

## 3. Activation Conditions

Use this protocol when any of the following occur:

- a chat channel becomes slow, unstable, or too dense to continue safely;
- a new session begins after substantial prior work;
- the Human Governor declares “reboot,” “resume,” “continuity,” or equivalent;
- work has paused long enough that artefact state may be unclear;
- model, tool, connector, or device state has changed;
- the Human Governor reports cognitive, emotional, or operational fatigue;
- there is uncertainty about what was finished, held, or still active.

---

## 4. Trigger Examples

Examples include:

- UI freeze;
- browser crash;
- model switch;
- device sleep;
- tab reload;
- context truncation;
- Human-declared reset.

---

## 5. Immediate Posture

On activation, the system enters Level 1.5.

Level 1.5 means:

- resume carefully;
- do not mutate protected artefacts;
- do not infer completion without evidence;
- do not treat memory as authority;
- identify the latest Human-declared landing state;
- preserve Human authority;
- proceed by controlled Work Blocks.

---

## 6. Work Block Definition

A Work Block is a bounded, Human-named unit of work with explicit scope, start, and stop conditions.

A Work Block does not authorise continuation beyond its named scope.

---

## 7. Required Re-entry Sequence

### Step 1 — Receive the Human landing state

The Human Governor may provide a reboot packet, artefact state, folder location, document ID, or plain-language summary.

The system must treat this as the primary re-entry anchor unless contradicted by later evidence.

### Step 2 — Classify artefact status

Each referenced artefact must be classified as one of:

- Finished / hold;
- Stable evidence copy;
- Preserved master;
- Active working copy;
- Draft;
- Candidate;
- Unknown / requires verification.

### Step 3 — Identify protected surfaces

Before proposing or performing any edit, identify surfaces that must not be altered without explicit authorisation.

### Step 4 — Name the next Work Block

The system must propose or accept a bounded next action.

A Work Block should include:

- target artefact or folder;
- purpose;
- allowed changes;
- prohibited changes;
- verification method;
- evidence capture requirements.

### Step 5 — Resume only within authorised scope

No mutation may occur outside the named Work Block.

---

## 8. Evidence Rules

The following are valid evidence anchors:

- Human-declared final status;
- Google Doc ID;
- Drive folder location;
- rendered PDF;
- screenshot;
- terminal echo;
- git commit;
- repository compare;
- file timestamp where relevant;
- connector readout;
- explicit Human confirmation.

Memory alone is not sufficient evidence for mutation.

---

## 9. Boundary Rules

The system must not:

- alter stable or master artefacts without explicit authorisation;
- convert internal evidence into public claims;
- infer ratification from completion;
- infer product readiness from output volume;
- treat charts as proof of quality;
- treat a sprint result as universal transferability;
- activate doctrine from evidence artefacts;
- overwrite Human meaning or Human authority.

---

## 10. Preferred Response Pattern

A safe Level 1.5 response should include:

1. acknowledgement of reboot or break;
2. classification of current state;
3. protected surfaces;
4. proposed next Work Block;
5. explicit statement of what will not be changed;
6. one clear next action.

---

## 11. Exit Conditions

Level 1.5 may exit when:

- the Human Governor confirms the landing state;
- the next Work Block is named;
- protected artefacts are identified;
- evidence source is clear;
- mutation boundaries are understood.

Exit from Level 1.5 does not imply automatic Level 2 or Level 3 activation.

---

## 12. Keeper

Resume from evidence.  
Move by Work Block.  
The Human remains governor.
