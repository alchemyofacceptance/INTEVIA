# LEVEL 3 FIELD STABILISATION RECOVERY PROTOCOL v0.1

Status: Draft operational protocol  
Scope: INTEVIA BACKUP / invocation continuity  
Authority: Human Governor retains final authority  
Activation posture: Recovery from Level 3 field stabilisation  
Publication status: Internal use only

---

## 1. Purpose

This protocol governs recovery after a high-intensity Level 3 field event.

A Level 3 field event may involve rapid synthesis, major artefact production, high-output governed AI-assisted work, emotional intensity affecting operational clarity, operational compression, deep architectural movement, or unusually dense evidence generation.

The purpose of this protocol is to preserve what was achieved without overclaiming what it proves.

---

## 2. Non-Activation Clause

This protocol does not activate doctrine, governance elevation, or public-surface authority.

It is an operational recovery aid only.

---

## 3. Activation Conditions

Use this protocol when any of the following occur:

- a sprint produces unusually high output in a short period;
- multiple artefacts, charts, commits, documents, or governance surfaces are created rapidly;
- the Human Governor reports breakthrough, overload, compression, exhaustion, or field intensity;
- a session has moved beyond ordinary task execution into whole-system coordination;
- evidence exists but interpretation has not yet been bounded;
- there is a risk of converting intensity into doctrine, public claim, or product-readiness language too quickly.

---

## 4. Immediate Recovery Posture

On activation, the system must stabilise before extending.

The correct posture is:

- hold the evidence;
- classify the work;
- preserve the Human;
- avoid public-surface escalation;
- avoid doctrine activation;
- avoid new mutation unless a new Work Block is named.

---

## 5. Work Block Definition

A Work Block is a bounded, Human-named unit of work with explicit scope, start, and stop conditions.

A Work Block does not authorise continuation beyond its named scope.

---

## 6. Stabilisation Sequence

### Step 1 — Name the field event

State what happened in bounded language.

Example:

A high-output governed sprint occurred. The work produced inspectable artefacts, evidence tables, charts, commits, and recovery observations.

### Step 2 — Separate evidence from interpretation

Evidence may include:

- commit counts;
- line counts;
- file changes;
- rendered artefacts;
- PDFs;
- screenshots;
- terminal echoes;
- Google Doc versions;
- Human-declared landing states.

Interpretation must remain bounded.

The evidence may show high-output governed activity.

It must not be treated as proof of:

- product maturity;
- quality;
- safety;
- certification;
- external validation;
- universal transferability;
- doctrine completion;
- public readiness.

### Step 3 — Identify the governance achievement

The primary achievement is not only the volume of output.

The governance achievement is that the output was:

- tracked;
- classified;
- bounded;
- reviewed;
- rendered inspectable;
- held under Human authority.

### Step 4 — Create recovery artefacts only if needed

Permitted recovery artefacts include:

- short readout;
- evidence index;
- protocol update;
- delta map;
- training candidate extraction;
- backup invocation note.

Do not create public-surface material unless explicitly authorised.

### Step 5 — Return to controlled Work Blocks

After stabilisation, proceed only through named Work Blocks with clear scope and exit conditions.

---

## 7. Human Protection Rules

After a Level 3 field event, the system must protect the Human Governor from unnecessary acceleration.

The system should prefer:

- closure over expansion;
- holding over polishing;
- evidence classification over new production;
- restabilisation over escalation;
- one Work Block at a time.

---

## 8. Artefact Protection Rules

The system must not alter finished artefacts unless explicitly authorised.

Finished artefacts may be:

- held;
- indexed;
- cited internally;
- copied for derivative work;
- used as evidence anchors.

They must not be silently mutated.

---

## 9. Language Boundaries

Preferred language:

- “evidence-bounded”;
- “internal evidence artefact”;
- “candidate finding”;
- “governed sprint activity”;
- “inspectable output signal”;
- “bounded recovery observation.”

Avoid language that implies:

- proof;
- validation;
- certification;
- deployment readiness;
- universal applicability;
- final doctrine;
- external endorsement.

---

## 10. Exit Conditions

Recovery may be considered stable when:

- the finished artefact has been held;
- evidence and interpretation have been separated;
- protected surfaces are named;
- the next Work Block is bounded;
- the Human Governor is not being pulled into unnecessary acceleration.

Exit Condition: Stabilisation ends only when the Human names the next Work Block or declares a landing state.
